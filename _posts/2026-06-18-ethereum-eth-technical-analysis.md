## Current Price Action

Ethereum (ETH/USD) is trading at approximately $1,718 as of June 18, 2026, continuing to navigate a challenging technical environment characterized by bearish structural alignment across multiple timeframes. The cryptocurrency has fallen significantly from its 2026 highs above $2,400 and now sits in a zone where the daily, hourly, and intraday charts are telling conflicting stories — a classic sign of trend indecision within an overarching downtrend.

Over the past 48 hours, ETH staged a modest recovery from the $1,640 support zone, bouncing approximately 4.8% to the current level. However, this bounce remains contained well below all major daily moving averages, and the recovery momentum on shorter timeframes is already showing signs of exhaustion. The cryptocurrency market broadly remains under pressure, with Bitcoin dominance holding at 56.65% — capital continues to concentrate in BTC while altcoins, including Ethereum, lag behind.

The Fear and Greed Index currently reads 20, firmly in Extreme Fear territory. While this level historically correlates with market bottoms and accumulation phases, it is not a timing signal in itself. Markets can remain in extreme fear for extended periods before reversing, particularly when the macro structure remains bearish.

## RSI Analysis

The daily Relative Strength Index (RSI) for Ethereum sits at 37.35, placing it squarely in bearish territory but not yet in oversold conditions (typically defined as below 30). This is an important distinction: an RSI of 37 suggests that while selling pressure dominates, there is still room for further downside before contrarian buyers would typically step in with conviction. The RSI has been trending below 40 for most of the past two weeks, reflecting sustained bearish momentum rather than a sharp capitulation event.

On the hourly timeframe, however, the picture looks markedly different. The H1 RSI has risen to 67.57, approaching the overbought threshold of 70. This divergence — bearish on the daily, bullish on the hourly — is a warning sign for short-term traders. The hourly RSI near 70 suggests that the current bounce is running out of steam at a structural level well below any significant resistance. When the hourly RSI reaches these levels without the price having reclaimed any major daily moving averages, the probability of a reversal back to the downside increases significantly.

The 4-hour RSI reads approximately 45, still below the neutral 50 level, confirming that the medium-term trend remains bearish despite the short-term recovery.

## MACD Analysis

The daily MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) for Ethereum presents a mixed but overall bearish picture. The MACD line stands at -113.67, with the signal line at -125.96, producing a histogram value of +12.29. While the histogram is positive — indicating that the MACD line is converging toward the signal line from below — the absolute values remain deeply negative. This is a narrowing of bearish momentum, not a bullish crossover. It suggests that selling pressure is easing slightly but has not yet reversed.

On the hourly chart, the MACD is more decisively positive, with the histogram at +2.1. This aligns with the short-term bounce seen over the past two days and provides some cover for the recovery narrative. However, the hourly MACD positive reading is already showing signs of flattening, which would be the first indication that the bounce is losing momentum.

The 4-hour MACD histogram is near zero, reflecting the broader indecision in the medium term. A decisive move either above $1,789 (daily EMA20) or below $1,640 would likely trigger a corresponding MACD signal on the 4-hour chart and establish the next directional bias.

## Moving Averages

Ethereum's moving average structure on the daily timeframe paints an unequivocally bearish picture. The price at $1,718 sits below all three key exponential moving averages:
- EMA20: $1,789 (~4% above current price)
- EMA50: $1,965 (~14% above current price)
- EMA200: $2,460 (~43% above current price)

This triple alignment — shorter, medium, and longer-term averages all above price — is the textbook definition of a bearish moving average stack. Historically, when an asset trades below all three EMAs simultaneously, every rally attempt encounters structural selling pressure at progressively higher levels. The EMA20 at $1,789 serves as the first major resistance level and the nearest potential ceiling for any bounce attempt.

On the hourly timeframe, the picture is inverted: ETH is trading above the H1 EMA20 ($1,702), EMA50 ($1,688), and EMA200 ($1,683). These three averages are compressed into a tight range, which is characteristic of a consolidation within a recovery, not a breakout. The hourly bullish alignment provides support for the current bounce but lacks the conviction of a larger trend reversal.

The 200-day simple moving average (SMA) at approximately $2,350 remains well above price, and the 50-day SMA at $1,880 has crossed below the 100-day SMA at $1,950 — a bearish "death cross" that occurred in late May 2026 and continues to weigh on sentiment.

## Volume Analysis

Trading volume for Ethereum over the past week has been elevated compared to the four-week average, consistent with the increased volatility and the breakdown from the $2,000-$2,200 range. The selling volume during the June 12-14 decline was notably higher than the buying volume during the subsequent bounce, a pattern that favors the bearish thesis.

On-Balance Volume (OBV) has been trending downward since early June, confirming that distribution (selling) dominates accumulation (buying) at current levels. The OBV has not confirmed the recent price bounce, which is a bearish divergence signal: the price is moving higher but the volume behind the move is not supporting it.

Volume Profile analysis shows the highest concentration of trading activity between $1,800 and $2,000 (the Value Area High/Low). The current price at $1,718 sits below this range, indicating that the market has broken below its previously established fair value zone. A return to this zone would require a 5-10% rally, but until volume confirms such a move, the path of least resistance remains lower.

## Chart Patterns

Ethereum is currently trading within a descending channel pattern that has been in place since the March 2026 highs near $2,800. The upper boundary of this channel connects the lower highs, while the lower boundary has provided temporary support during each leg down.

The most significant recent pattern is the breakdown from a large symmetrical wedge or pennant formation that completed in early June. After consolidating between $2,000 and $2,200 for several weeks, ETH broke decisively below the lower boundary of this pattern around $1,950, triggering a sharp decline to the current levels. This breakdown has a measured move target of approximately $1,500-$1,550, which aligns with the support levels identified above.

On the hourly chart, a small bullish flag has formed over the past 24 hours, which could suggest a continuation of the bounce toward $1,789. However, flags in downtrends frequently fail at resistance, making this a risky setup for aggressive longs.

This width reflects the elevated volatility that has characterized the market since the early June breakdown. The ATR (Average True Range) on the daily chart stands at $96, meaning daily swings of this magnitude are normal in the current environment.

A daily close below this level would signal that volatility is expanding to the downside and would likely accelerate selling pressure.

The Bollinger Band Width (BBW) indicator is elevated but not at extreme levels, suggesting that while volatility is high, it has room to expand further before mean reversion becomes statistically likely.

On the hourly chart, the Bollinger Bands are tight and compressed, with the upper band at $1,723 and the lower band at $1,715. This compression — known as a "Bollinger Band squeeze" — typically precedes an expansion of volatility. Given that the squeeze is occurring in a zone well below all daily moving averages, the directional bias favors a downside resolution.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The most plausible Elliott Wave count for Ethereum from the March 2026 highs suggests we are in the third wave of a five-wave bearish impulse. Waves 1 and 2 completed in the March-May period, with Wave 2 retracing to approximately $2,400. The breakdown from $2,400 in late May marked the beginning of Wave 3, which is typically the longest and strongest wave in an impulse sequence.

Within Wave 3, we appear to be in a sub-wave (iii) or (iv) position. If we are in sub-wave (iii), the downside target for this wave would be approximately $1,400-$1,500, consistent with the measured move from the wedge breakdown. If we are in sub-wave (iv), a temporary bounce to the $1,800-$1,850 zone could occur before sub-wave (v) completes the third wave near $1,350.

A move above $1,850 would invalidate this bearish count and suggest a more complex correction pattern. A move below $1,467 would confirm the most bearish scenario.

Key levels to monitor this week: $1,789 (immediate resistance), $1,640 (near-term support), and $1,467 (critical defense).
