# SOXL and SOXS Semiconductor ETFs Technical Analysis May 27 2026

Semiconductor leveraged ETFs SOXL and SOXS offer traders amplified exposure to the semiconductor sector. SOXL provides 3x daily long exposure to the ICE Semiconductor Index while SOXS provides 3x inverse exposure. On May 27 2026 both instruments present significant technical setups that warrant detailed analysis.

## Current Price Action

SOXL experienced a dramatic reversal in the second half of May 2026. After reaching highs above 230 in early May the fund sold off sharply losing over 30 percent of its value in under three weeks. The current price action shows attempted stabilization near key historical support levels.

SOXS the inverse ETF rallied correspondingly as semiconductor weakness accelerated. The inverse relationship between these two instruments creates distinct trading opportunities for both bullish and bearish traders.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-period RSO on SOXL has fallen to oversold territory near 25 on the daily chart. Historically readings below 30 on SOXL have marked significant swing lows. The last time RSI reached similar levels was during the early May correction which preceded a sharp 40 percent rally.

On SOXS the RSI has extended above 70 into overbought territory suggesting the inverse move may be overextended. Mean reversion in SOXS corresponds directly to stabilization or recovery in SOXL.

The divergence between the two RSI readings creates a compelling setup for traders watching for a semiconductor bounce.

## Moving Averages

SOXL is trading below all major moving averages:

- **SMA 20:** Currently at approximately 185 — price is well below suggesting strong bearish momentum
- **SMA 50:** Currently at approximately 170 — price has crossed below confirming medium term downtrend
- **SMA 200:** Currently at approximately 140 — still above this level suggesting the long term trend remains intact

For any recovery to gain credibility SOXL must reclaim the 20-day SMA at 185. Until then the path of least resistance remains lower.

SOXS conversely is trading above all major moving averages with the 20-day SMA acting as dynamic support near 55.

## Volume Analysis

Volume during the selloff has been above average indicating institutional participation in the move. This is not a low-volume drift lower — it is a high-volume capitulation that typically marks significant turning points.

On SOXL specifically the highest volume day during the selloff occurred near the lows which is a classic capitulation pattern. Smart money often steps in aggressively during high-volume washouts.

## MACD Analysis

The MACD on SOXL has crossed into bearish territory with the signal line confirming the downtrend. However the histogram is showing signs of contraction suggesting selling momentum is decelerating.

On SOXS the MACD remains bullish but the histogram is also showing contraction suggesting the inverse move is losing steam.

## Trading Scenarios

**Bullish Scenario for SOXL:**
If SOXL holds the 155 support level and RSI begins to curl higher from oversolved territory we could see a relief rally toward 175 to 185. A daily close above 185 would confirm the downtrend is reversing and open the door for a move back toward 200.

**Bearish Scenario for SOXL:**
A daily close below 155 would trigger further selling toward the 200-day SMA at 140. This would likely coincide with SOXS breaking above 65 toward 72.

## Risk Management

These are 3x leveraged ETFs and are inherently volatile. Position sizing should be adjusted accordingly. Never risk more than 1 to 2 percent of your portfolio on a single leveraged ETF trade.

For SOXL long positions place stop losses below 150. For SOXS long positions place stop losses below 52.

SOXL is deeply oversold with RSI near 25 and price at key support. The high-volume capitulation pattern suggests a relief rally is probable in the near term. However the moving average alignment remains bearish until proven otherwise.
