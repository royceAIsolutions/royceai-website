## Current Price Action

Bitcoin BTCUSD is trading near 62,400 dollars on June 24 2026, having lost the critical 63,500 support level earlier this week. The pullback from recent highs marks the third consecutive decline, with price now testing the 62,000 psychological support zone that previously held as a consolidation base in late June.

Daily volume has declined 18 percent from the weekly average, suggesting the selling pressure may be exhausting itself. However, the failure to reclaim 63,500 on any pullback attempt indicates that bears remain in control of the near-term structure. The 24-hour change sits at negative 2.3 percent, with intraday price oscillating between 61,800 and 63,200.

The broader context remains important: Bitcoin is still holding above the ascending trendline from the April 2026 low near 54,000 dollars. That trendline currently sits near 60,500 and represents the line in the sand for the medium-term bullish structure.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-period RSI on the daily chart has dropped to 34.2, approaching oversold territory for the first time since early June. This is a significant reading — it suggests the correction has been sharp enough to reset momentum without yet triggering the extreme oversold conditions that typically mark trend exhaustion.

On the 4-hour timeframe, RSI sits at 29.8, technically oversold. This creates a divergence: daily RSI is approaching oversold while 4-hour RSI has already reached it. Historically, this combination often precedes a short-term bounce or consolidation, particularly when the daily RSI has not yet crossed below 30.

The weekly RSI remains at 48.6, well above the neutral 50 line. This tells us that while the short-term correction is real, the broader weekly momentum structure has not yet broken down. A weekly RSI below 45 would signal a more concerning shift in trend structure.

## MACD Analysis

The daily MACD line has crossed below the signal line for the first time since May 15, confirming the bearish shift in momentum. The histogram is printing negative bars at an accelerating rate, with the current reading at negative 1,045 — the most negative reading in three weeks.

On the 4-hour chart, the MACD crossover occurred on June 22, and the histogram has been expanding steadily since then. There are early signs of deceleration — the histogram bars are getting shorter on the most recent 4-hour candles, which could signal that bearish momentum is losing steam.

The weekly MACD remains bullish, with the MACD line still 2,800 points above the signal line. This creates a conflict: weekly bulls remain in control while daily and 4-hour bears have taken over. In such environments, the higher timeframe typically wins out, and the lower timeframe bearishness represents a correction within a larger uptrend rather than a trend reversal.

The moving average picture is mixed and reveals the tension between short-term bearishness and medium-term bullishness:

- **SMA 20:** 64,200 dollars — price is now below the 20-day SMA, which has flattened and is curling downward. This is the first close below the 20 SMA since May 28.
- **SMA 50:** 61,800 dollars — price is testing this level from above. The 50 SMA has been a reliable support level throughout June, and its first test is a critical moment.
- **SMA 100:** 59,400 dollars — still rising and well below current price. This represents strong medium-term support.
- **SMA 200:** 56,200 dollars — the long-term bullish anchor, rising steadily.
- **EMA 12:** 63,100 dollars — price has fallen below the short-term EMA, confirming the bearish shift.
- **EMA 26:** 62,600 dollars — price is hovering just below this level. A close below 62,600 would be technically significant.

The alignment shows a market in transition. Short-term averages are flattening or declining while medium-term averages remain bullish. This is textbook corrective behavior, not trend breakdown.

## Volume Analysis

Daily volume on June 24 is tracking at approximately 28,000 BTC on major exchanges, down from the 34,000 BTC average earlier in the week. The declining volume on declining price is actually a positive signal — it suggests the selling is not accelerating.

The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator has declined 8 percent over the past five days but remains above its 20-day average. This indicates that while distribution is occurring, it is not yet at levels associated with major trend reversals.

Volume Profile Visible Range (VPVR) analysis shows a high-volume node at 61,500 to 62,000 dollars, which aligns with the current price action. This zone should provide significant support as it represents an area of high trading interest where many positions were established.

The Volume Price Trend (VPT) indicator has flattened, confirming the consolidation phase. A break below the VPT 20-line at current levels would signal a deeper correction toward 59,000.

## Chart Patterns

The daily chart reveals a clear breakdown from a rising wedge pattern that formed between May 28 and June 20. The rising wedge is typically bearish, and the breakdown below the lower trendline at 63,500 confirms the pattern. The measured move target — based on the widest point of the wedge — projects to 59,800 dollars.

On the 4-hour chart, there are signs of a developing descending channel. Price has made three lower highs and two equal lows near 61,800. A break below 61,800 would complete the channel and target 60,200. A break above the channel upper boundary near 63,200 would invalidate the short-term bearish structure.

A potential bullish divergence is forming on the 4-hour RSI — price is making lower lows while RSI is making higher lows. This is an early signal that bearish momentum is weakening, though it has not yet been confirmed by price action.

## Bollinger Bands

The daily Bollinger Bands (20-period, 2 standard deviations) have expanded significantly during this correction. The upper band sits at 67,800, the middle band (20 SMA) at 64,200, and the lower band at 60,600.

Price is currently trading in the lower half of the bands, approaching the lower band at 60,600. This is a statistically significant zone — approximately 95 percent of price action occurs within the bands, and touching the lower band often signals a mean-reversion opportunity.

The bandwidth indicator has expanded to 11.2 percent, the highest reading in six weeks. Expanding bandwidth during a correction followed by contraction typically signals the correction is nearing completion.

## Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud analysis on the daily chart shows price has broken below the cloud for the first time since May 10. This is a significant bearish signal in the Ichimoku system.

- **Tenkan-sen (9-period):** 62,800 — price is below, confirming short-term bearishness
- **Kijun-sen (26-period):** 63,400 — price is below, confirming medium-term bearishness
- **Senkou Span A:** 63,100 — the leading span is now above price, creating resistance
- **Senkou Span B:** 61,200 — the lagging span provides support
- **Cloud color:** Bearish (Senkou Span A below Senkou Span B on the next 26 periods)

The Kijun-sen at 63,400 is a critical level. A daily close above the Kijun-sen would shift the Ichimoku bias back to neutral and suggest the correction is ending. Until then, the system maintains a bearish reading.

The Chikou Span (lagging line) is currently at 62,400, right at current price. This creates a congestion zone that often acts as a pivot point.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count suggests Bitcoin is in Wave 4 of a larger impulse that began at the April 2026 low of 54,000. The count is as follows:

- **Wave 1:** 54,000 to 59,200 (completed)
- **Wave 2:** 59,200 to 56,800 (completed)
- **Wave 3:** 56,800 to 67,400 (completed)
- **Wave 4:** 67,400 to current (in progress)

Wave 4 typically retraces 38.2 percent of Wave 3, which calculates to approximately 59,900 dollars. This aligns with the 61.8 percent Fibonacci retracement of the full move (59,886) and the 100-day SMA (59,400). The confluence of these levels around 59,400 to 60,000 creates a high-probability support zone.

Wave 4 should not overlap Wave 1 territory (below 59,200). If price breaks below 59,200, the Elliott Wave count is invalidated and a deeper correction or trend reversal becomes more likely.

## Risk Factors

Several factors could invalidate the bullish medium-term thesis:

1. **ETF outflow acceleration:** If weekly ETF outflows exceed 2 billion dollars consistently, the selling pressure could overwhelm institutional absorption.
2. **Macro risk-off:** A broader equity market selloff would likely drag Bitcoin lower regardless of crypto-specific fundamentals.
3. **Regulatory escalation:** Any adverse regulatory action against major exchanges or stablecoins could trigger panic selling.
4. **Break of 59,200:** This level is the Wave 1 high and a break below it would invalidate the entire bullish wave count.

## Conclusion

Bitcoin is at a critical juncture. The short-term trend is clearly bearish — price has broken below the 20-day SMA, the Ichimoku cloud, and the 63,500 support level. However, the medium-term structure remains intact as long as the 59,200 to 60,000 support zone holds.

The confluence of the 100-day SMA (59,400), the 61.8 percent Fibonacci retracement (59,886), the Elliott Wave 4 target (59,900), and the psychological 60,000 level creates a high-probability bounce zone. Traders should watch for signs of stabilization in this area, particularly a daily close back above the Kijun-sen at 63,400, which would signal the correction is ending.

Until then, caution is warranted. The path of least resistance remains sideways to lower, with the 61,800 to 62,000 zone providing the first line of defense for bulls.
