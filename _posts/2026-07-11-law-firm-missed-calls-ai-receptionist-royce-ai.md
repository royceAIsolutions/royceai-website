## Introduction

Every day, thousands of potential legal clients call law firms across America seeking representation. They have been in accidents, face criminal charges, need estate planning, or require family law services. But according to the Clio Legal Trends Report, approximately 35% of those calls go unanswered — and an alarming 60% or more of after-hours calls never reach a live person. For a profession built on client acquisition, these missed calls represent a staggering loss of revenue and opportunity. In 2026, law firms are projected to lose over $182,000 annually on average to unanswered intake calls, making phone answering one of the most expensive operational blind spots in the legal industry.

## The Problem

The numbers paint a clear picture of the crisis facing law firms. Across the United States, legal practices receive an estimated 557 million phone calls per year. If 35% of those calls go unanswered, that is over 195 million missed opportunities annually. The consequences are severe: 67% of legal clients choose the first attorney who answers their call, meaning delayed responses directly translate to lost business. Furthermore, 46% of clients first contact law firms by phone, making the telephone the single most important client acquisition channel in the legal industry.

The timing of missed calls compounds the problem. After-hours calls — those coming between 6 PM and 8 AM, on weekends, and during holidays — have the highest miss rate at over 60%. Yet these calls are disproportionately valuable: roughly 60% of after-hours and weekend callers are first-time prospects who have never contacted the firm before. These are the highest-intent leads, and law firms are sending two-thirds of them straight to voicemail.

## Why Current Solutions Fail

Traditional law firm phone management comes in three forms, each with significant drawbacks.

Human receptionists are expensive, with total employer costs ranging from $35,000 to $55,000 per year plus benefits. They work a standard 40-hour week, leaving 128 hours uncovered. During busy periods, they can only handle one call at a time, and hold times above 30 seconds dramatically increase hang-up rates.

Receptionist agencies and answering services offer an alternative but come with per-minute or per-call pricing that adds up quickly. A law firm receiving 100 calls per day at $1.50 per minute averages $4,500 per month or $54,000 per year — and these services still miss intakes during peak periods. Many use offshore agents who lack familiarity with legal terminology and intake procedures.

Voicemail is the default fallback, but it is the most expensive option of all. Only 18% of callers leave a voicemail after an unanswered call, and of those who do, fewer than half return a follow-up call. When that initial call was from a prospective client in crisis — a DUI arrest, a workplace injury, a custody emergency — every hour of delay risks losing the case to a competitor.
