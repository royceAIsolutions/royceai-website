## Why Traditional Intake Processes Fail
Manual referral intake is inherently error-prone. When a referring physician sends a referral via fax or phone, the receiving practice must manually transcribe patient information, verify insurance coverage, check specialist availability, and schedule an appointment. Each step introduces opportunities for error: misread handwriting, missed faxes, unanswered phone calls, and outdated insurance data.

The MGMA (Medical Group Management Association) reports that 40% of referral-related delays are caused by incomplete patient information, while 25% result from failed communication between referring and receiving practices. For patients, this means longer wait times, repeated visits to their primary care provider, and in some cases, worsening health conditions due to delayed specialist care.
