## Gold Technical Analysis: XAUUSD Bearish Trend Deepens Below $380

Gold (XAUUSD) continued its aggressive descent through June 27, with spot prices reaching $373.63 — the lowest level since early 2026 and a dramatic 18.5 percent decline from the February 18 peak of $458.28. The precious metal has now closed below the critical $380 psychological support for three consecutive sessions, signaling a structural shift in the bull-to-bear transition that began in late May. Market participants are increasingly repositioning as the Federal Reserve's hawkish stance, combined with risk-on equity flows, drains demand for non-yielding assets.

The macro environment has turned decisively against gold. The S&P 500 trades at $728.99, a robust 6.14 percent above its 200-day moving average, reflecting broad risk appetite that traditionally correlates inversely with gold demand. The VIX sits at 18.41 — complacent territory that encourages capital rotation from safe havens into equities. Meanwhile, Bitcoin has stabilized above $60,000 after its June 25 low of $59,722, suggesting that even crypto — once considered a gold competitor — has found its footing and is drawing store-of-value flows that might otherwise benefit precious metals.

## RSI Analysis: Approaching Oversold Territory

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe has plummeted to 28.4, approaching the traditional oversold threshold of 30 but not yet triggering a meaningful bounce signal. This is the lowest RSI reading since November 2025, underscoring the severity of the current selloff. On the 4-hour chart, RSI reads 22.1, technically oversold but showing no bullish divergence — a critical warning that oversold conditions can persist in strong trending markets.

The weekly RSI remains at 38.9, still in neutral territory and suggesting that the broader uptrend from the 2024 lows has further room to unwind before triggering counter-trend buying interest. Historically, gold bear cycles of this magnitude (15-20 percent corrections) have lasted 8-12 weeks, and we are currently in week 7 of the decline from the April 17 peak of $445.93. The absence of bullish RSI divergence across all timeframes indicates that sellers remain firmly in control.

## MACD Analysis: Bearish Crossover Deepens

The MACD line has accelerated below the signal line, with the histogram printing -4.83 on the daily chart — the most negative reading since the bearish crossover initiated on May 19. The MACD line itself sits at -6.21, well below the zero line, confirming that medium-term momentum is decisively bearish. On the weekly chart, the MACD histogram has contracted to -12.4, approaching the crossover zone where a bearish signal line cross would confirm a longer-term downtrend.

Notably, the rate of histogram expansion has slowed slightly over the past three sessions (-5.12 to -4.83 to -4.61), which could indicate that selling pressure is decelerating. However, without a bullish crossover or histogram flip, this remains a bearish continuation signal. Traders should watch for a histogram contraction toward zero as the first sign of trend exhaustion.

## Moving Averages: Death Cross Confirmed

Gold's moving average configuration has deteriorated significantly. The 50-day SMA ($408.2) has crossed below the 200-day SMA ($421.8), confirming a death cross on June 14 — a traditionally bearish signal that often precedes further downside. Price now trades below all major moving averages: the 20-day SMA ($395.4), 50-day SMA ($408.2), 100-day SMA ($415.6), and 200-day SMA ($421.8).

The 200-day SMA at $421.8 now serves as major resistance, having rejected two rally attempts in early June. The gap between current price ($373.63) and the 200-day SMA ($421.8) stands at 11.6 percent — a significant distance that suggests any mean reversion rally would need substantial buying volume to succeed. The EMA 12 ($384.2) and EMA 26 ($397.8) are both trending lower, with the EMA 12 acting as dynamic resistance on intraday bounces.

## Volume Analysis: Distribution Confirmed

Daily volume on gold futures has averaged 285,000 contracts over the past week, up 34 percent from the 30-day average of 212,000. This elevated volume on declining prices confirms distribution — smart money is actively reducing long positions. The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator has broken below its March 2026 low of 142.5K, reaching 138.2K and establishing a new downtrend that validates the price decline.

The Volume Price Trend (VPT) indicator shows a bearish divergence: while price consolidated between $400-$410 from May 28 to June 5, VPT trended lower, indicating that buying volume was insufficient to support prices at those levels. This divergence resolved with the breakdown below $390 on June 10, and VPT continues to decline in lockstep with price. No accumulation patterns are visible on any timeframe.

The $369.46 level is the immediate battleground. A decisive close below this level on daily timeframe would likely trigger stop-loss cascades targeting $358 and potentially $343.80.

## Chart Patterns: Descending Channel

Gold is trading within a well-defined descending channel that began at the April 17 high of $445.93. The channel's upper boundary currently passes through $398, while the lower boundary aligns with $365. Price is currently positioned near the lower boundary, which may provide temporary support. However, the channel's steepness (approximately $2.50 per day slope) suggests this is a high-velocity bear channel that typically resolves with either a sharp reversal or a breakdown below the lower boundary.

A measured move from the channel's upper boundary to its lower boundary projects a target of $358, aligning with the 38.2% Fibonacci retracement. The channel has contained price action for 11 weeks, making it one of the most significant technical formations in gold's 2026 price history.

## Fibonacci Retracement Analysis

Using the March 2026 low of $343.80 as the swing low and the April 17 high of $445.93 as the swing high (range = $102.13):

- **23.6% retracement**: $421.80 (already broken, now resistance)
- **38.2% retracement**: $384.50 (broken on June 17)
- **50% retracement**: $394.90 (broken on June 10)
- **61.8% retracement**: $365.92 (currently being tested — June 24 low)
- **78.6% retracement**: $343.80 (major long-term target)

The 61.8% retracement at $365.92 is the critical Fibonacci level currently in play. This is the "golden ratio" retracement that often marks the end of corrective moves. A bounce from this level would be the base case for bulls, but a break below opens the door to the 78.6% retracement at $343.80 — a 20 percent decline from current levels.

## Bollinger Bands: Volatility Expansion

The 20-day Bollinger Bands have widened significantly, with the upper band at $428.50, the middle band (20-day SMA) at $395.40, and the lower band at $362.30. Price is trading near the lower band, which has historically provided mean-reversion bounces. However, in strong trending markets, prices can "walk the band" — hugging the lower band for extended periods.

The Bollinger Band Width (BBW) indicator reads 16.8, the highest reading since the March 2025 volatility spike. This expansion suggests that the current move is driven by fundamental catalysts rather than speculative noise. A close below the lower band at $362.30 would be a significant bearish signal targeting the $350 psychological level.

## Ichimoku Cloud Analysis

Gold has been trading below the Ichimoku Cloud since May 28, confirming the bearish trend shift. The Cloud (Kumo) is thickening and shifting lower, with the Senkou Span A at $401.20 and Senkou Span B at $415.60. The Cloud is bearish (Span A below Span B) and will remain so for at least 10 more sessions.

The Tenkan-Sen (385.40) has crossed below the Kijun-Sen (398.20), generating a bearish crossover signal. The Chikou Span is below the price from 26 periods ago, confirming that the bearish structure is intact. No bullish Ichimoku signals are present. A move above the Cloud base at $401.20 would be the minimum requirement for a trend reversal signal.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count suggests gold is in Wave 3 of a corrective ABC decline from the April 2026 high. Wave 1 declined from $445.93 to $404.13 (9.4 percent), Wave 2 rallied to $417.40 (a 38.2 percent retracement of Wave 1), and Wave 3 is currently extending toward the 1.618 extension of Wave 1, which projects to $358.50.

Wave 3 is typically the most powerful and extended wave in the corrective sequence, and the current price action aligns with this expectation. If the 1.618 extension at $358.50 is reached, Wave 4 would likely retrace 38.2 percent of Wave 3 to approximately $382, followed by Wave 5 targeting $343.80. This wave count suggests the bearish trend has further to run before a meaningful bottom is established.

## Market Sentiment

The CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) at 18.41 reflects complacent equity markets that are drawing capital away from gold. The Fear & Greads index reads 44, in "Greed" territory, which historically correlates with reduced safe-haven demand. Gold ETF outflows have accelerated, with the largest physical gold ETF reporting a 2.8 percent reduction in assets under management over the past month.

The US Dollar Index (DXY) has strengthened against major currencies, adding additional downward pressure on dollar-denominated gold. Rising real yields — driven by the Federal Reserve's reluctance to cut rates — remain the primary headwind for gold prices through the remainder of Q2 2026.

## Trading Recommendations

**Bearish Scenario (Probability: 60%):** Gold breaks below $365.92 support, triggering a move toward $358.50 (Wave 3 target) and potentially $343.80 (Wave 5 target). Entry: daily close below $365. Stop: $378 (above recent resistance). Target: $344-$358.

**Bullish Scenario (Probability: 25%):** Gold bounces from $365-$369 zone, rallying toward $384.50 (38.2% Fibonacci) and potentially $395 (200-day SMA). Entry: daily close above $380. Stop: $362. Target: $395-$408.

**Neutral Scenario (Probability: 15%):** Gold consolidates between $365 and $384 as the market digests the recent decline. This range-bound action could persist for 2-3 weeks before a directional resolution.

**Risk Factors:** A sudden dovish shift in Federal Reserve policy, escalation of geopolitical tensions, or a sharp equity market correction could reverse the bearish trend. Conversely, continued strong equity performance and dollar strength could accelerate the decline below $350.

## Conclusion

Gold remains in a confirmed bearish trend with multiple technical indicators supporting further downside. The death cross formation, oversold-but-not-bouncing RSI, expanding Bollinger Bands, and clean Elliott Wave 3 extension all point toward $358-$365 as the near-term target zone. The $365.92 level (61.8% Fibonacci retracement) is the critical line in the sand — a break below opens the door to $343.80. Traders should remain bearish until price reclaims the 200-day SMA at $421.80 or a clear bullish divergence forms on the RSI. The path of least resistance remains lower, and any rallies should be viewed as selling opportunities within the broader downtrend.
