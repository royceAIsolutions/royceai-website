### Why Veterinary Practices Miss So Many Calls

Veterinary medicine faces unique challenges that make phone management especially difficult:

#### 1. Front Desk Overload

The front desk is the busiest spot in any veterinary clinic. Staff are simultaneously checking in patients, processing payments, answering questions from waiting room clients, managing discharge instructions, and answering phones. When a walk-in client arrives or an emergency case comes through the door, the phone gets deprioritized — and calls go to voicemail.

Unlike many other service industries, veterinary clinics cannot simply add more front desk staff. Veterinary margins are tight, and the industry is facing a severe labor shortage — over 25% of veterinarians leave the profession each year, with projected shortfalls of 15,000 veterinarians by 2030 (CoVet). Adding front desk personnel in a tight labor market is expensive and often impossible.

#### 2. After-Hours Coverage Gaps

Pets do not get sick on a 9-to-5 schedule. A vomiting dog at 10 PM, a limping cat on a Sunday morning, a scared owner calling after hours to ask whether symptoms warrant an emergency visit — these calls happen constantly, and most practices cannot staff phones 24/7.

The result: anxious pet owners either leave voicemails that go unanswered for hours, or worse, they call the next closest practice. In the veterinary world, the practice that answers first often wins the patient for life.

#### 3. Multi-Line Phone Management

A busy veterinary clinic may have 3-5 phone lines ringing simultaneously during peak hours (morning drop-off, lunchtime callbacks, and evening pick-up). Even a well-staffed front desk can only handle one call at a time. The other callers get busy signals or roll to voicemail.

#### 4. Call Complexity and Triage

Veterinary calls are not simple transactional calls. Pet owners need to describe symptoms, ask about medication dosages, inquire about pricing, schedule appointments, and sometimes make urgent triage decisions. This complexity means calls take longer to handle, reducing the total number of calls a front desk team can process in a day.
