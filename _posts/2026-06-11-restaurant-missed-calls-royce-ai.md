# Restaurants Lose $25B+ to Missed Calls — Royce AI Solution

## The Hidden Revenue Leak

Every dinner rush at a busy restaurant brings a paradox: the dining room is full, tables are turning, but the phone is ringing — and nobody can answer it. Line cooks are prepping, servers are running food, and the host stand is buried in walk-ins. So the phone rings off the hook, and call after call goes to voicemail.

The numbers are staggering. **43% of inbound calls to restaurants go unanswered** — nearly half of every reservation inquiry, takeout order, catering request, and special occasion booking is left on the table. In 2025 alone, missed calls translated to **over $25 billion in lost sales** across the restaurant industry. In 2026, the problem is only accelerating as diners increasingly call before booking online.

A single missed catering call can cost more than a week's worth of takeout orders. Restaurants with active catering programs report losing **3–7 catering leads per week** to unanswered calls.

## Why Traditional Solutions Don't Work

### The Host Stand Trap
- Hosts are pulled in every direction during peak hours
- Average hold time during dinner rush: 4–7 minutes (callers hang up after 45 seconds)
- 62% of callers who reach voicemail never call back
- 60% of those callers go to a competitor immediately

### Voicemail Is Worse Than Useless
- **85% of callers who reach voicemail hang up without leaving a message**
- Of those who leave messages, only 30% get a call back within 2 hours
- By the time the restaurant calls back, the customer has already booked elsewhere

### Call Centers Don't Understand Restaurants
- Generic call centers can't answer menu questions ("Do you have gluten-free pasta?")
- They don't know table availability or reservation policies
- Average cost: $2,500–$4,500/month with poor restaurant-specific knowledge
- Diners complain about impersonal service, hurting your Yelp rating
