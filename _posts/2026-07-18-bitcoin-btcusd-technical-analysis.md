Bitcoin is trading at $63,888 as of July 18, 2026, down approximately $225 or 0.35 percent on the day. The cryptocurrency has been oscillating in a narrowing range between $62,500 and $64,200 over the past week, compressing into what technical analysts recognize as a coil pattern that typically precedes a significant directional move. The intraday range spans from a low of $62,500 to a high of $64,200, with volume levels running near the 30-day average.

The broader picture shows Bitcoin trading 49 percent below its all-time high of $126,080, following a 26 percent surge earlier this year that was sharply rejected near the $70,000 resistance zone. This rejection has left the market in a state of indecision, with bulls and batteries fighting for control around the psychologically important $64,000 level. The total crypto market cap sits at $2.27 trillion, with Bitcoin dominance at 56.41 percent.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-day Relative Strength Index for Bitcoin currently reads 49, placing it squarely in neutral territory on the daily timeframe. This represents a slight recovery from the July 13 reading of 48.9, but remains below the 50 midline that separates bullish from bearish momentum.

On the 4-hour chart, the RSI oscillates between 42 and 55, reflecting the choppy trading conditions and lack of clear directional bias. The hourly RSI shows more volatility, bouncing between 38 and 58 within individual trading sessions. This intraday RSI range suggests scalp-worthy moves for active traders but offers limited guidance for swing-position sizing.

The RSI moving average sits at 47.7, confirming that short-term momentum remains slightly negative despite the recent stabilization. A sustained move above 55 on the daily RSI would signal the first meaningful bullish momentum since the rejection from $70,000. Conversely, a break below 40 would indicate accelerating bearish pressure and increase the probability of a test of the $60,000 support zone.

No clear bullish or bearish divergences are present on the daily chart. The RSI has been range-bound between 40 and 55 since mid-June, reflecting the broader consolidation pattern. A breakout of this RSI range will likely coincide with a directional breakout in price.

## MACD Indicator

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator on the daily timeframe shows a mixed picture. The MACD line is essentially flat and trading near the signal line, reflecting the ongoing indecision. The histogram bars have been oscillating between positive and negative territory for the past two weeks, reaching neither extreme that would indicate a resolute trend.

On the weekly timeframe, the MACD is showing early signs of a potential bullish crossover. The MACD line has begun curling upward from a deeply oversold position relative to the signal line. This weekly MACD setup is similar to the pattern that preceded the 26 percent rally that began in late June, although the current histogram readings are less compressed than they were prior to that move.

The monthly MACD remains bearish, with the histogram printing consistently negative bars. The monthly MACD line is well below the signal line, reflecting the extended downtrend from the $126,080 all-time high. A monthly MACD bullish crossover typically takes several months to develop and would require sustained buying pressure across multiple weekly candles.

Traders should watch for the daily MACD to cross above the signal line with expanding histogram bars as confirmation of renewed upside momentum. A bearish cross on the daily timeframe, particularly with widening histogram bars, would argue for a retest of the lower support levels.

The 20-day SMA is trending lower and has acted as resistance on recent bounces.

On the bearish side, the 100-day and 200-day SMAs are both declining and positioned well above current price levels. Volume contracted during the most recent dip to $62,500, which is a mildly constructive signal suggesting that sellers are not aggressively adding to short positions at these levels.

On-Balance Volume has been essentially flat over the past two weeks, confirming that neither buyers nor sellers have seized control. OBV remains below its 20-day moving average, indicating that cumulative volume flow favors sellers over this timeframe, but the gap is narrowing.

## Chart Patterns

The dominant chart pattern on the daily timeframe is a symmetrical triangle or coil formation. Price has been making successively lower highs and higher lows since the rejection at $70,000, compressing into a narrowing range. This pattern is typically resolved by a sharp breakout in either direction, with the measured move target calculated as the height of the triangle projected from the breakout point.

The symmetrical triangle has been developing for approximately three weeks. The apex of the triangle is approaching, suggesting that a breakout is imminent within the next five to seven trading days. The lack of a clear directional bias in the pattern itself means that traders should prepare for a move in either direction with equal probability until confirmed by volume and follow-through.

On the weekly chart, the pattern resembles a descending broadening wedge, which is typically a bullish reversal pattern. The weekly wedge has been developing since the all-time high in early 2025, with multiple lower highs and lower lows but with expanding range. A breakout above the upper trendline of this weekly wedge would be a significant long-term bullish signal with implications for the multi-month trend.

The Bollinger Bands on the daily chart have narrowed considerably over the past two weeks, with the upper and lower bands converging. The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart shows Bitcoin trading below the cloud, which is a bearish signal for the intermediate trend. The cloud itself is flat to slightly bearish, with the Senkou Span A below Senkou Span B, indicating that resistance above current levels is stronger than support below.

The Chikou Span remains below price and below the cloud, which is a confirmatory bearish signal. For a sustained reversal, the Chikou Span would need to cross above both price and the cloud, a process that typically takes multiple weeks to develop.
