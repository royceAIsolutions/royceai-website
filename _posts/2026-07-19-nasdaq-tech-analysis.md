## Current Price Action

QQQ closed Friday July 17 at $695.33, down 1.5% from the prior close of $705.94 and extending a multi-week correction from the June 3 all-time high of $748.65. The Invesco QQQ Trust, which tracks the Nasdaq-100 index, has now dropped 7.1% from its 52-week high as profit-taking and sector rotation hit mega-cap technology names.

The daily range was wide at $686.76 to $702.30, with the $686.76 intraday low representing the weakest level since May 19 when QQQ bottomed at $695.25 during a prior pullback. Volume surged to 54.1 million shares — 1.3 times the 30-day average of 41.8 million — confirming institutional distribution during the Friday selloff.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-day Relative Strength Index sits at 46.0, firmly in neutral territory with a bearish lean. This reading is notable because it is not yet oversold — meaning QQQ has room to fall further before reaching levels that typically trigger mean-reversion buying.

Key RSI observations:

- RSI has declined from 68 on July 3 to 46 currently, reflecting consistent selling pressure over the past two weeks
- The RSI is below its 14-day signal line, confirming short-term bearish momentum
- Oversold territory (RSI below 30) was last seen during the June 5 panic when RSI dropped to 28 and QQQ bounced from $705
- If selling continues, RSI could reach 35-38 before finding meaningful support
- Bullish divergence (price making lower lows while RSI makes higher lows) is not yet present — a reversal signal has not formed

## MACD Analysis

The MACD indicator paints a bearish picture. The MACD line sits at -2.12, below the signal line at 0.56, with the histogram at -2.68 and expanding downward.

This configuration indicates:

- The MACD crossed below its signal line on July 10 when QQQ was near $725, providing an early sell signal
- The histogram has been negative and expanding for seven consecutive trading days
- The MACD line crossed below the zero line on July 16, confirming the bearish shift from a medium-term perspective
- For a bullish reversal, the MACD would need to stop expanding lower and begin converging toward the signal line — not yet happening
- The last time MACD was this negative was during the June 3-10 correction when QQQ fell from $748 to $693

## Moving Averages

The moving average structure is deteriorating rapidly:

- **SMA 10:** $714.29 — QQQ closed $18.96 below, the widest gap since June 10
- **SMA 20:** $718.35 — $23.02 below, price has been under the 20-day SMA for 6 consecutive sessions
- **SMA 50:** $717.71 — Below the 50-day for 5 consecutive sessions, a bearish mid-term signal
- **SMA 100:** Approximately $699 — Price broke below this level intraday on Friday and closed right at it, a critical test
- **SMA 200:** $604 (estimated from SPY MA200 correlation) — $90 below current price, providing a long-term floor

The moving averages are arranged in bearish alignment with the 10-day below the 20-day and 50-day. This "short-term below medium-term" configuration — commonly called a bearish crossover setup — increases the probability of a longer-lasting correction rather than a quick dip.

## Volume Analysis

Friday's 54.1 million shares represented the highest volume day since July 2 (51.1 million). Elevated volume during a price decline confirms distribution — institutions are selling, not accumulating.

On-Balance Volume (OBV) analysis based on the sequence pattern shows:

- Volume spiked on June 5 at 99.6 million during the crash from $731 to $705 — distribution climax
- The subsequent recovery to $744 on June 15 occurred on below-average volume (46.7 million), suggesting weak buying conviction
- Recent selloffs from July 13 onward have been on above-average volume, confirming the downtrend has force behind it
- The 30-day average volume of 41.8 million is elevated compared to the 14-day average of 36.1 million, indicating heightened activity overall — consistent with a correction phase

## Chart Patterns

QQQ is forming a lower-high, lower-low sequence since the June 3 peak. The structure shows:

- **Peak 1:** $748.65 on June 3
- **Trough 1:** $686.37 on June 9 (intraday wick)
- **Peak 2:** $745.45 on June 22 (lower high)
- **Trough 2:** $695.33 on July 17 (lower low — continuing)

This pattern is consistent with a developing downtrend channel. The width from peak 1 to trough 1 is approximately $62. If this pattern plays out symmetrically, the next measured move target from the June 22 lower high of $745 would be approximately $683.

A descending channel can be drawn connecting the June 3 high ($748.65) with the June 22 lower high ($745.45) as the upper trendline, and the June 9 low ($686.37) with the July 17 low ($686.76) as the lower trendline. QQQ is currently riding the lower channel boundary, suggesting a potential bounce is possible — but a breakdown below $686 would open the door to $660.

## Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the March-to-June rally (from $622 to $748.65):

- **23.6%:** $718.70 — Already broken, now acting as resistance
- **38.2%:** $700.15 — Broken intraday on Friday
- **50%:** $685.33 — Only $10 below current price and coincides with the channel support
- **61.8%:** $670.36 — The "golden ratio" level; would represent a healthy correction within a bull market
- **78.6%:** $649.04 — Deep retracement; breaking this would challenge the primary uptrend

The 61.8% Fibonacci level at $670 aligns with the June 2026 consolidation zone and represents the most probable target if selling continues.

## Bollinger Bands

- The lower band is likely around $675-680 based on the band width expansion
- Price has hugged the lower band since July 16, indicating strong downside momentum
- Band width is expanding, suggesting volatility is increasing — not contracting toward a breakout
- A close back above the middle band ($718) would be needed to signal the selling pressure has exhausted

- **Kijun-sen (Base Line):** The 26-period midpoint is flattening near $715

- The "cloud twist" — where Senkou Span A crosses below Senkou Span B — occurred around July 7, creating a bearish cloud structure that will persist for several weeks

## Market Sentiment

The broader market regime as measured by the Meridian macro dashboard is Green (risk-on), with VIX at 18.77 (normal range) and the S&P 500 trading 7.2% above its 200-day moving average. However, the Nasdaq has been the weakest major index in recent weeks as technology stocks lead the pullback.

Key sentiment indicators:

- The Fear and Greed Index for crypto sits at 29 (Fear), suggesting risk-off sentiment in growth assets broadly
- Credit spreads remain tight at 2.71% (BAML HY OAS), indicating no systemic stress
- SPY is at $743.29, still above its MA200 of $693.44 — the broader market is holding up better than tech

The divergence between SPY (near highs) and QQQ (correcting 7%) suggests sector rotation out of mega-cap technology into value, cyclicals, and small caps — a common mid-cycle rotation pattern.

## Trading Recommendations

**Bearish Scenario (Probability: 55%):** QQQ continues lower toward the 61.8% Fibonacci level at $670. The breakdown below $700 on elevated volume suggests institutional selling is not yet complete. A retest of $686 and a break below would confirm this scenario. Target: $670-675. Duration: 1-3 weeks.

**Bullish Scenario (Probability: 30%):** QQQ finds support at $686-690 (channel support + round number) and stages a recovery toward $718 (SMA 20). This would require a catalyst such as better-than-expected big tech earnings or a Fed policy shift. Target: $718-725. Duration: 2-4 weeks.

**Neutral Scenario (Probability: 15%):** QQQ trades in a $686-$718 range for 2-3 weeks, working off the oversold condition without making a decisive break in either direction.

**Key Trigger Levels:**
- **Above $718:** Bearish thesis invalidated; trend turns neutral
- **Below $686:** Bearish thesis confirmed; next target $660
- **Above $730:** Trend turns bullish; back within the long-term uptrend
- **Below $660:** Structural damage; primary uptrend at risk

## Risk Factors

- Big tech earnings season begins in the coming weeks — Microsoft, Apple, Amazon, Nvidia, and Meta could reverse the trend with strong reports
- The Fed remains a wild card: any dovish pivot would likely boost growth stocks immediately
- Geopolitical risks (tariffs, regulatory action on AI) could accelerate selling
- The 10-year yield trajectory affects tech valuations directly — rising yields are headwind, falling yields tailwind
- Options expiration dynamics could exaggerate moves in either direction

## Conclusion

QQQ is in a confirmed short-term downtrend with bearish signals across RSI, MACD, moving averages, and volume. The 7.1% correction from the June 3 all-time high has room to extend toward $670 (61.8% Fibonacci retracement) if the $686 support level fails. However, the broader market remains in a risk-on regime, and the selling has been orderly rather than panicked. Traders should watch $686 as the line in the sand — a break below opens the door to deeper losses, while a reclaim of $718 would signal the correction is over. For now, the path of least resistance is lower, but the bull market structure above the MA200 remains intact as long as QQQ holds above $660.
