# Solana (SOL) Technical Analysis — July 3, 2026

## Current Price Action

Solana is trading at approximately $81.61 as of July 3, 2026, with a 24-hour range of $80.38 to $81.79. The asset opened the session at $80.65 and has shown moderate upward movement, reflecting a cautiously bullish intraday bias. SOL has been consolidating in the low-to-mid $80 range over the past several sessions, building a potential base after a period of decline from higher levels seen earlier in the year. The current price action suggests a market in equilibrium, with buyers and sellers evenly matched near a key psychological level.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe is reading near 48-50, placing SOL in neutral territory. This is a significant improvement from the oversold readings below 30 that were observed during the June selloff. The recovery of RSI from oversold levels back toward the midline is a constructive signal, suggesting that selling pressure has exhausted and buyers are beginning to step in. On the 4-hour chart, RSI is around 52, showing a mild bullish bias in the short term. The weekly RSI remains below 50, indicating that the broader trend is still consolidating rather than reversing. A sustained move above 55 on the daily RSI would confirm a shift in momentum toward bullish territory.

## MACD Analysis

The MACD on the daily chart shows a bullish crossover, with the MACD line moving above the signal line for the first time in several weeks. The histogram has turned positive and is expanding, indicating building upward momentum. On the weekly timeframe, the MACD is still negative but appears to be narrowing, suggesting that the bearish momentum that dominated the past two months is fading. The MACD on the 4-hour chart is firmly bullish with the histogram at its highest level in the current cycle, supporting the case for continued short-term upside. Traders should watch for the weekly MACD to cross above zero as a confirmation of a broader trend shift.

## Moving Averages

Solana's price is currently trading above the 20-day SMA, which is flattening around $79.50, suggesting short-term momentum is turning positive. The 50-day SMA sits near $84.00, providing overhead resistance. The 100-day SMA is at $91.00, and the 200-day SMA is near $88.00, both well above current price. This bearish alignment — price below the 100 and 200-day SMAs — confirms that the longer-term trend remains downward. However, the 20-day SMA is acting as a critical support level, and if SOL can reclaim the 50-day SMA at $84.00, it would signal a potential trend change. The EMA 12/26 crossover is bullish on the daily chart, with the 12 EMA crossing above the 26 EMA, providing additional confirmation of near-term momentum.

## Volume Analysis

Trading volume has been relatively subdued over the past week, which is typical during consolidation phases. The declining volume during the recent price stabilization suggests that sellers are losing interest rather than accumulating. If SOL breaks above resistance with increasing volume, it would provide strong confirmation of a genuine breakout. The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator is showing a slight uptick, suggesting that buying pressure is gradually building. Volume Price Trend (VPT) is also turning higher, supporting the bullish case. Spot volume on major exchanges remains healthy, with SOL consistently ranking among the top 10 cryptocurrencies by daily trading volume.

Key support levels are identified at $80.00, which is a major psychological round number that has held multiple tests over the past week. Below that, $78.00 serves as the next support level, corresponding to the June swing low. The $75.00 level represents a critical support zone, as a break below this would open the door to the $70.00 area, which was last tested during the June selloff.

On the upside, immediate resistance is at $84.00, where the 50-day SMA converges. The next significant resistance is at $88.00, aligning with the 200-day SMA. A breakout above $88.00 would target the $91.00-$92.00 zone, where the 100-day SMA sits. The $95.00 level represents a major resistance from June price action.

## Chart Patterns

Solana is forming a potential double bottom pattern on the daily chart, with lows near $78.00 in mid-June and late June. This pattern targets a move toward $92.00-$95.00, which is the pattern's measured move. The neckline of this potential double bottom is at $84.00-$85.00, where the 50-day SMA provides resistance. A confirmed break above the neckline with volume would be a strong bullish signal. In the shorter term, SOL has been trading within a descending channel since March, and the current consolidation near the lower boundary of this channel could represent a potential reversal zone.

## Fibonacci Retracement

Using the March high to June low swing, the 38.2% Fibonacci retracement level sits at $84.50, closely aligned with the 50-day SMA, creating a strong resistance zone. The 50% retracement is at $88.00, and the 61.8% level is at $92.00. SOL has already recovered from the June low and is now testing the 23.6% level at $80.50, which is acting as support. A move above the 38.2% level would open the path toward the 50% and 61.8% retracement levels. The Fibonacci extension levels suggest that if SOL breaks above the June high, the 1.272 extension targets $102.00 and the 1.618 extension targets $110.00.

## Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart shows price trading below the cloud, indicating a bearish overall trend. However, the Conversion Line (Tenkan-sen) is flattening and approaching the Base Line (Kijun-sen), with a potential bullish cross developing. The Lagging Span (Chikou Span) is still below price but has started to flatten, which is a preliminary sign of potential trend improvement. A move above the cloud would be a significant bullish signal, with the cloud's lower boundary near $85.00 providing resistance.

The move from the June low at $78.00 to the current $81.61 level could represent Wave 1 of a 5-wave impulse within the larger Wave A. If this count is correct, SOL could see a pullback to the $79.00-$80.00 area (Wave 2) before rallying toward $86.00-$88.00 (Wave 3). The alternative count suggests this could be a complex flat correction, where SOL trades in a range between $78.00 and $88.00 before resolving higher.
