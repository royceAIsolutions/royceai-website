# SP500 Technical Analysis Pullback from Highs

## Current Price Action

The S&P 500 Index (SPX) closed at 7,483.24 on July 2, 2026, down 0.13 percent from the previous close of 7,490. The SPY ETF, which tracks the index, closed at $744.78 after trading in a range of $740.03 to $751.31 during the session. Volume came in at 57.5 million shares, below the 20-day average of 64.6 million, suggesting reduced participation as the market enters the holiday-shortened week.

The index has been in a corrective phase since reaching its all-time high of 7,604 on June 2, 2026. From that peak, SPX has pulled back approximately 1.5 percent, consolidating gains from the strong rally that began in March 2026. The 52-week range spans from 6,178 to 7,604, placing the current price in the upper quartile of the yearly range.

The June 2 high of 7,604 represents a critical resistance level. The index has tested this zone twice and failed to break through, suggesting selling pressure at these levels. The market is now in a short-term downtrend within a larger bullish structure.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-day Relative Strength Index for SPY stands at 54.8, placing it in neutral territory above the 50 midpoint but well below the overbought threshold of 70. This reading indicates that the recent pullback has relieved the overbought conditions that prevailed during the May to June rally when RSI peaked above 72.

The RSI reading of 54.8 is significant because it suggests room for movement in either direction. If the index breaks above resistance, RSI can move into the 60 to 70 range without becoming overbought. If selling pressure intensifies, a drop below 50 would confirm bearish momentum, with 40 and 30 serving as subsequent support levels.

On a weekly timeframe, RSI is at 58, indicating a neutral-to-bullish bias for the intermediate term. The weekly RSI has been declining since the June peak, suggesting momentum is cooling but has not yet turned negative.

## MACD Analysis

The MACD indicator shows a narrowing positive reading. The MACD line (12-day EMA minus 26-day EMA) stands at 1.32, while the signal line is at 1.10. The histogram has been declining over the past two weeks, indicating that bullish momentum is fading.

The MACD crossed above the signal line in late June, generating a buy signal that propelled the index from the 7,250 area to the 7,480 area. However, the narrowing histogram suggests this signal is losing strength. A bearish cross below the signal line would confirm the corrective phase is deepening.

The MACD remains above the zero line, confirming that the intermediate-term trend is still bullish. The zero line at approximately 0.50 serves as a critical threshold. A MACD move below zero would signal a shift from correction to potential trend reversal.

## Moving Averages

The moving average structure presents a mixed picture. Price is trading above all major moving averages, which is a bullish configuration. However, the short-term averages are flattening, suggesting the uptrend is losing momentum.

The 5-day SMA sits at $741.46, and price at $744.78 is above it, indicating very short-term bullish momentum. The 10-day SMA at $739.96 and 20-day SMA at $741.08 are both below price, confirming the short-term uptrend from the June 26 low of $728.99.

The 50-day SMA at $737.43 is the most important support level. Price has held above the 50-day SMA since the April lows, and this moving average has acted as dynamic support throughout the rally. A close below $737 would be a significant bearish signal.

The 200-day SMA is approximately $685, well below current price, indicating the long-term trend remains strongly bullish. The distance between price and the 200-day SMA is about 8.7 percent, which is historically normal for a mature bull market.

The moving average alignment is bullish with the 5-day above the 10-day above the 20-day above the 50-day above the 200-day. However, the 5-day SMA has been oscillating around price over the past week, suggesting indecision.

## Volume Analysis

Volume analysis reveals important clues about market conviction. The June 5 sell-off saw volume spike to 93.9 million shares, the highest in the 3-month period, indicating panic selling. The subsequent recovery on June 15 saw strong volume of 60.1 million, suggesting institutional buying.

The June 26 low of $728.99 was accompanied by volume of 71 million shares, above average and indicating selling climax characteristics. The rally from that low to the current $744.78 has occurred on declining volume, with the July 2 session at 57.5 million shares below the 20-day average. This divergence between rising price and falling volume suggests the recovery lacks strong conviction.

On-Balance Volume (OBV) has been trending sideways since the June 2 peak, confirming that buying pressure has not returned to the levels seen during the May rally. A break above the June 2 OBV high would be a strong bullish signal.

The average volume over the past 20 days is 64.6 million shares, down from the 30-day average of 64.0 million. The declining volume profile during the July recovery suggests a bearish divergence.

The third support level is at 7,250 (SPY $725), the June 26 low and the lowest point of the recent correction. This level is critical because it represents the bottom of the pullback from the June 2 high. A break below 7,250 would signal a deeper correction.

The fourth support level is at 7,170 (SPY $717), the May zone of consolidation. This level would come into play on a significant breakdown below 7,250.

Resistance levels are equally clear. The first resistance is at 7,513 (SPY $751), the July 2 intraday high. This level was tested and rejected in the most recent session.

The second resistance is at 7,586 (SPY $758), the June 1 high. This level acted as resistance during the June recovery attempt.

The third resistance is the all-time high at 7,604 (SPY $760.40), the June 2 peak. This is the most significant resistance level and the bulls ultimate target. A breakout above 7,604 would open the door for further upside.

## Chart Patterns

The daily chart shows a potential descending channel pattern forming since the June 2 high. The upper trendline connects the June 2 high at 7,604 with the June 11 high at 7,400 and the July 2 high at 7,513. The lower trendline connects the June 5 low at 7,375 with the June 26 low at 7,250.

Price is currently testing the upper boundary of this channel. A breakout above the channel would be a bullish signal targeting new highs. A rejection from the upper boundary would suggest a retest of the lower channel boundary near 7,250.

The pattern from the March 2026 low at 6,320 to the June 2 high at 7,604 represents a complete impulsive wave structure. The current pullback from the June 2 high is likely a corrective wave within the larger uptrend.

The CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) has been trading in the 14 to 18 range, indicating moderate fear but not panic. This is consistent with a healthy correction within an uptrend. VIX below 12 would indicate complacency, while above 20 would signal significant fear.

The Fear and Greed Index is likely in the neutral zone given the moderate pullback from highs. The index has been gradually declining from the greed zone in May to the current neutral reading.

Institutional flows show a rotation out of technology and into defensive sectors, consistent with a corrective phase. Healthcare, utilities, and consumer staples have been relative outperformers in recent weeks.

## Risk Factors

The Federal Reserve's monetary policy stance remains the primary risk factor. Any hawkish surprise that suggests higher for longer interest rates could trigger a deeper correction. The CPI and PPI releases in July will be closely watched.

Geopolitical risks including trade tensions and global economic slowdown concerns could pressure markets. The upcoming earnings season will be critical for validating the current valuation levels.

The seasonal pattern for July is historically mixed, with the market often experiencing volatility during the summer months. Lower trading volumes during the holiday period can amplify price movements in either direction.

## Conclusion

The S&P 500 is in a healthy correction within a strong uptrend. The technical indicators are mixed with neutral RSI, fading MACD momentum, but a bullish moving average structure. The Fibonacci retracement levels provide clear support and resistance zones for traders to monitor.

The key levels to watch are support at 7,250 and resistance at 7,604. A break above 7,604 would confirm the resumption of the uptrend and target the next Fibonacci extension. A break below 7,250 would signal a deeper correction with potential targets at 7,000 or lower.
