## Why Dealership BDCs and Human Receptionists Cannot Keep Up

The traditional solution to the missed-call problem has been the Business Development Center (BDC) — a dedicated team of representatives whose job is to answer inbound calls, respond to internet leads, and follow up with prospects. On paper, the BDC model makes sense. In practice, it is failing. Ringlyn AI's 2026 analysis found that dealership BDCs miss 30% or more of inbound calls even when fully staffed, and the cost of maintaining a BDC is staggering at over $90,000 per year per representative including salary, benefits, training, and overhead. A typical dealership BDC requires three to five representatives to cover business hours, meaning the annual cost ranges from $270,000 to $450,000 before factoring in turnover, training, and management overhead.

Human receptionists face inherent limitations that no amount of training can overcome. They need breaks, sick days, and vacation time. They cannot work 24 hours a day, seven days a week, 365 days a year. They handle only one call at a time, creating hold times that frustrate customers and drive them to competitors. During peak hours — lunchtime, end of day, Saturday mornings — the call volume overwhelms even the most experienced receptionist, causing calls to ring indefinitely or route to a voicemail box that most callers never leave a message in. Foureyes' study of 22,500 dealerships found that the average dealership spends $45,000 per month on advertising to generate approximately 150 internet leads, yet fails to make a real phone call on 43% of those leads. That means nearly half of every advertising dollar is effectively wasted on leads that never hear a human voice.

The voicemail problem is particularly damaging. Industry data consistently shows that 85% of callers who reach voicemail will not leave a message. They hang up and call the next dealership on their list. For the 15% who do leave a message, the voicemail-to-booked-appointment conversion rate is under 10%, according to Twig AI's May 2026 research. This means that of every 100 missed calls, only 1 to 2 will eventually convert to a booked appointment through the voicemail callback process. The other 98 callers are lost permanently, taking their business to a competitor who answered the phone.
