## Current Price Action

The Invesco QQQ Trust (QQQ), the flagship Nasdaq 100 ETF, is trading around $705 as of June 8, 2026, following a sharp pullback from its recent highs. After reaching a 52-week high of $748.65 earlier in June, the ETF has experienced a significant correction driven by AI-sector valuation concerns and a strong jobs report that pushed the 10-year Treasury yield higher. The recent sell-off has erased approximately 6% from the peak, placing QQQ at a critical juncture where several technical indicators are flashing mixed signals.

The broader context for this pullback stems from what analysts are calling the "Dot-AI Bubble" unwind. On June 5, a jobs shock sent yields spiking and triggered a rotation out of mega-cap tech stocks. Nvidia, the largest QQQ holding, saw its $4.5 trillion valuation questioned as earnings growth expectations face reality checks. Fortune reported on June 8 that top analysts fear the AI bubble is popping, with Acadian Asset Management's Owen Lamont drawing direct parallels to the 1999 tech bubble.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-period Relative Strength Index (RSI) on QQQ currently reads 77.32, placing the ETF firmly in overbought territory. This is down from even higher readings during the early June rally but remains above the critical 70 threshold that typically signals an impending pullback or consolidation phase.

On the daily timeframe, the RSI has been diverging from price action since the early June highs. While price made a higher high at $748.65, the RSI failed to confirm with a corresponding higher high, creating a bearish divergence pattern. This non-confirmation is often one of the earliest warnings of trend exhaustion.

The weekly RSI is also elevated near 72, suggesting that the overbought condition exists across multiple timeframes. Historically, when both daily and weekly RSI readings are above 70 simultaneously, the probability of a 5-10% correction within the following two weeks increases significantly.

On the 4-hour chart, RSI has dipped below 40 during the recent sell-off, indicating that short-term momentum has shifted decisively bearish. The RSI on this timeframe would need to reclaim 50 to signal that buyers are regaining control.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) on QQQ is showing early warning signs of a bearish crossover. The MACD line currently sits at 21.78, while the signal line has begun to flatten. The histogram, which measures the difference between the two lines, has been contracting since the June 5 high, indicating that bullish momentum is fading.

A bearish MACD crossover would be a significant technical event, especially if accompanied by increasing volume. On the weekly chart, the MACD remains bullish but the histogram is also contracting, mirroring the daily timeframe's loss of momentum.

The MACD's current trajectory suggests that any further downside pressure could trigger the crossover, which would likely accelerate selling as trend-following traders exit long positions. However, if QQQ can stabilize and base near current levels, the MACD could flatten and potentially re-accelerate, avoiding the crossover entirely.

## Moving Averages

QQQ's moving average structure remains technically bullish, though the gap between price and the averages has narrowed considerably during the pullback.

The 50-day simple moving average (SMA) sits at approximately $691.60 — just 2% below the current price. This level represents the first major support test since the ETF broke above it in May. A clean break below the 50-day SMA would be a significant bearish signal, as it would indicate that the short-term trend has failed.

The 200-day SMA is at $628.70, a full 11% below current prices. The fact that the 50-day SMA remains well above the 200-day SMA confirms that the "golden cross" pattern is still intact. This formation, where the 50-day crosses above the 200-day, is widely regarded as a bullish long-term signal. However, the narrowing distance between price and the 50-day SMA is concerning.

The 20-day EMA (exponential moving average) has already been violated, suggesting that the very short-term trend has turned bearish. The 100-day SMA near $670 provides an intermediate support level between the 50-day and 200-day.

Key moving average levels to watch:
- 20-day EMA: ~$725 (resistance, already broken)
- 50-day SMA: ~$691 (critical support)
- 100-day SMA: ~$670 (secondary support)
- 200-day SMA: ~$628 (major long-term support)

## Volume Analysis

Volume patterns during the recent sell-off have been telling. On June 5, QQQ saw volume of 12.93 million shares — approximately 193% of the average daily volume. This surge on a down day confirms that institutional distribution is underway. Large players are reducing exposure to tech-heavy positions as AI valuation concerns mount.

The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator has rolled over from its June highs, confirming that volume is flowing out of the ETF rather than accumulating. This divergence between price and OBV at the peak is a classic sign of distribution.

The Volume-Price Trend (VPT) indicator is also showing weakness, with the indicator declining faster than price during the pullback. This suggests that the selling pressure is more aggressive than a typical profit-taking event.

## Support and Resistance Levels

Key support levels to monitor:
- S1: $691 — 50-day SMA (immediate support)
- S2: $670 — 100-day SMA and prior consolidation zone
- S3: $660 — Psychological round number and May breakout level
- S4: $628 — 200-day SMA (major long-term support)

Key resistance levels to watch:
- R1: $725 — 20-day EMA and recent breakdown point
- R2: $748 — 52-week high
- R3: $750 — Pivot point third-level resistance per Barchart
- R4: $775 — Projected target if uptrend resumes

The $691 level is the most critical near-term support. A daily close below this level would open the door to the $670 area and potentially signal a deeper correction. Conversely, a bounce from $691 would confirm the 50-day SMA as a reliable support level.

## Chart Patterns

The daily chart is showing a potential bearish flag pattern, characterized by a sharp vertical decline (the flagpole) followed by a brief consolidation (the flag). The flagpole formed during the June 5-8 sell-off, and the flag is currently developing as price attempts to stabilize. A breakdown below the lower boundary of the flag would target a measured move toward the $660 area.

On the weekly chart, QQQ has formed an extended bullish channel since the October 2025 lows. The upper boundary of this channel was tested near $748, and the ETF has now pulled back toward the middle of the channel. A test of the lower boundary near $650 would not be unusual in a healthy bull market correction.

## Fibonacci Retracement

Using the March 2026 low near $642 to the June 2026 high at $748, the Fibonacci retracement levels are:
- 23.6%: $723 (already broken)
- 38.2%: $707 (current price zone — testing this level)
- 50.0%: $695 (near the 50-day SMA)
- 61.8%: $682 (typical correction target in a bull market)

The fact that QQQ is testing the 38.2% Fibonacci level around $707 suggests the correction is in the normal range for a bull market pullback. A breakdown below the 61.8% level at $682 would indicate a deeper correction and potentially a trend change.

## Bollinger Bands

The Bollinger Bands on QQQ have widened significantly during the sell-off, reflecting increased volatility. The upper band was tested during the June rally, and price has now fallen below the middle band (20-day SMA), settling in the lower half of the bands.

Band width (Bollinger Bandwidth) has expanded to levels typically associated with trend changes. The %B indicator, which measures price position within the bands, has fallen from above 1.0 (above the upper band) to approximately 0.3, indicating that price is in the lower third of the bands.

A characteristic of healthy bull markets is that price tends to find support near the lower Bollinger Band and then reverse higher. If QQQ reaches or approaches the lower band near $675-680, that would represent a potential buying opportunity for swing traders.

## Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud analysis on QQQ shows that price is still above the cloud, which is a bullish long-term signal. However, the Tenkan-sen (conversion line) has crossed below the Kijun-sen (base line), forming a bearish cross that warns of potential further weakness.

The cloud itself is flat to slightly bullish, with the Senkou Span A above Senkou Span B. The cloud provides support near the $680 area for the next several weeks. If price falls into the cloud, the bullish trend would be in jeopardy.

The Chikou Span (lagging span) is still above price from 26 periods ago, confirming the long-term uptrend, but it is beginning to flatten.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

From an Elliott Wave perspective, QQQ appears to have completed a five-wave advance from the October 2025 lows. The top near $748 likely represents the end of Wave (3) or Wave (5) of a larger degree impulse.

If the current decline is Wave (4), the typical retracement target is between the 38.2% and 50% Fibonacci levels, or approximately $695-707. A Wave (4) correction should not retrace more than 61.8% ($682) to maintain the bullish count.

Alternatively, if the entire impulse is complete, the current decline could be the beginning of a larger ABC correction that could retrace 50-78.6% of the entire bull move from the lows. This bearish count would target $580-640 over the coming months.

The most likely scenario, given the fundamentals, is a Wave (4) correction that finds support near the 50-day SMA before Wave (5) pushes QQQ to new highs above $750.

## Market Sentiment

Market sentiment has shifted bearish in the short term. The put-call ratio on QQQ options is at 1.0, indicating a balanced but cautious options market. Implied volatility has spiked, with the IV rank reaching 82.54% — meaning volatility is at 82% of its 52-week range.

The Fear & Greed Index has likely moved from "Extreme Greed" territory into "Greed" or "Neutral" during the recent sell-off. The JPMorgan warning about "a complex mix of geopolitical fractures, tariffs, high asset prices, and sticky inflation risks" has added to the cautious tone.

A Bank of America survey shows most global fund managers now call AI stocks "a bubble," which is a contrarian signal worth noting. When everyone agrees a bubble is forming, it often has more room to run before the actual top.

## Trading Recommendations

**Bullish Scenario:** If QQQ holds the $691 support level (50-day SMA) and reverses on increasing volume, the uptrend remains intact. A move back above $725 would signal that the correction is over. Target: $750-775. Stop loss: $680.

**Bearish Scenario:** A breakdown below $691 on elevated volume would suggest the correction has further to go. The next support zone at $660-670 would become the target. A break below $628 (200-day SMA) would indicate a trend change. Stop loss for shorts: $715.

**Neutral/Sideways:** QQQ could consolidate between $680 and $725 for 2-4 weeks while the moving averages catch up. This range-bound trading would offer opportunities for options sellers but limited directional profits.

## Risk Factors

The primary risk to the bullish thesis is the AI valuation unwind. Fortune's June 8 report specifically warns that analysts are "getting out over their proverbial skis" on earnings growth expectations for mega-cap tech stocks. If the AI trade continues to unwind, QQQ could see accelerated selling regardless of technical levels.

Additional risks include geopolitical tensions (US-Iran negotiations stalling), the 10-year yield remaining elevated, and the IPO liquidity drain as major companies like SpaceX potentially come to market.

## Conclusion

QQQ is at a critical technical inflection point. The golden cross between the 50-day and 200-day SMAs remains intact, and the long-term trend is still bullish. However, the RSI divergence, weakening MACD, and surge in volume on the sell-off all point to further downside risk in the near term.

The $691 level (50-day SMA) is the line in the sand. Bulls need to defend this level to maintain control. A bounce from here would present a buying opportunity with a target of $750+. A breakdown would signal a deeper correction toward $660-670.

Traders should approach with caution, reduce position sizes, and wait for confirmation of support before committing capital. The fundamentals are mixed, and the technicals are sending conflicting signals — a recipe for volatility in the days ahead.
