## Why Dental Practices Are Leaving Revenue on the Table

The average dental practice in the United States receives 40-60 phone calls per day. New patient bookings, appointment changes, insurance verification questions, and emergency walk-in requests all come through the phone. But here is the problem that most practice owners do not realize until they run the numbers: 15-20 percent of those calls go unanswered.

When a potential new patient calls a dental practice and nobody answers, that person does not just wait patiently. They call the next practice on their list. In an era where same-day scheduling is the norm and patients expect immediate responses, every missed call is a permanent loss. According to industry data from Dental Intelligence and MGMA, a single new patient is worth an average of $1,200-$2,500 in annual production to a dental practice. With most practices missing 6-12 calls per day, the annual revenue leakage adds up to $120,000 or more per location.

## The Real Cost of Missed Calls for Dentists

The cost of missed calls goes beyond lost new patients. Existing patients who cannot reach the office for scheduling changes or emergency questions become frustrated. They start looking for practices that offer better communication. Studies from the American Dental Association show that poor phone customer service is one of the top three reasons patients leave a dental practice, alongside cost and location convenience.

For dental practices running on tight margins — where hygiene production covers overhead and doctor production covers profit — losing even 5 percent of new patient flow can be the difference between a profitable month and a break-even month. A practice producing $80,000 monthly needs every single new patient inquiry to maintain growth targets.

Consider the math on a typical four-operatory dental practice:

- 50 calls per day average
- 10 calls missed (20 percent)
- 5 of those are new patient inquiries
- Each new patient worth $1,600 annually
- Annual loss: 5 calls x 22 days x 12 months x $1,600 = $211,200

That is over two hundred thousand dollars walking out the door because nobody picked up the phone.

## Why Traditional Answering Services Fall Short

Many dental practices have tried human-based answering services to solve this problem. These services typically charge $300-$800 per month and employ remote operators who take messages during off-hours. But the limitations are significant.

Human answering services can only take messages. They cannot:

- Schedule appointments directly into the practice management system
- Answer insurance questions with any depth
- Triage emergencies and book urgent slots
- Send appointment confirmations or reminders
- Handle multiple calls simultaneously during peak times
- Provide consistent scripting for brand voice and compliance

The result is that the answering service takes a message, the practice calls the patient back the next day, and by then the patient has already booked with a competitor who answered immediately. The answering service costs money but only partially solves the problem.

## Common Scheduling Mistakes That Cost Dental Practices Money

Even when calls are answered, poor scheduling practices cost dental practices significant revenue. The most common mistakes include:

- Overbooking procedures without accounting for hygiene room turnover time, leading to backups that push appointments late and frustrate patients
- Failing to maintain a same-day emergency slot, forcing urgent patients to go to competing practices or emergency rooms
- Not double-booking hygiene recall appointments during slow periods, leaving chair time unfilled
- Manual confirmation processes that rely on front desk staff remembering to call, resulting in 15-20 percent no-show rates for appointments booked more than two weeks out
- Inconsistent phone coverage during lunch breaks when the front desk staff steps away, which is when 30 percent of new patient calls actually come in
