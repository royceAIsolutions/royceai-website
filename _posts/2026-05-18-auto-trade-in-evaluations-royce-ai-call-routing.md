## Why Traditional BDC Routing Fails Trade-Ins
Most dealerships rely on human-led Business Development Centers (BDCs) or basic phone trees to route calls. Both approaches have glaring flaws when it comes to trade-in evaluations:
- **Human error**: BDC staff handling 50+ calls a day often misidentify trade-in inquiries as general sales questions, especially during peak hours (10 AM – 2 PM) when call volume spikes. A 2026 study by the Automotive BDC Association found that BDC staff misroute 19% of all calls during peak hours, with trade-in calls being the most frequently mishandled category.
- **After-hours gaps**: 34% of trade-in calls come in outside of standard business hours (5 PM – 9 AM, per PwC 2026 data), when BDCs are closed and calls go to voicemail. Weekend trade-in calls are even more likely to be missed: 47% of Saturday trade-in calls go unanswered, per NADA 2026.
- **Multilingual barriers**: 28% of U.S. auto buyers speak Spanish or Korean as their primary language, per McKinsey 2026. Basic phone trees can’t route these callers to bilingual staff, leading to frustration and hang-ups. For Korean-speaking buyers in markets like Irvine, CA, the lack of bilingual support leads to a 63% lower trade-in capture rate, per local market research.
- **Specialized knowledge gaps**: Trade-in calls for electric vehicles (EVs) require staff trained in EV battery valuations and federal tax credit eligibility. Routing these calls to general sales staff leads to inaccurate information and lost trust. Only 34% of sales staff are trained on EV trade-in processes, per Deloitte 2026, meaning most misrouted EV trade-in calls result in lost sales.
