## Why Traditional Solutions Fail to Capture Every Call

Most dealerships have tried to solve the missed call problem with traditional approaches. The results have been consistently disappointing across every conventional solution.

Human receptionists are the most common solution, but they have fundamental limitations. A single receptionist can only handle one call at a time. During peak hours, calls pile up in a queue. Customers hang up after thirty to sixty seconds of waiting. Even the best receptionists need breaks, lunch hours, and time off. Calls that come in during those windows go straight to voicemail or an automated attendant.

Call centers promise 24/7 coverage but introduce their own problems. Outsourced agents lack specific knowledge about the dealerships inventory, service pricing, and promotions. Customers get frustrated repeating their information to multiple agents. The cost of a dedicated call center runs $2,000 to $5,000 per month for basic coverage, and quality varies dramatically between shifts.

Voicemail is the default fallback, and it is the most expensive option of all. As mentioned, fewer than 3 percent of callers leave messages. Those who do leave voicemails often receive callbacks hours later, long after they have already moved on to a competitor. A voicemail box is not a revenue capture system. It is a revenue loss confirmation system.

Automated attendants and IVR trees frustrate customers even further. Studies show that 69 percent of callers prefer speaking to a human rather than navigating a phone tree. When dealerships force customers through multiple menu options before reaching a person, they risk losing the caller entirely.
