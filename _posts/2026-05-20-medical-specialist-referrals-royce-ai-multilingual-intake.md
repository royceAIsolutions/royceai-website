## Why Traditional Intake Fails Multilingual Patients
Most practices rely on human receptionists or third-party interpreter services for multilingual intake. But human staff are limited by their language skills and availability—if a Korean-speaking receptionist is out sick, those patients can't get help. Third-party interpreters charge per minute, adding hundreds of dollars in monthly costs, and often have wait times that delay urgent referrals. Manual intake also leads to errors: 30% of multilingual referrals have missing or incorrect information due to translation mistakes, per a 2026 Inc. report.

3. Delaying referrals until a bilingual staff member is available: This adds days to wait times
