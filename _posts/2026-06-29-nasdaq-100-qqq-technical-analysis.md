The Nasdaq 100, as tracked by the Invesco QQQ Trust ETF, is trading near $725 on June 29, 2026, maintaining a bullish technical structure that has delivered a 15.28% year-to-date return — significantly outperforming the broader S&P 500's 2.85% category average. While the broader market shows mixed signals with the VIX at 18.31 and credit spreads elevated at 4.0%, the Nasdaq 100 continues to benefit from strong technology sector momentum and AI-driven earnings growth.

This analysis examines the Nasdaq 100's technical posture across multiple indicators to assess whether the uptrend has further room to run or whether a correction is becoming overdue.

## Current Price Action

QQQ is trading at approximately $725, consolidating near its recent highs after a strong rally from the April low of $632. The ETF has gained approximately 14.7% from its April 2026 low, outperforming the S&P 500's 6.1% gain over the same period. The current price sits just below the 20-day simple moving average at $724.76, suggesting a brief consolidation phase after the recent advance.

On the daily chart, QQQ has been making higher highs and higher lows since the April bottom, establishing a clear uptrend structure. The most recent swing high was established at $748 on June 18, followed by a modest pullback to $718 on June 24 before recovering to current levels. This pullback was low-volume and orderly, characteristics of a healthy consolidation within an uptrend rather than a trend reversal.

The weekly chart shows QQQ has now recorded seven positive weeks out of the last nine, with the two down weeks both showing below-average volume. This is a textbook bull market pattern where corrections are shallow and brief.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) on the daily timeframe reads 58, a neutral reading that suggests room for further upside before reaching overbought territory:

- RSI has been oscillating between 45 and 72 over the past two months, indicating a healthy uptrend with regular pullbacks
- The 14-day RSI made a higher low at 45 during the June 24 pullback while price made a lower low — a bullish divergence that often precedes continuation moves
- On the weekly RSI, the reading is 65, approaching but not yet reaching overbought (70+)
- The RSI on the 4-hour chart shows 52, confirming the short-term consolidation is neither overbought nor oversold

The RSI structure is notably different from the overheated readings seen in late 2025 when QQQ's weekly RSI reached 78 before a 12% correction. Current readings suggest the uptrend has room to extend before momentum becomes a concern.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) on the daily chart is firmly bullish:

- MACD line: +8.5 (above zero and rising)
- Signal line: +6.2
- Histogram: +2.3 and expanding (bullish momentum accelerating)

The MACD made a bullish crossover on May 8 when the MACD line crossed above the signal line, and the gap has been widening since then. This is the longest continuous bullish MACD reading since the November 2025 breakout. On the weekly chart, the MACD is at its most positive reading since the 2023 AI rally began.

A potential caution signal: the 4-hour MACD histogram has been making lower highs over the past five days while price has held steady. This bearish divergence on a shorter timeframe could indicate that the near-term momentum is decelerating, potentially setting up a pullback to the 50-day SMA at $702.79.

For the bullish structure to be invalidated, QQQ would need to break below the 200-day SMA at $632 — a decline of approximately 12.8% from current levels.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count from the April low suggests QQQ is in Wave 3 of an impulse sequence:

- Wave 1: $632 to $685 (April 7 to April 28) — 8.4% gain
- Wave 2: $685 to $658 (April 28 to May 7) — 4.0% retracement (38.2% of Wave 1)
- Wave 3: $658 to current (May 7 to present) — 10.2% gain and extending
- Wave 4: Expected to pull back to $700-$710 (38.2% retracement of Wave 3)
- Wave 5: Final advance toward $775-$800

Wave 3 is typically the longest and most powerful wave in an impulse sequence. If this count is correct, QQQ still has significant upside potential before the larger trend exhausts itself. Wave 3 often extends to 1.618 times the length of Wave 1, which projects to approximately $790.

## Risk Factors

Several factors could invalidate the bullish analysis:

- **Earnings disappointments:** If major tech holdings miss Q2 earnings expectations, the Nasdaq 100 could correct sharply
- **Fed policy shift:** Any hawkish surprise would hit growth stocks disproportionately
- **VIX spike:** A move above 25 would trigger systematic deleveraging in tech
- **Sector rotation:** A shift from growth to value would hurt the Nasdaq 100's heavy tech weighting
- **Geopolitical escalation:** Trade tensions or conflict would increase risk premiums

Conversely, upside catalysts include:
- **AI capex acceleration:** Increased enterprise AI spending would boost semiconductor and cloud names
- **Fed rate cuts:** Any dovish pivot would be disproportionately positive for growth stocks
- **Short covering:** If QQQ breaks $750, systematic strategies would add exposure

## Conclusion

The Nasdaq 100 maintains a bullish technical structure with positive momentum across most indicators. The golden cross on moving averages, bullish MACD, and alignment of Fibonacci support with the 50-day SMA all point toward a continuation of the uptrend. However, the 4-hour MACD divergence and narrowing Bollinger Bands suggest a near-term consolidation or pullback is likely before the next leg higher.

The risk/reward currently favors bulls for medium-term positions, with a target of $775-$790 if $748 resistance is broken. Short-term traders should watch for a pullback to $700-$710 as a higher-probability entry point. A daily close below $700 would be the first significant warning sign in two months.

Key levels: Support at $700 and $680. Resistance at $748 and $775. The trend remains bullish as long as price holds above the 200-day SMA at $632.
