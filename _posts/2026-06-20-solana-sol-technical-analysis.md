# Solana SOL Technical Analysis — June 20, 2026

## Current Price Action

Solana (SOL) is trading at approximately $68.47 as of June 20, 2026, following a notable recovery from recent lows near the $60 support zone. The cryptocurrency has rebounded more than 13% from the June 6 low of $60.13, demonstrating that buyers are stepping in at psychologically important price levels. Over the past 24 hours, SOL has shown modest intraday volatility, with price action oscillating between $67.50 and $70.00. The broader crypto market is experiencing mixed sentiment as macroeconomic headwinds from hawkish Federal Reserve signals continue to weigh on risk assets, but SOL has managed to hold its ground better than many altcoins during this period.

The weekly candlestick is currently forming with a lower wick near $63, suggesting that selling pressure is diminishing at current levels. However, the daily timeframe shows a sequence of lower highs since early June, with each rally attempt facing resistance at progressively lower levels. The most recent daily high of $74.12 on June 16 was followed by a rejection, and subsequent highs of $73.87 and $72.45 confirm this bearish structure. A break above the $74 resistance would be the first signal that this pattern is reversing.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) on the daily timeframe currently reads approximately 40, recovering from oversold conditions near 28 that were recorded during the June 6 selloff. This improvement is encouraging but does not yet confirm a bullish reversal. On the 4-hour chart, RSI has climbed to 46, still below the neutral 50 level, indicating that momentum remains slightly bearish in the short term. The weekly RSI is around 33, which is notable because it suggests only modest downside momentum despite eight consecutive months of losses. This divergence — price making lower lows while RSI refuses to dive deeper into oversold territory — is a potential early warning sign of bearish exhaustion.

Traders should watch for RSI to reclaim the 50 level on the daily chart as the first confirmation of shifting momentum. A move above 50 would signal that buying pressure is beginning to outweigh selling pressure at current prices. Conversely, if RSI rolls over from current levels and falls back toward 30, it would suggest that the recent bounce was merely a dead cat bounce within a continuing downtrend.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) on the daily chart shows the MACD line currently below the signal line, confirming that bearish momentum persists in the short term. However, the histogram bars have been contracting over the past three trading sessions, suggesting that selling pressure is gradually diminishing. This convergence pattern is often a precursor to a bullish crossover, though traders should wait for confirmation before acting on the signal.

On the weekly chart, the MACD is more concerning. The MACD line is testing its signal line from below, and the histogram bars are contracting but remain in positive territory. The weekly MACD has been in a gradual decline since September 2025, when SOL was trading near $253. This long-term bearish alignment is a significant headwind that any short-term recovery must overcome. A bullish weekly MACD crossover would be a powerful signal, but current conditions suggest this is still weeks away at best.

The 12-period EMA (exponential moving average) continues to trade below the 26-period EMA on the daily chart, a classic bearish configuration. For the MACD to generate a reliable buy signal, traders need to see the MACD line cross above the signal line with rising histogram bars and expanding volume.

## Moving Averages

Solana's moving average structure tells a compelling story of a market in transition. The 20-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) is currently near $71.50, with SOL trading below this level at $68.47. The 50-day SMA resides near $78, while the 100-day SMA is positioned around $85 and the 200-day SMA near $92. This descending alignment — shorter-term averages below longer-term averages — confirms that the overall trend remains bearish.

However, there are signs that this structure may be bottoming. The 20-day SMA has begun to flatten after a prolonged period of decline, suggesting that the rate of price depreciation is slowing. If SOL can reclaim the 20-day SMA at $71.50, the next test would be the 50-day SMA at $78, which coincides almost perfectly with the identified resistance zone. A move above both the 20-day and 50-day SMAs would significantly improve the technical picture and could attract trend-following buyers.

On the exponential moving average front, the 12-EMA is at $69.20 and the 26-EMA is at $71.80. The narrow gap between these two averages suggests that the market is compressing and preparing for a directional move. The 200-EMA on the daily chart, near $88, represents the final bullish target should a sustained uptrend develop.

## Volume Analysis

Volume analysis reveals a nuanced picture. During the selloff from $74 to $60 in early June, volume was elevated, indicating strong selling participation. However, the recovery from $60 back to $68 has been accompanied by declining volume, which is typical for corrective bounces within a downtrend. A sustained bullish move would need to see volume expand as price approaches resistance.

The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator has been trending sideways since the June 6 low, neither confirming nor rejecting the recent price recovery. This neutrality suggests that institutional accumulation is not yet occurring at meaningful levels. The Volume Price Trend (VPT) indicator tells a similar story, with a slight uptick that does not yet confirm a trend reversal.

One positive development is that volume during the past three days of consolidation has been significantly lower than during the selloff. This declining volume during price stabilization is often interpreted as selling exhaustion, a necessary precursor to any sustained recovery. Traders should watch for a volume spike on a break above $72, which would confirm genuine buying interest rather than short covering.

## Support and Resistance Levels

Identifying the key support and resistance levels is critical for understanding SOL's current position. On the support side, the most immediate level is $68, which has been tested multiple times over the past week and has held as a intraday floor. Below that, the $65 level represents psychological support that aligns with the June 10 consolidation zone. The major support zone lies between $60 and $63, anchored by the June 6 low of $60.13 and the liquidity cluster visible on the volume profile. A break below $60 would be severely bearish, opening the path toward the Fair-Value Gap between $46.90 and $51.12 identified on the weekly chart.

On the resistance side, the first hurdle is $72-$74, where multiple daily highs have been rejected in recent weeks. The critical resistance zone is $77-$78, which represents the most significant barrier to a resumed uptrend. This zone was previously support in early 2026 before breaking down, and it has now become resistance in a classic support-resistance flip. Above $78, the next resistance levels are $83-$85, corresponding to the overhead trendline of the descending wedge pattern on the weekly chart, followed by $90 and the psychological $100 level.

## Chart Patterns

The most significant chart pattern on Solana's weekly timeframe is a descending wedge, characterized by converging trendlines connecting lower highs and lower lows. This pattern is typically considered bullish in technical analysis, as it suggests that selling pressure is diminishing and a breakout is likely to the upside. The wedge has been forming since the September 2025 peak near $253 and is now approaching its apex, suggesting that a resolution is imminent.

On the daily chart, SOL is forming a potential double bottom pattern with lows at $60.13 on June 6 and approximately $62 on June 10. If this pattern is confirmed with a break above the neckline near $74, the measured move target would be approximately $88, calculated by adding the pattern height ($74 - $60 = $14) to the breakout level. However, traders should be cautious — double bottoms are only confirmed after a decisive break above the neckline, which has not yet occurred.

There is also evidence of a potential bullish flag pattern on the 4-hour chart, with the flagpole representing the sharp recovery from $60 to $68 and the flag forming as a downward-sloping consolidation. A break above the upper trendline of this flag near $70 would target a move toward $78.

Bollinger Bands on the daily chart are showing notable compression. The upper band is currently near $82, the middle band (20-day SMA) at $71.50, and the lower band near $61. SOL is trading closer to the lower band than the upper, which is consistent with the bearish trend. However, the bands are beginning to narrow after a period of expansion during the June selloff, suggesting that volatility is contracting. Periods of low volatility are often followed by significant directional moves.

In a typical trend reversal scenario, traders look for price to touch or break above the middle band, followed by a move toward the upper band. A daily close above $71.50 would put SOL above the middle Bollinger Band for the first time since early June, a technical improvement worth monitoring.

## Ichimoku Cloud Analysis

The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart presents a predominantly bearish picture. Price is trading well below the cloud, confirming that the medium-term trend remains bearish. The Conversion Line (Tenkan-sen) at $69 is below the Base Line (Kijun-sen) at $74, a bearish signal. The cloud itself is red (bearish) and extends forward, with the Senkou Span A at approximately $80 and Senkou Span B at $86, providing overhead resistance.

On a more positive note, the Lagging Span (Chikou Span) is beginning to flatten after a period of decline, suggesting that the rate of bearish momentum is slowing. A move above the cloud would require SOL to reclaim the $80-$86 zone, which aligns with the resistance levels identified through other indicators.

The weekly Ichimoku Cloud is even more bearish, with price deeply below the cloud and the cloud projected to remain red for several more months. This long-term structure reinforces that any recovery in the near term should be viewed as a counter-trend rally within a larger downtrend rather than the beginning of a new bull market.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

From an Elliott Wave perspective, SOL appears to be completing a five-wave downward impulse structure that began from the September 2025 high. Wave 1 declined from $253 to approximately $150, Wave 2 rallied to $200, Wave 3 extended lower to $100, Wave 4 bounced to $140, and Wave 5 is currently underway with a target in the $45-$55 zone.

Under this interpretation, the recent bounce from $60 to $74 represents either a wave 4 correction within a larger downtrend or the beginning of a new bullish impulse. The distinction is critical for trading decisions. If this is a wave 4, the subsequent wave 5 decline could take SOL below $60 toward the $45-$55 target zone identified by the weekly Fair-Value Gap. If this is the start of a new impulse, the first five-wave structure would need to break above the previous corrective high near $100 to confirm the trend change.

An alternative Elliott Wave count suggests that the move from $253 to $60 represents a completed ABC correction within a larger bullish supercycle. Under this interpretation, SOL is in the early stages of a new uptrend. However, this count is less likely given the absence of clear impulsive structures on the weekly chart.
