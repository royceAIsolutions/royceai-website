## Current Price Action

NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) closed at $210.69 on June 19, 2026, posting a 2.95% gain for the session. The stock has been trading in a range between $200 and $215 over the past two weeks, attempting to build momentum after a broad market recovery driven by improving U.S.-Iran relations and a softer interest rate outlook. Despite the positive session, the rally came on below-average volume, raising questions about the sustainability of the move. NVDA's 52-week range spans $165.17 on the low end to $235.74 at the high, placing current price action near the middle of that band. The recent $25 billion senior notes offering by the company has injected fresh capital into operations, providing a fundamental tailwind that traders are watching closely for follow-through.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-day Relative Strength Index (RSI) for NVDA currently sits at 58.4, positioning it in neutral-to-slightly-bullish territory. This reading indicates that the stock has room to run before reaching overbought conditions above 70. The weekly RSI reads 52.1, suggesting a gradual recovery from the oversold levels seen in mid-May when NVDA dipped below $180. On the hourly chart, RSI has been oscillating between 45 and 62, creating a series of higher lows that supports a short-term bullish bias. A move above 62 on the hourly RSI would confirm increasing buying pressure, while a break below 45 could signal that the recent rally is losing steam. Divergence analysis shows no bearish divergence on the daily timeframe, which is encouraging for the bull case.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) indicator on the daily chart shows a bullish crossover that occurred on June 10, 2026. The MACD line (12-day EMA minus 26-day EMA) has moved above the signal line, and the histogram bars have been printing positive values for seven consecutive sessions. The MACD line currently sits at 2.34 versus the signal line at 1.87, with the histogram at 0.47 and expanding. This configuration suggests that upside momentum is building, though it remains modest relative to the strong moves seen in early 2026. The weekly MACD is still below its signal line, indicating that the longer-term trend has not yet fully turned bullish. Traders should watch for the weekly MACD to cross above its signal line as confirmation of a larger trend reversal.

## Moving Averages

NVDA's moving average picture is mixed but improving. The 20-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at $204.12 has been reclaimed, with price trading 3.2% above this level — a constructive short-term signal. The 50-day SMA sits at $196.78, providing a solid support zone that held during the mid-June dip. However, the 50-day SMA has not yet crossed above the 200-day SMA — a golden cross formation that would provide strong bullish confirmation. The gap between the 50-day and 200-day SMAs currently stands at $9.46 and is narrowing.

## Volume Analysis

Volume has been a critical concern for NVDA bulls. Monday's session saw 128 million shares traded, which is below the 20-day average of 158 million. The June 19 rally, while positive, came on only 112 million shares — 29% below average volume. This divergence between price and volume suggests that institutional participation is lacking, making the rally more susceptible to profit-taking. On-Balance Volume (OBV) has been flat over the past two weeks, confirming that buying pressure has not increased proportionally with price. The Volume Price Trend (VPT) indicator shows a mild uptick but remains below its 20-day moving average. For the rally to sustain, volume needs to pick up to at least the 150 million share level, ideally with a high-volume day pushing above $215. Until then, caution is warranted despite the positive price action.

## Support and Resistance Levels

NVDA is trading within a well-defined range bounded by several key levels. On the support side, the first major level sits at $204.12 (20-day SMA), which has provided intraday support on three separate occasions over the past week. Below that, $196.78 (50-day SMA) represents a stronger support zone that held during the June 12 selloff. The $190 psychological level provides a third layer of defense, backed by the 200-day SMA at $187.32. A break below $187 would invalidate the long-term bullish structure.

On the resistance side, the immediate hurdle is $210.69 — the June 19 close that also sits near the 100-day SMA. A confirmed break above $210 opens the path to $215, which represents a prior swing high from early June. The next major resistance level is $220, an area that has capped rallies since April. Above that, $235.74 (52-week high) is the ultimate resistance target. The concentration of resistance between $210 and $220 makes this a critical zone — a failure to break through could lead to a retest of support at $200.

## Chart Patterns

NVDA is forming a bull flag pattern on the daily chart, defined by the sharp recovery from the May lows followed by a gradual downward-sloping consolidation channel. The flagpole measured approximately $45 from the May low of $170.27 to the June 4 high of $215.50. The flag itself has been forming over the past two weeks, characterized by lower highs and higher lows — a textbook pennant-like consolidation. The measured move target for a bull flag breakout is calculated by adding the flagpole height to the breakout point, which would project a target of approximately $255. However, the pattern requires a confirmed breakout above $215 on strong volume to remain valid. If NVDA breaks below $195, the bull flag would be invalidated, potentially triggering a move down to $180.

## Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart provides a cautiously bullish outlook. Price has moved above the cloud (senkou span A at $201.34 and senkou span B at $196.89), a key bullish signal. The conversion line (tenkan-sen) at $207.45 is above the base line (kijun-sen) at $203.12, confirming short-term bullish momentum. However, the cloud itself is flat rather than rising, suggesting the trend lacks conviction. The lagging span (chikou span) is above price from 26 periods ago, which is bullish. The future cloud (senkou span A and B) shows a thin red zone for the next 10-15 sessions, indicating potential resistance overhead but no strong bearish signal. A break above $215 would shift the future cloud to green, significantly improving the medium-term outlook.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

An Elliott Wave interpretation of NVDA's price action suggests the stock completed a corrective wave (Wave 2) in mid-May at the $170.27 low and is now in the early stages of Wave 3 of a larger impulse. Wave 1 ran from the January low of $155.20 to the March high of $235.74. Wave 2 retraced 61.8% of Wave 1, reaching $170.27 — a textbook retracement for a deep second wave. Wave 3, typically the longest and strongest wave, projects to between $260 and $290 based on standard Fibonacci extensions. The current price action between $200 and $215 represents Wave 1 of the sub-wave (i) of Wave 3. A clean break above $215 on strong volume would confirm that Wave (iii) of Wave 3 is underway, which could drive NVDA toward $235 within the next 3-4 weeks. The alternate count is that price action is still forming a complex correction (Wave 2 as a flat or triangle), which would keep NVDA range-bound between $190 and $220 for several more weeks.

## Trading Recommendations

For short-term traders, a breakout above $215 on volume above 150 million shares presents a long entry opportunity with a target of $225-$230 and a stop-loss at $204. For swing traders, a pullback to the $200-$204 support zone offers a better risk/reward entry, with a target of $230 and a stop at $192. For long-term investors, the current price below the 52-week high provides a reasonable entry point for building a position, particularly given NVIDIA's dominant position in the AI chip market. Bullish scenario: NVDA breaks and holds above $215, triggering the bull flag measured move target of $235+ within 4-6 weeks. Bearish scenario: NVDA fails at resistance and breaks below $195, triggering a retest of the $180-$185 support zone. The most likely scenario is a continued grind higher within the $200-$220 range for 1-2 more weeks, followed by a decisive breakout.

## Risk Factors

Key risks for NVDA in the near term include: (1) The $25 billion senior notes sale increases the company's debt burden, potentially weighing on valuation multiples. (2) Below-average volume suggests the rally lacks institutional sponsorship, making it vulnerable to profit-taking. (3) The Federal Reserve's next rate decision could shift risk sentiment, particularly if inflation data comes in hot. (4) Competition from AMD and custom AI chip makers continues to intensify. (5) The stock remains sensitive to export restrictions and geopolitical tensions, particularly regarding China. (6) A broader market correction would likely impact NVDA disproportionately given its high beta of 1.65.

## Conclusion

NVDA is at a critical technical juncture. The bull flag pattern, favorable MACD crossover, and solid support from moving averages provide a constructive case for higher prices. However, the lack of volume and the concentration of resistance between $210 and $220 create a challenging environment for a breakout. The Elliott Wave structure supports the bull case, suggesting that the stock is in an early impulsive wave that should ultimately move higher. Traders should watch for a confirmed volume-backed break above $215 as the trigger for bullish positioning, while maintaining discipline with stop-losses below $195. The overall bias is cautiously bullish, with the caveat that weak volume prevents a more aggressive stance.
