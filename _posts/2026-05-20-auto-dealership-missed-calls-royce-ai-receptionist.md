## Why Human Receptionists Can’t Scale
Traditional dealership receptionists are stretched thin even on slow days. A single receptionist can only handle one call at a time, meaning peak periods like Saturday mornings, end-of-month sales pushes, or new model launch weekends leave dozens of callers waiting or hanging up. Staffing shortages in the auto industry have made this problem worse, with many dealerships struggling to hire and retain front-desk staff who can handle high call volumes and demanding customers.

Human receptionists also can’t cover after-hours calls, weekend inquiries, or holiday rushes without paying overtime or hiring additional shift staff. For dealerships in competitive markets, being unavailable when a potential buyer calls on a Sunday afternoon or at 7 PM on a weeknight means losing that lead to a competitor with 24/7 coverage.

Language barriers add another layer of complexity. With growing Hispanic and Korean populations across the US, dealerships that can’t communicate with callers in Spanish or Korean are missing out on a massive segment of the market. Most human receptionists only speak English, leaving non-English speaking callers frustrated and likely to take their business elsewhere.
