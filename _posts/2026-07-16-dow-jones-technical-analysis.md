# Dow Jones Technical Analysis - July 16, 2026

## Current Price Action

The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed at 52,658.64 on July 15, 2026, gaining 150.34 points or 0.29 percent from the previous close of 52,508.27. The daily range spanned from a low of 52,428.54 to a high of 52,823.95, indicating a moderately volatile session driven by positive economic data and technology sector strength. Volume on the NYSE remained above the 20-day average, suggesting genuine buying interest rather than low-volume price drift. The index has been in a steady uptrend since the May lows, recovering from the pullback that tested the 50,800 level in late spring.

The DJIA is now trading approximately 1.28 percent above its 200-day moving average of 51,991, confirming the long-term bullish trend remains intact. Price action over the past month shows higher lows and higher highs, a textbook continuation pattern that suggests the next leg higher is building. The index has gained 5.2 percent year-to-date and 8.1 percent from the June lows. With the second quarter earnings season underway, corporate results will determine whether the Dow can sustain its upward trajectory through the summer months.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-day Relative Strength Index stands at 55.90, placing the Dow comfortably in neutral-bullish territory. This reading tells an important story: the index has room to run before approaching overbought conditions at the 70 level. In a strong uptrend, the RSI can stay in the 50 to 70 range for extended periods, and the current reading suggests momentum is building without the exhaustion signal that typically precedes a pullback.

Looking at multiple timeframes adds useful context. The weekly RSI reads 58.2, confirming the intermediate trend is bullish but not overheated. The daily RSI has been oscillating between 50 and 60 since mid-June, which is typical of a trending market that is consolidating before its next leg higher. A move above 60 on the daily RSI would signal that buying pressure is accelerating, potentially triggering a breakout above 53,000. Conversely, a drop below 50 would suggest the consolidation is deepening and could test the lower support levels.

## MACD Analysis

The MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) reading is 33.41, with the signal line also trending upward and both lines above the zero line. This configuration is textbook bullish: the MACD line above the signal line indicates that short-term momentum exceeds long-term momentum, and both lines above zero confirm the trend is positive in absolute terms. The histogram shows expanding positive bars, indicating that momentum is accelerating rather than fading.

The weekly MACD confirms the bullish picture with the MACD line at 67.82 and the signal line rising. The weekly histogram has been printing positive bars since the May turnaround, adding weight to the argument that the current uptrend has structural support. A potential divergence to watch is if price makes a new high while the MACD histogram prints a lower high, which would signal weakening momentum. As of July 16 no such divergence is present.

## Moving Averages

The moving average landscape is strongly bullish. The 20-day SMA sits at 52,285, the 50-day SMA at 52,040, the 100-day SMA at 51,840, and the 200-day SMA at 51,991. All four short-term moving averages are stacked in ascending order with the current price above all of them, meeting the textbook definition of a bullish alignment. The 50-day moving average crossed above the 200-day moving average in early June, a golden cross that historically precedes sustained upward moves.

The 20-day SMA at 52,285 provides the nearest support level. A pullback to this level would represent only a 0.7 percent decline from current prices, suggesting shallow dips are likely to be bought. The proximity of the 50-day and 200-day SMAs at 52,040 and 51,991 creates a support cluster that should provide a strong floor for any deeper retracement. The EMA 12 at 52,395 and EMA 26 at 52,263 are also in bullish alignment with the EMA 12 above the EMA 26, confirming positive short-term momentum.

## Volume Analysis

Volume patterns support the bullish thesis. Daily volume on the July 15 session was approximately 350 million shares on the NYSE composite, above the 30-day average of 310 million. Elevated volume on up days relative to down days is a hallmark of institutional accumulation. Since the June lows, the Dow has posted 14 up days with above-average volume versus only 6 down days with above-average volume, a ratio of 2.33 to 1 that confirms the buying is deliberate and broad-based.

The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator is trending upward and has made a new high above its June peak. OBV measures cumulative volume flow and confirms price trends when it moves in the same direction as price. The current OBV alignment with price indicates that volume is supporting the uptrend and that the buying is not a short-covering rally but genuine accumulation. Volume Price Trend (VPT) also shows a positive divergence, with VPT rising faster than price, suggesting strong underlying demand.

## Support and Resistance Levels

Key support levels are identified at 52,400, 52,000, and 51,500. The first support at 52,400 corresponds to the July 15 intraday low and the upper boundary of the early July consolidation range. This level has been tested three times in the past two weeks and held each time, establishing it as near-term support. The next support at 52,000 is a psychological round number reinforced by the 50-day SMA at 52,040 and the 200-day SMA at 51,991. This confluence of technical factors makes 52,000 a significant support zone.

On the resistance side, 52,800 represents the recent intraday high from July 15. A clean break above this level would target 53,000, which is the next major psychological resistance. Above 53,000, the next resistance is 53,500, the May highs that preceded the June correction. Beyond that, 54,000 is the 2026 year-to-date high and the ultimate bull target for the current move. A weekly close above 53,000 would confirm the breakout and open the path toward 54,000.

## Chart Patterns

The daily chart reveals a bullish flag pattern that has been forming since late June. The flagpole represents the sharp rally from 50,800 to 52,800 in the second half of June, a move of 2,000 points. Since then, the index has been consolidating in a tight range between 52,200 and 52,800, forming the flag portion of the pattern. Bullish flags typically resolve upward with a move equal to the flagpole, suggesting a target of approximately 54,200 once the breakout above 52,800 is confirmed.

The weekly chart shows a rounded bottom formation that began in April 2026. The rounded bottom is a long-term reversal pattern that indicates a gradual shift from selling pressure to buying pressure. The pattern spans approximately 14 weeks and is approaching its breakout point at 53,000. A weekly close above this level would complete the rounded bottom and project a measured move target of 56,000 based on the depth of the pattern.

## Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands on the daily timeframe show the middle band at 52,285 (the 20-day SMA) with the upper band at 53,160 and the lower band at 51,410. The current price at 52,658 sits in the upper half of the bands, indicating bullish momentum without being stretched. The band width is moderate, suggesting normal volatility rather than an impending expansion or contraction pattern.

Band width has been contracting over the past two weeks, which typically precedes a significant directional move. Bollinger Band squeezes, defined as band width at its narrowest level in six months, are precursors to increased volatility. The current band width is 56 percent of its 90-day average, placing it in squeeze territory. When the squeeze resolves, the direction of the initial breakout tends to be the dominant trend. Given the bullish alignment of the moving averages and RSI, an upward resolution is the higher-probability scenario.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count suggests the Dow is in Wave 3 of a five-wave advance that began at the May low near 50,800. Wave 1 ran from 50,800 to 52,200, a distance of 1,400 points. Wave 2 retraced to 51,200, a 71 percent retracement of Wave 1, which is deeper than typical but still within the acceptable range for a zigzag correction. Wave 3, which began in late June, has already traveled from 51,200 to 52,658, a distance of 1,458 points.

Wave 3 typically extends to 1.618 times the length of Wave 1, which would project a Wave 3 target of approximately 53,465. If Wave 3 completes near that level, Wave 4 would likely retrace 38.2 percent of Wave 3 to approximately 52,600 before Wave 5 extends to new highs above 53,500. The alternate count suggests Wave 3 is still in its early stages and could extend to 2.618 times Wave 1, targeting 54,860. This count would be validated by a weekly close above 52,800.

## Sector Breadth

Breadth analysis shows that 15 of the 30 Dow components are trading above their 50-day moving averages, while 12 are above their 200-day moving averages. The sectors leading the advance include financials, industrials, and technology, with UnitedHealth Group, Goldman Sachs, and Microsoft contributing the most to the recent gains. Defensive sectors such as utilities and consumer staples are lagging, which is consistent with a risk-on market environment rather than a defensive rotation.

The Dow Transports index, a bellwether for economic activity, is also trending higher and trading above its 200-day moving average. The Dow Theory principle states that the Industrial and Transportation averages must confirm each other for a sustainable trend, and both are currently aligned in an uptrend. This confirmation adds weight to the bullish thesis and suggests the rally has broad economic support rather than being driven by a narrow set of names.

## Risk Factors

The primary risk to the bullish outlook is the earnings season currently underway. Below-expectations results from major Dow components could trigger profit-taking after the recent run-up. The Federal Reserve's policy stance remains a wild card, with any hawkish surprise on interest rates potentially strengthening the dollar and weighing on equities. Geopolitical risks including trade tensions and global economic slowdown concerns could also cap upside.

The VIX at approximately 14.5 indicates complacency in the options market, which historically tends to precede periods of increased volatility. A sudden VIX spike above 20 would signal a shift in market sentiment and could trigger a broad sell-off. However, low VIX readings in the context of a confirmed uptrend are more consistent with a healthy bull market than an imminent top.
