## Why Manual Reminder Systems Fail
Most practices still use a combination of staff-made phone calls and generic text reminders sent 24 hours before an appointment. But these systems break down for three key reasons:

First, staffing shortages mean receptionists rarely have time to make personal calls to every patient. A 2026 survey from the American Medical Association (AMA) found 68% of practice managers say their front desk team can only reach 40% of patients scheduled for next-day appointments.

Second, language barriers exclude millions of patients. Over 13% of U.S. residents speak Spanish at home, and 1.8 million speak Korean as their primary language. Generic English-only reminders often go unread, leading to avoidable no-shows for non-English speaking patients.

Third, after-hours reminders don't reach patients who work during standard business hours. A patient who works 9-5 is unlikely to see a text sent at 2 PM until after their appointment has already passed.
