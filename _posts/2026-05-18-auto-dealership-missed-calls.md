## The Real Cost of Missed Calls
Missed calls aren’t just a nuisance—they’re a revenue leak. According to a 2026 report from Useflai, dealerships lose an average of $850K to $1.17M per year in service and sales revenue from unanswered calls alone. That’s not a rounding error; that’s a massive chunk of potential profit walking out the door.

The problem is most acute during peak shopping times: evenings after 6 PM, weekends, and holidays. These are when working professionals have time to research vehicles and call dealerships, but when human receptionists are off the clock. Invoca’s 2023 industry benchmark (still widely cited in 2026) found that dealerships miss 15-20% of all incoming calls during these off-peak windows, with no follow-up in 30% of those cases.

Even during business hours, high call volume and understaffed BDC teams lead to dropped calls and long hold times. Shoppers today have zero patience for voicemail: 78% of consumers say they’ll hang up after 30 seconds on hold and call a competitor instead. For dealerships, that means every missed call is a lost opportunity to close a sale, book a service appointment, or build long-term customer loyalty.

## Why Human Receptionists Can’t Scale
Human receptionists and BDC staff are the backbone of most dealerships, but they have inherent limitations that make it impossible to capture every call. First and foremost: they can’t work 24/7. Even with rotating shifts, most dealerships close by 8 PM and are dark on Sundays, leaving after-hours leads completely unaddressed.

Turnover is another major issue. The average BDC turnover rate in the auto industry is 45% annually, according to the National Automobile Dealers Association (NADA). Every time a receptionist leaves, you lose institutional knowledge, and new hires take weeks to get up to speed on inventory, promotions, and lead routing processes. Inconsistent messaging across staff also leads to confused shoppers and lost trust.

Humans also can’t handle multiple calls at once. During peak times—like when a new model launches or a big promotion runs—call volume can spike 3x normal levels. Even the best BDC team will drop calls or put shoppers on hold for minutes at a time, driving away high-intent leads.
