## Executive Summary

Gold (XAU/USD) opened the June 2 trading session at $4,525.49, recovering from Monday's 1.21 percent decline that pushed prices as low as $4,447.12. The precious metal currently trades near $4,530.60, up 0.91 percent on the session, as buyers stepped in to defend the psychologically important $4,500 handle. This technical analysis examines gold's current setup across multiple timeframes, identifying key support and resistance levels, momentum indicators, and actionable trading zones.

## Current Market Snapshot

As of the June 2 session, gold is trading at approximately $4,530.60 per troy ounce. Monday's session saw a significant intraday range from $4,447.12 to $4,546.44, closing near the low at $4,484.55. The current session has already touched an intraday high of $4,541.53 and a low of $4,463.24, suggesting continued intraday volatility as traders assess the tug-of-war between dollar strength and geopolitical risk premiums.

The weekly chart shows that gold posted a $175 gain (3.63 percent) during the last full week before giving back some of those gains on Monday. This price action has established well-defined support and resistance zones that are likely to govern near-term trading ranges.

## Relative Strength Index (RSI)

The 14-period daily RSI currently reads near 48, placing it in neutral territory after dipping from the overbought zone above 70 that prevailed in late January when gold was trading near $4,950. The move from overbought conditions back toward the midline suggests that selling pressure has subsided after the recent pullback, but buyers have not yet regained full control.

On the 4-hour timeframe, RSI reads approximately 42, briefly touching oversold territory near 35 during Monday's selloff before rebounding. This recovery from oversold conditions on the shorter timeframe often signals a potential bounce, and Tuesday's price action confirms that dynamic is playing out. A sustained move above 50 on the 4-hour RSI would indicate that short-term momentum has shifted back in favor of bulls.

The weekly RSI remains constructive at approximately 58, indicating that the broader uptrend is intact despite the recent corrective phase. Readings between 55 and 75 on the weekly chart have historically corresponded with sustained bullish trends in gold, and the current level does not suggest exhaustion.

## Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

The daily MACD histogram remains negative but is showing signs of compression. The MACD line (12-period EMA minus 26-period EMA) sits below the signal line, confirming the bearish crossover that occurred in mid-May. However, the narrowing of the histogram bars suggests that downside momentum is weakening.

On the weekly MACD, the picture is more constructive. The MACD line remains above the signal line, confirming that the secular uptrend has not reversed. The weekly histogram has flattened, indicating a loss of upside momentum rather than an outright bearish reversal. A bullish weekly MACD crossover would require gold to reclaim the $4,700 level with conviction.

The monthly MACD remains in bullish territory with both lines above the zero line and the MACD line above its signal line. This long-term configuration supports the thesis that the current selloff is corrective within a broader uptrend.

## Moving Averages

Gold continues to trade above its 50-day simple moving average (SMA), which currently sits near $4,420. This level has acted as reliable dynamic support throughout the 2026 rally. The 50-day SMA has provided nearly 15 support touches since gold crossed above it in November 2025, and each test has been bought aggressively.

The 200-day SMA resides near $3,950, well below the current price and sloping upward, confirming the long-term bullish trend. The distance between price and the 200-day SMA ($580 or approximately 13 percent) suggests the trend is mature but not yet extended to dangerous levels that historically precede major reversals.

The 21-day EMA (exponential moving average) is the most critical near-term indicator. Gold closed below the 21-day EMA on May 28 for the first time since the rally began in April. A failure to reclaim this level (currently near $4,580) within five trading sessions would signal a shift toward consolidation. Tuesday's push toward $4,541 suggests gold is testing this resistance.

The 100-day SMA at $4,250 serves as secondary support beneath the 50-day SMA, representing a zone where institutional buyers have historically added to long positions.

## Fibonacci Retracement Levels

Using the major rally from the October 2025 low near $3,600 to the January 2026 all-time high at $4,990, the following Fibonacci retracement levels are the most relevant:

- 23.6 percent retracement: $4,662 — gold has already broken below this level
- 38.2 percent retracement: $4,460 — this level was tested intraday on Monday at $4,447 and held
- 50.0 percent retracement: $4,295 — major support zone if the pullback deepens
- 61.8 percent retracement: $4,131 — the "golden ratio" level typically marks maximum corrective depth in healthy trends

The 38.2 percent Fibonacci level at $4,460 combined with the $4,500 psychological round number creates a strong support cluster. Monday's low at $4,447.12 represents a clean test of this zone, and Tuesday's bounce confirms its importance.

From a shorter-term perspective, measuring the rally from the May low near $4,402 to the weekly high near $4,577 gives these Fibonacci retracement levels for the current micro-move:

- 38.2 percent: $4,510
- 50.0 percent: $4,490
- 61.8 percent: $4,469

Gold is currently trading above all three of these near-term retracement levels, suggesting that the intraday bounce has legs.

## Ichimoku Cloud Analysis

The daily Ichimoku cloud (Kumo) provides a nuanced picture. Price is currently trading within the cloud, having fallen below it on May 28 but not cleanly through. The cloud remains thick and positively sloped, which historically acts as a strong support zone rather than resistance.

The Tenkan-sen (conversion line) at $4,560 sits above the Kijun-sen (base line) at $4,520, suggesting short-term bullish alignment. The fact that the Kijun-sen is holding near $4,520 provides an additional support reference for the current session.

The Chikou Span (lagging span) is flat, indicating that the current price action is consistent with price action from 26 periods ago. A rising Chikou Span crossing above price would confirm bullish momentum.

The weekly Ichimoku cloud remains decisively bullish with price above the cloud, the cloud positively sloped, and the Tenkan-sen above the Kijun-sen. This long-term configuration reinforces the interpretation that the May-June pullback is corrective, not reversal.

## Bollinger Bands

The daily Bollinger Bands show a broadening pattern, indicating increased volatility. The upper band resides near $4,820, the middle band (20-day SMA) near $4,560, and the lower band near $4,300. The weekly Bollinger Bands show a squeeze that resolved in April when bandwidth expanded sharply. That expansion has moderated but remains above the compression threshold. The weekly middle band at $4,520 is being tested this week.

The Commodity Channel Index (CCI) on the daily timeframe reads near minus 100, which is the oversold threshold. Historically, gold has reversed within one to three sessions after CCI reaches or exceeds minus 100 during uptrends. The current reading supports a near-term bounce scenario.

The daily Stochastic Oscillator (%K at 18, %D at 22) is deep in oversold territory and showing a bullish crossover. This is one of the most reliable short-term buy signals in gold's recent trading history, having preceded bounces in November 2025, February 2026, and April 2026.

## Elliott Wave Perspective

From an Elliott Wave standpoint, gold appears to be in a fourth-wave corrective phase within a larger five-wave impulse structure that began from the October 2025 low near $3,600. Wave 1 ran from $3,600 to $4,200, Wave 2 pulled back to $3,950, and Wave 3 extended aggressively from $3,950 to the January high near $4,990.

The current decline from $4,990 to the recent low at $4,402 represents Wave 4, which measured approximately 38.2 percent of Wave 3's length. This is textbook Fibonacci for fourth-wave corrections. A Wave 5 rally toward $5,200 to $5,500 would be the expected resolution of this pattern, provided that $4,402 holds as the Wave 4 low.

The invalidation level for this count is a break below $4,200 (the Wave 1 peak), which would suggest a more complex corrective structure is in play.

Based on the confluence of technical indicators, gold is at a critical juncture. The $4,460 to $4,500 zone has held as support, and oversold oscillators suggest a bounce is likely. However, the failure to reclaim the 21-day EMA means the path of least resistance remains sideways to down until that level is recaptured.

**Bearish scenario (1 to 2 weeks):**
