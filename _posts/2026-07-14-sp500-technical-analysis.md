# SPX Technical Analysis — July 14, 2026

## Current Price Action

The S&P 500 index closed at 7,515.34 on July 13, down 60.05 points or 0.79% from the prior session. The index traded in a range of 7,506.41 to 7,565.37, with the 52-week range spanning 6,201.59 to 7,620.90. The index sits roughly 1.4% below its all-time high of 7,620.90 reached on June 1, 2026, reflecting a modest pullback from peak levels as mid-year uncertainty creeps into the market.

The daily chart shows the index forming a shallow descending channel since the June high, with lower highs and lower lows over the past six weeks. Price action remains above both the 50-day and 200-day moving averages, suggesting the broader uptrend is intact despite the near-term softness.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-day Relative Strength Index currently reads 54.46, placing the S&P 500 in neutral territory. This is down from overbought readings above 70 seen in late May, indicating that selling pressure has absorbed much of the earlier buying enthusiasm. The RSI has held above the 50 midpoint on every pullback since October 2025, which is a constructive sign for bulls. A move below 50 would be the first bearish signal in nine months and would warrant close attention. The weekly RSI sits near 58, still in bullish territory but declining from its May peak of 72.

## MACD Analysis

The MACD line (12,26,9) currently reads 3.55, with the signal line positioned at 2.80. The MACD histogram shows declining green bars, indicating that upward momentum is weakening. The MACD lines remain above the zero line, which confirms the intermediate-term trend as bullish. However, the narrowing spread between the MACD and signal lines raises the risk of a bearish crossover in the coming days if selling pressure persists. A cross below the signal line at current levels would be a short-term sell signal, potentially targeting the 7,400 area.

## Moving Averages

The 50-day simple moving average sits at 7,514.11, essentially flat with the current price. The 200-day SMA lies near 7,290, providing a substantial support level roughly 3% below the current price. The 20-day EMA is at 7,490, and the 100-day SMA is near 7,380. The alignment remains bullish with shorter-term averages above longer-term averages, though the 20-day EMA has flattened, indicating slowing momentum. The 5-day SMA of 7,528 has already crossed below the 10-day SMA of 7,540, a short-term bearish signal.

The moving average ribbon is still in positive alignment (bullish), but the compression between the 20-day and 50-day averages suggests a potential volatility expansion is coming. A break above 7,600 would re-establish the bullish posturing, while a close below 7,400 would threaten the entire uptrend structure.

## Volume Analysis

Trading volume has been below the 20-day average for the past four sessions, indicating that sellers lack conviction despite the declining price. The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator shows a slight downtrend since mid-June but remains well above levels seen in April. Volume has been declining on down days and picking up slightly on up days, which is a mixed signal. The Chaikin Money Flow (CMF) remains positive but declining, suggesting distribution is starting to outweigh accumulation. A sustained volume surge above the 30-day average on a green day would be the first strong bullish divergence signal. Conversely, a high-volume breakdown below 7,460 would confirm institutional selling.

## Support and Resistance Levels

Key support levels stand at 7,460 (June low), 7,380 (100-day SMA), 7,290 (200-day SMA), and 7,200 (psychological round number). The 7,460 level has been tested three times in the past two weeks and held each time, making it the most critical near-term support. A breakdown below 7,460 would likely trigger stop-losses and accelerate selling toward 7,380.

On the upside, resistance is at 7,565 (current week high), 7,620 (all-time high), and 7,700 (psychological extension). The 7,565 level has rejected price twice in the past week, establishing it as near-term resistance. A break above 7,565 with volume would target a retest of the all-time high at 7,620. The 7,700 level represents a 1.05% extension from the ATH and would likely require a significant catalyst to reach.

## Chart Patterns

The daily chart is forming a descending wedge pattern with converging trendlines since the June 1 high. Descending wedges are typically bullish reversal patterns, suggesting the current pullback may resolve to the upside. The wedge target, measured from the widest point, projects a move to approximately 7,700. For the pattern to remain valid, the index must not close below the lower trendline currently near 7,400. A break above the upper trendline near 7,570 would confirm the pattern.

The weekly chart shows a bull flag pattern, with the sharp rally from January through June representing the flagpole and the current consolidation representing the flag. Bull flags typically resolve in the direction of the prevailing trend, making this a constructive pattern for medium-term bulls.

## Fibonacci Retracement

Using the October 2025 low of 6,201 to the June 2026 high of 7,620, the 23.6% retracement sits at 7,284, nearly coincident with the 200-day SMA. The 38.2% retracement lies at 7,077. The index has already exceeded the shallow 23.6% level, suggesting strong underlying demand. The current pullback has found support right at the 14.6% level of 7,416, which is the first-level retracement in extended Fibonacci analysis. A deeper pullback to the 50% retracement at 6,910 would represent a significant trend change and is not the base case.

## Bollinger Bands

The 20-period Bollinger Bands are currently 145 points wide, which is moderately narrow compared to the 200-day average band width of 180 points. Narrow bands suggest a period of low volatility that historically precedes a significant directional move. The current price is trading near the middle of the bands, giving no directional bias from this indicator alone. A touch of the lower band near 7,365 would represent a potential oversold condition, while a move to the upper band near 7,655 would confirm renewed momentum. Band width contraction has been in progress since June 15.

## Ichimoku Cloud

The daily Ichimoku cloud is thin and green, indicating a mildly bullish trend. The price is trading above the cloud, which is a bullish signal. The Tenkan-sen (conversion line) at 7,490 has crossed below the Kijun-sen (base line) at 7,510, forming a bearish cross that suggests short-term weakness. The Chikou span (lagging span) is slightly below the price from 26 days ago, confirming the short-term bearishness. The cloud projects support at 7,350 for the next two weeks, meaning any pullback to that level would still be within the bullish framework. The Kumo twist is two weeks away and could flip the cloud structure if prices remain under pressure.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The count from the October 2025 low suggests we are in a Wave 4 correction of a larger-degree five-wave advance. Wave 1 ran from 6,201 to 6,800, Wave 2 pulled back to 6,500, and Wave 3 extended from 6,500 to 7,620. Wave 4 corrections typically retrace 23.6% to 38.2% of Wave 3, targeting a range of 7,355 to 7,284. This aligns well with the Fibonacci retracement levels and the 200-day SMA. If this count is correct, Wave 5 should push the index to new highs above 7,620 once the correction completes. The alternative count suggests we are in a Wave 2 of a terminal impulse, which would limit upside potential.

## Market Sentiment

The CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) is trading near 14.5, up from its June lows near 11 but still well below the long-term average of 19.5. The put/call ratio sits at 1.05, slightly elevated from the neutral 0.70 level, indicating modest protective positioning. The CNN Fear & Greed Index reads 51, squarely in neutral territory, suggesting no extreme sentiment that would indicate a market top or bottom. Institutional flow data from the Commitment of Traders report shows hedge funds reducing equity long exposure, while pension funds maintain their allocation.

## Trading Recommendations

For traders, the current setup favors a neutral-to-cautious approach. The descending wedge and Bullish Flag patterns provide upside targets of 7,620 to 7,700, but the weakening momentum indicators suggest timing is critical. A long position entered above 7,565 with a stop at 7,450 and a target of 7,620 offers a favorable 1:2 risk-reward ratio. For swing traders, waiting for a re-test of the 200-day SMA near 7,290 would provide a higher-probability entry with a stop at 7,100.

Short-term trades should respect the 7,460 support level. A daily close below 7,460 would invalidate the bullish wedge pattern and suggest a deeper correction toward 7,290. For risk-averse traders, staying in cash or hedged positions until the wedge resolves is the prudent approach.

## Risk Factors

The primary risk to the bullish thesis is a sharper-than-expected economic slowdown. Bank of America has warned that the S&P 500 could fall to 7,100 if speculation unwinds too quickly. The Federal Reserve's next rate decision and CPI data release are the key macro catalysts in the coming weeks. Additionally, sector rotation from technology to defensive sectors is accelerating, which typically precedes broader market weakness. The AI trade, which drove the majority of 2026 gains, is showing signs of exhaustion with semiconductor stocks pulling back from highs.

## Conclusion

The S&P 500 is in a well-defined pullback within a larger uptrend. The converging wedge pattern, combined with a bullish moving average structure and neutral sentiment indicators, suggests the path of least resistance remains higher. A break above 7,565 would confirm the resumption of the uptrend, while a close below 7,460 would require a reassessment. The medium-term outlook remains constructive, with the 200-day SMA at 7,290 providing a strong safety net. Patience and strict risk management are warranted until the wedge resolves.
