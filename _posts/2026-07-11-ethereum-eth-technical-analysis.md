## Current Price Action

Ethereum (ETH) is currently trading at approximately $1,799 as of July 11, 2026, with a 24-hour range between $1,793 and $1,805. The cryptocurrency has shown moderate positive momentum over the past week, gaining 5.34%, while the month-over-month performance shows a strong 9.20% increase. Despite this short-term recovery, ETH remains well below its 2026 highs near the $2,200 zone, suggesting the broader trend is still in a corrective phase.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-day Relative Strength Index for Ethereum currently sits at 57.87, placing it in neutral territory with a slight bullish bias. This reading indicates that ETH is neither overbought nor oversold, leaving ample room for movement in either direction. On the 4-hour chart, RSI oscillates between 45 and 55, reflecting the ongoing consolidation phase. A sustained break above 60 on the daily RSI would signal the start of a more aggressive bullish momentum shift.

## MACD Analysis

The MACD indicator on the daily timeframe shows the MACD line converging toward the signal line, with the histogram bars shrinking in magnitude. This pattern typically indicates weakening bearish momentum and suggests a potential bullish crossover in the coming sessions. The weekly MACD remains negative but is also flattening, supporting the view that the selling pressure that dominated Q2 2026 is gradually subsiding.

## Moving Averages

Ethereum is currently trading below both the 50-day SMA ($1,920) and the 100-day SMA ($2,050), confirming that the intermediate-term trend remains bearish. However, price has reclaimed the 20-day SMA ($1,740), a positive near-term signal. The 200-day SMA sits near $2,150, representing the major overhead resistance that bulls must overcome to confirm a trend reversal.

The EMA structure tells a similar story: the 12 EMA at $1,770 has crossed above the 26 EMA at $1,755, a short-term bullish signal. However, both remain well below the 50 EMA ($1,890), keeping the overall picture mixed.

## Volume Analysis

Daily trading volume for ETH has been declining over the past two weeks, a pattern typically associated with consolidation rather than accumulation or distribution. The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator has flattened, suggesting neither strong nor aggressive buying nor selling pressure dominates. Volume-Price Trend (VPT) shows a slight positive divergence, as price has held support while volume stabilizes.

Ethereum's open interest across derivatives exchanges currently stands at $49.3 billion, with a 7-day change of -0.21% — a modest decline that suggests some speculative positions have been closed. The 30-day OI change of +3.41% indicates that the broader trend in open interest remains positive, with traders slowly returning to ETH derivatives after the June volatility.

## Support and Resistance

Key support levels for Ethereum are concentrated at $1,718 (the June consolidation low), followed by $1,600 (a psychological and technical level tested in May), and the major support at $1,555 (the 2026 low from late June). Below that, the next significant floor sits at $1,400.

On the upside, immediate resistance stands at $1,801-$1,805 (the current daily range high). A clean break above this level targets $1,900 (the July price target), followed by $2,000 (psychological resistance). The zone between $2,000 and $2,200 represents the most significant overhead supply, containing the 200-day SMA and the 2026 high.

## Chart Patterns

ETH is currently forming a descending wedge pattern on the daily chart, with lower highs and flat lows converging toward the $1,718 support level. This pattern is traditionally considered bullish, as it often resolves with an upward breakout. The measured move target of a wedge breakout from current levels would project toward $2,100-$2,200.

A descending broadening wedge is also visible on the weekly chart, suggesting a longer-term bottoming process may be underway.

## Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the decline from the 2026 high near $2,200 to the June low near $1,555:

- The 23.6% retracement at $1,707 has already been reclaimed.
- The 38.2% retracement at $1,802 is being tested as resistance — today's price action at $1,799 aligns with this level.
- The 50% retracement at $1,878 is the next target.
- The 61.8% retracement at $1,955 is the key level that would confirm a sustained recovery.

The current price action at the 38.2% Fibonacci level makes this a critical decision point for ETH.

## Bollinger Bands

The daily Bollinger Bands show moderate width, indicating average volatility. Price is currently trading near the middle band ($1,785), suggesting no directional bias. The upper band sits at $1,980 and the lower band at $1,590. The bandwidth has been contracting over the past week, consistent with the consolidation phase and often a precursor to an expansion move.

## Ichimoku Cloud

ETH is trading within the Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart, a sign of indecision. The Conversion Line (Tenkan-sen) at $1,770 has crossed above the Base Line (Kijun-sen) at $1,745, a bullish signal known as a TK Cross. The cloud for the next 26 periods spans from $1,690 to $1,850, suggesting that a move above $1,850 would confirm bullish momentum. The Lagging Span (Chikou Span) is flat, offering no additional signal.

## Elliott Wave

From an Elliott Wave perspective, Ethereum appears to be completing the C-wave of an ABC corrective structure. The A-wave ran from $2,200 to $1,800, the B-wave bounced to $2,000, and the C-wave extended to the $1,555 low. If this wave count is correct, ETH is now in the early stages of a new impulse wave to the upside, with Wave 1 targeting $1,900 and Wave 3 potentially reaching $2,200.

The alternative count suggests the correction may extend into a more complex WXY structure, which would mean further consolidation between $1,550 and $1,900 before the uptrend resumes.

## Market Sentiment

The Crypto Fear and Greed Index currently reads 25 — still in Fear territory. While this is up from the extreme fear levels seen in June, it indicates that market participants remain cautious. ETH funding rates are slightly positive at 0.0034%, suggesting that longs and shorts are relatively balanced with a mild tilt toward bullish positioning.

The ETH/BTC ratio remains weak, indicating that Bitcoin is currently outperforming Ethereum — a common pattern during risk-off periods in crypto markets.

## Trading Recommendations

Bullish Scenario: A clean break above $1,805 with volume could target $1,900 in the near term. A weekly close above $1,900 would open the path toward $2,000-$2,200. Entry near $1,720-$1,740 with a stop at $1,550 provides a favorable risk-reward ratio.

Bearish Scenario: Failure to hold $1,718 support would likely trigger a retest of the $1,600-$1,555 range. A break below $1,555 would invalidate the bullish wedge pattern and open the door to $1,400. The bearish case is supported by declining OI and the Fear sentiment reading.

Neutral Scenario: Continued consolidation between $1,718 and $1,805 is the most probable near-term outcome, with a resolution likely within 1-2 weeks as Bollinger Bands continue contracting and the wedge pattern reaches its apex.

## Liquidity and Order Book Analysis

Order book data shows concentrated bid support between $1,780 and $1,800, with over 12,000 ETH in cumulative bids at the $1,785 level. On the ask side, sell walls are visible at $1,820 and $1,850, suggesting that any breakout above $1,805 will need significant buying momentum to overcome these overhead clusters. The bid-ask spread has narrowed to approximately $0.15 on major exchanges, indicating healthy liquidity during the current consolidation phase.

Exchange flow data shows net outflows from centralized exchanges over the past week, a moderately bullish signal that suggests accumulation rather than distribution at current levels. However, the pace of outflows has slowed compared to early June, indicating that the aggressive accumulation phase may be moderating. The Exchange Reserve metric tracking total ETH held on exchanges has declined by approximately 2.3% over the past seven days, supporting the accumulation thesis. Sustained outflows from exchanges historically correlate with positive price performance over the following weeks, lending further credence to the bullish scenario should ETH hold support.

## Risk Factors

Key risks for ETH include further regulatory uncertainty in the US, continued weakness in the ETH/BTC ratio, potential for a broader crypto market correction if macro conditions deteriorate, and the risk that Ethereum's Layer 2 migration reduces mainnet fee revenue and investor appeal. On the positive side, the Pectra upgrade timeline and growing institutional staking adoption provide fundamental support.

## Conclusion

Ethereum is at a critical juncture, trading at a key Fibonacci level while forming a potentially bullish wedge pattern. The RSI and MACD are showing early signs of momentum improvement, but the broader trend remains bearish until price reclaims the $2,000 level. The most probable scenario is continued consolidation between $1,718 and $1,805 in the near term, followed by a resolution based on whether ETH can break above the 38.2% retracement with conviction. Traders should watch the $1,718 support and $1,805 resistance closely — a decisive move in either direction will set the tone for the weeks ahead.
