# Gold (GLD) Technical Analysis - July 19, 2026

## Elliott Wave Analysis

Applying Elliott Wave principles to GLD's price action since the January 29 high reveals a potentially completed or nearly completed ABC corrective wave. The decline from $509.70 to $395.92 (June 5 low) can be counted as Wave A, a 22.3% decline. The bounce from $395.92 to $421.82 (June 5 to June 9 high) represents Wave B, a 6.5% retracement. The subsequent decline from $421.82 to the June 24 low of $365.92 constitutes Wave C, a 13.2% decline that broke below the Wave A low, as expected in a standard ABC zigzag correction. Wave C equaled 1.618 times Wave A in duration, consistent with Elliott Wave guidelines. If this wave count is correct, the correction from the January high completed on June 24 at $365.92, and what we are seeing now is an early-stage impulsive wave to the upside. However, the July 17 retest of the $363-366 area creates ambiguity. An alternative count suggests that Wave C may be forming an ending diagonal, with the June 24 low representing Wave 3 of C and the current decline representing Wave 5 of C. In this scenario, Wave 5 would need to complete near $350-355 to satisfy Fibonacci extension targets. A third possibility is that the entire ABC correction may be forming as a flat pattern, which would target a retest of the $360 area before a sustained reversal. The lack of impulsive upside waves (strong 5-wave advances) on the daily chart favors the view that the correction is not yet fully complete and that gold may make one more leg lower before establishing a durable bottom.
