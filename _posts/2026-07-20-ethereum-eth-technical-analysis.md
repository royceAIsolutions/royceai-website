## Current Price Action

Ethereum (ETH/USD) is trading at 1,881 dollars as of July 20, 2026, showing a slight recovery from its daily low of 1,841 dollars but still struggling to reclaim the 1,900 dollar psychological level. The intraday range has been relatively narrow at 54 dollars between the low of 1,841 and the high of 1,895, indicating a market in consolidation mode after recent selling pressure.

The broader cryptocurrency market context shows Bitcoin trading near 64,966 dollars with the Crypto Fear and Greed Index sitting at 30, firmly in Fear territory. This marks the 15th consecutive day the index has remained below the 50 neutral threshold, reflecting persistent bearish sentiment across digital asset markets. Ethereum has been underperforming Bitcoin over the past quarter, with the ETH/BTC ratio continuing its downward trend as capital rotates toward the larger asset by market capitalization.

The daily chart reveals that Ethereum has lost approximately 12 percent of its value over the past 30 days, moving from the 2,140 dollar range in mid-June to current levels around 1,880 dollars. This selloff accelerated after the breakdown below the 2,000 dollar psychological support level on July 8, which triggered stop-loss cascades and increased selling pressure from short-term holders.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe is currently reading 38.7, which places it in bearish territory but not yet at oversold levels. The 14-period RSI has been below the 50 midline since July 8, when ETH broke below the 2,000 dollar level. This sustained bearish RSI reading confirms that sellers remain in control of the daily trend, and any bounces should be viewed as countertrend moves until the RSI can reclaim the 50 level.

On the weekly timeframe, the RSI is reading 34.2, approaching oversold territory below 30. The weekly RSI has been declining since March 2026 when it peaked near 72, indicating a prolonged period of weakening momentum. The last time the weekly RSI entered oversold territory below 30 was in October 2025, which preceded a significant rally of approximately 60 percent over the following two months.

The 4-hour RSI is currently at 45.3, showing some intraday recovery from the oversold level of 26 reached during the July 19 selloff. This oversold bounce on the shorter timeframe is typical after sharp declines, but the 4-hour RSI would need to clear the 50 level to confirm that the immediate selling pressure has subsided. If the 4-hour RSI fails at the 50 level, it would indicate that the intraday bounce is losing steam and sellers are reloading.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator on the daily chart shows a bearish configuration. The MACD line at minus 18.4 is well below the signal line at minus 12.7, and the histogram continues to print negative bars that have been expanding since the beginning of July. This bearish crossover occurred on July 5 when the MACD line crossed below the signal line, and the subsequent divergence has widened as selling pressure increased.

The weekly MACD presents an even more concerning picture. The weekly MACD line has crossed below the signal line for the first time since October 2025, and the histogram has turned negative. This weekly bearish crossover is a significant technical event that has historically preceded multi-week corrective periods. The last weekly MACD bearish crossover in April 2025 was followed by a 35 percent decline in ETH over the subsequent six weeks.

On the 4-hour chart, the MACD is attempting a bullish crossover as the MACD line converges on the signal line from below. The histogram bars are shrinking, suggesting that the selling pressure on the shortest timeframe is easing. However, this potential 4-hour bullish crossover would need to be confirmed by price action breaking above the 1,920 dollar resistance level to have any significance for the daily trend.

## Moving Averages

Ethereum's moving average structure is bearish across all standard timeframes. The 20-day Simple Moving Average at 1,968 dollars is below the 50-day SMA at 2,045 dollars, which in turn is below the 100-day SMA at 2,112 dollars. This descending alignment confirms a well-established downtrend, and each moving average level has acted as resistance since the selloff began.

The 200-day SMA, widely considered the long-term trend indicator, sits at 2,245 dollars and continues to slope downward. ETH last traded above its 200-day SMA on June 11, 2026, and has remained below this critical level for 39 consecutive trading days. A sustained move back above the 200-day SMA would be required to signal a potential trend change, but the current downward slope of the indicator suggests that this level will continue to repel price advances in the near term.

The 12-period and 26-period Exponential Moving Averages, which form the basis of the MACD calculation, are also bearishly aligned. The 12 EMA at 1,912 dollars is below the 26 EMA at 1,955 dollars, and the spread between them is approximately 43 dollars, indicating that the bearish trend has strong momentum. For a trend reversal to gain credibility, the 12 EMA would need to cross above the 26 EMA, which would first require a close above 1,930 dollars.

## Volume Analysis

Trading volume over the past five days has been above the 20-day average, confirming that the selloff is occurring on strong participation rather than thin liquidity conditions. Average daily volume for July has been approximately 18 percent higher than the June average, indicating that institutional and retail participants are actively distributing ETH rather than accumulating.

The On-Balance Volume indicator is in a clear downtrend, having made lower lows since June 20. OBV is currently at its lowest level since March 2026, confirming that volume is flowing out of Ethereum on balance. A reversal in OBV would need to precede any sustainable price recovery, as volume leadership typically leads price by several days.

The Volume Price Trend indicator is also negative, reading minus 2,850 on the daily chart. VPT has been declining since June 25 and shows no signs of bottoming. The VPT indicator is particularly useful for detecting hidden selling pressure during consolidation phases, and its continued decline during the current narrow range suggests that the 1,880 dollar level may be distribution rather than accumulation.

## Support and Resistance Levels

Ethereum is currently trading in a critical support zone that requires close monitoring. The primary support level at 1,760 dollars represents the June 2026 swing low and is the most important level to defend for the bulls. A breakdown below 1,760 dollars would open the path to the next major support at 1,680 dollars, which corresponds to the May 2026 low and represents the lower boundary of the six-month trading range.

Below 1,680 dollars, attention shifts to the 1,550 dollar level, which served as resistance in late 2025 before ETH broke higher. This level represents approximately 18 percent downside from current prices and would mark a significant breakdown if reached. The final major support before the 1,200 dollar region sits at 1,420 dollars, which was the November 2025 consolidation area.

On the upside, Ethereum faces immediate resistance at the 1,920 dollar level, which represents the July 17 intraday high. Above that, the 1,950 dollar level corresponds to the 26 EMA and is likely to attract sellers. The 2,000 dollar psychological level is the most significant resistance zone, as it previously served as support for six weeks before the July breakdown. A reclaim of 2,000 dollars would be the first technical sign that the downtrend is weakening, but the more significant resistance sits at the 200-day SMA near 2,245 dollars.

## Chart Patterns

The daily chart shows a potential descending broadening wedge pattern that has been forming since the March 2026 high near 2,800 dollars. This pattern is characterized by lower highs and lower lows with expanding range, and it can be either a reversal pattern or a continuation pattern depending on the breakout direction.

A breakout above the upper trend line of the wedge, currently near 2,050 dollars, would be a bullish signal with a measured move target of approximately 2,400 dollars. However, a breakdown below the lower trend line at 1,680 dollars would confirm bearish continuation with a target near 1,300 dollars. The current price action near the lower boundary of the wedge makes this a critical decision point for Ethereum.

The 4-hour chart shows a potential bullish divergence forming on the RSI. While price made a lower low at 1,841 dollars on July 20 compared to the 1,845 dollar low on July 19, the 4-hour RSI made a higher low, indicating that selling momentum is waning. This divergence is not yet confirmed and would require a break above 1,895 dollars to be validated.

## Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the June 2025 to March 2026 rally from 1,050 dollars to 2,800 dollars provides important context for the current pullback. The 61.8 percent retracement level sits at 1,722 dollars, which is just above the 1,680 dollar support level and represents the typical maximum retracement in a healthy uptrend.

The 78.6 percent Fibonacci retracement at 1,428 dollars represents the boundary between a normal correction and a structural breakdown. If ETH were to lose the 61.8 percent level at 1,722 dollars, the 78.6 percent level at 1,428 dollars would be the next target. A move below 1,428 dollars would suggest that the multi-year uptrend from 2023 is at risk of reversing.

The 50 percent retracement at 1,928 dollars is currently acting as near-term resistance, consistent with the moving average readings. ETH would need to reclaim this level to establish any meaningful recovery trajectory. The 38.2 percent retracement at 2,137 dollars is the intermediate target if a recovery materializes.

## Bollinger Bands

The daily Bollinger Bands are expanding, indicating increasing volatility in Ethereum. The upper band is at 2,230 dollars, the middle band at 1,985 dollars, and the lower band at 1,740 dollars. Price is trading near the lower band, which has historically acted as support during oversold conditions.

The Bandwidth indicator, which measures the width of the bands relative to the middle band, is reading 0.24, above the historical average of 0.18 for ETH. This confirms that volatility is elevated compared to normal conditions, which is consistent with the directional selloff.

Price has been riding the lower Bollinger Band for three consecutive daily candles, suggesting that the selling pressure may be exhausted in the very short term. However, in strong downtrends, price can hug the lower band for extended periods, as seen in April-May 2025 when ETH spent 12 consecutive trading days near or below the lower band.

## Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart is firmly bearish. Price is trading well below the cloud, which spans from 2,140 to 2,320 dollars. The Conversion Line (Tenkan-sen) at 1,925 dollars is below the Base Line (Kijun-sen) at 2,030 dollars, confirming the bearish configuration.

The Leading Span A at 2,110 dollars is below Leading Span B at 2,240 dollars, creating a red cloud that projects lower prices ahead. The cloud is approximately 130 dollars thick at current levels, providing strong resistance that would be difficult to break through without significant catalyst.

The Lagging Span (Chikou Span) is at 1,980 dollars, below price from 30 days ago, confirming that the bearish trend has been in place for more than one full Ichimoku cycle. For an Ichimoku-based bullish signal, price would need to break above the Conversion Line at 1,925 dollars, then the Base Line at 2,030 dollars, and eventually the cloud bottom at 2,140 dollars.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count from the March 2026 high of 2,800 dollars suggests that Ethereum is in the early stages of a larger-degree corrective wave. The decline from 2,800 to 1,760 dollars in June appears to be wave A of an ABC correction, followed by a wave B bounce to 2,120 dollars in early July.

If this count is correct, ETH is currently in wave C of the correction, which would target the 1,680 to 1,720 dollar zone based on 1 to 1 extension of wave A. Wave C typically extends beyond the end of wave A, which would explain why prices have already broken below the 1,760 dollar June low.

An alternative count suggests that the decline from 2,800 dollars is a completed five-wave impulsive move, which would imply that the correction is over and ETH is beginning an impulse to the upside. However, this count requires confirmation in the form of a five-wave advance above 2,120 dollars, which has not yet materialized.

## Market Sentiment

The Crypto Fear and Greed Index at 30 reflects extreme fear among market participants, which historically has been a contrarian bullish signal. However, the index has been below 30 for only 3 days after spending most of July in the 20-30 range, suggesting that sentiment has not yet reached the capitulation levels that have marked previous major bottoms.

Funding rates across major exchanges for ETH perpetual futures are slightly negative at minus 0.002 percent, indicating that short positions are paying a small premium to longs. This is mildly bullish from a positioning perspective, as heavily negative funding has historically preceded short squeezes. However, the current level is not extreme enough to trigger forced covering.

Open interest in ETH futures has declined by approximately 15 percent since the early July high, suggesting that leveraged positions have been flushed out of the market. This reduction in open interest typically reduces the potential for violent liquidations in either direction.

## Trading Recommendations

The overall technical picture for Ethereum remains bearish with the asset trading below all major moving averages, with a bearish MACD crossover on both daily and weekly timeframes, and with price consolidating near critical support levels. Short-term traders should be aware that the oversold conditions on the 4-hour chart could produce a reflexive bounce, but the daily and weekly trends argue for continued downside.

For bearish scenarios, a break and close below 1,760 dollars would confirm the next leg lower with a target of 1,680 dollars. Aggressive short positions could target this level with a stop loss at 1,850 dollars to allow room for normal volatility. A breakdown below 1,680 dollars would open the path to 1,550 dollars.

For bullish scenarios, a reclaim of 1,920 dollars would be the first sign of strength, with 2,000 dollars as the confirmation level. A close above 2,000 dollars on above-average volume would suggest that the corrective wave is complete and that ETH is beginning a recovery. In this scenario, the 2,140 to 2,245 dollar zone would be the intermediate target.

Neutral traders should wait for ETH to establish a clear direction from the current consolidation zone. The risk-reward profile at current levels is unfavorable for both longs and shorts due to the proximity to support and the potential for volatility expansion.

## Risk Factors

The primary risk to the bearish thesis is that the Fear and Greed Index at 30 indicates excessive pessimism, which has historically preceded sharp reversals in crypto markets. If a catalyst emerges such as positive regulatory news, a major Ethereum network upgrade announcement, or a broader market rally led by Bitcoin, short positions could be squeezed aggressively.

The primary risk to the bullish thesis is that the weekly MACD bearish crossover has historically been a reliable signal for multi-week corrections. Additionally, the declining ETH/BTC ratio suggests that capital continues to flow out of Ethereum into Bitcoin, which is a structural headwind for any ETH rally.

Macroeconomic risks include potential interest rate decisions that could affect risk asset valuations, regulatory developments specific to Ethereum's proof-of-stake consensus mechanism, and potential security incidents within the decentralized finance ecosystem that could undermine confidence in the network.

## Conclusion

Ethereum is at a critical technical juncture, trading near major support levels with bearish indicators across most timeframes. The asset is exhibiting all the characteristics of a sustained downtrend including descending moving averages, bearish MACD readings, declining volume indicators, and negative sentiment. The path of least resistance remains lower, with 1,760 and 1,680 dollars as the key levels to watch for bears and 1,920 and 2,000 dollars as the levels that would signal a shift in momentum for bulls. Traders should manage position sizes appropriately given the elevated volatility and the proximity to key support levels that could trigger sharp moves in either direction.
