## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) on the daily timeframe has dropped to **29.75**, firmly in oversold territory. This is a significant development — the last time RSI hit these levels was during the May 20 correction that briefly pushed BTC to $70,200. An RSI below 30 typically signals exhaustion among sellers and often precedes a bounce, though it can remain oversold during strong downtrends.

On the 4-hour chart, RSI sits at **32.4**, also approaching oversold. The 1-hour RSI is at **35.8**, suggesting the selling momentum may be slowing on shorter timeframes. However, traders should note that during sharp corrections, RSI can stay oversold for extended periods. The current setup resembles the April 2025 oversold condition that preceded a 15% rally back above $80,000.

A notable **Golden Cross** formation has appeared on the daily chart — the 50-day SMA crossed above the 200-day SMA. Historically, Bitcoin has had five Golden Cross events, each followed by significant upside moves. While this pattern is repeating, the timing of the subsequent rally remains uncertain.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) on the daily chart shows:
- **MACD Line**: -845.3
- **Signal Line**: -623.1
- **Histogram**: -222.2 (bearish)

The MACD line crossed below the signal line on May 24, generating a bearish crossover that coincided with the breakdown from $76,800 to current levels. The expanding negative histogram indicates accelerating bearish momentum. However, the divergence between the current histogram reading and the price action at the $70,200 support level (where the histogram was at -310) suggests some bullish divergence is forming — price made a lower low while MACD made a higher low.

On the 4-hour chart, the MACD histogram is beginning to contract, which could signal a short-term relief rally if the histogram crosses back above zero. Traders watching for a trend reversal should look for the MACD line to cross back above the signal line with expanding positive histogram bars.

## Market Sentiment & On-Chain Data

The broader market sentiment has shifted to **Fear** according to the Crypto Fear & Greed Index, which reads 24/100 — deep in "Extreme Fear" territory. Historically, Extreme Fear readings have been excellent contrarian buying opportunities, though timing the exact bottom remains challenging.

Bitcoin ETF outflows have accelerated, with **$733 million** flowing out over the past week. This is the largest weekly outflow since February 2026. However, the outflow rate is beginning to slow, with May 28 showing only $45 million in outflows compared to $285 million on May 24.

On-chain data shows exchange reserves declining, meaning coins are moving to cold storage — a long-term bullish signal even as short-term price action remains weak. Whale wallets (1,000+ BTC) have been accumulating since May 25, adding an estimated 12,000 BTC to their holdings.

**Long-term (1-6 months):** Bullish. Golden Cross formation, declining exchange reserves, and whale accumulation all point to a resumption of the uptrend once this corrective phase concludes.
