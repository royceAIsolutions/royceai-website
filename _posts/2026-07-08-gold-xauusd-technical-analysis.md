# Gold (XAU/USD) Technical Analysis — July 8, 2026

## Current Price Action

Gold is trading at $4,059 per troy ounce as of July 8, 2026, down 1.13 percent on the session and 4.73 percent over the past month. The precious metal has pulled back sharply from its recent highs near $4,550, erasing significant gains from the first half of 2026. Despite the short-term weakness, gold remains up 22.5 percent year-to-date, reflecting the strong bullish trend that dominated the first five months of the year. The current decline appears to be a corrective retracement within a longer-term uptrend, but the magnitude of the pullback demands careful technical analysis. Key support lies at the $4,000 psychological level, while resistance has formed near $4,254.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe is reading near 38, putting gold in oversold territory. The RSI dipped below 30 intraday earlier this week, marking the deepest oversold reading since the March 2026 correction. Historically, gold tends to bounce strongly when the daily RSI reaches these levels. Tickeron data indicates that when XAUUSD RSI exited the oversold zone previously, 14 out of 26 instances resulted in a sustained price increase, giving a 54 percent probability of a bounce. The 4-hour RSI is showing early signs of bullish divergence with price making lower lows while RSI prints higher lows. This divergence pattern often precedes trend reversals and suggests that selling pressure is exhausting.

The weekly RSI reads near 45, still in neutral territory but declining. A weekly close above 50 would signal renewed bullish momentum. The monthly RSI remains above 60, confirming that the long-term trend is still bullish despite the current correction. Traders should watch for the daily RSI to reclaim 40 as an early signal that the selling pressure is abating.

## MACD Analysis

The MACD on the daily chart has been declining since mid-June 2026. The MACD line crossed below the signal line on June 14, generating a sell signal that has remained in effect. The histogram has been printing progressively deeper red bars, indicating accelerating bearish momentum. However, the rate of histogram expansion has slowed over the past three trading sessions, suggesting that the selling pressure may be reaching exhaustion.

The weekly MACD is beginning to flatten after declining for three consecutive weeks. A bullish crossover on the weekly timeframe would be a powerful confirmation of a trend reversal, but this signal is likely several weeks away. On the 4-hour chart, the MACD histogram is showing signs of compression, often a precursor to a trend change. A bullish MACD crossover on the 4-hour timeframe would provide an early entry signal for short-term traders looking to catch the bounce.

The moving average alignment shows a compressed configuration with the 20-day SMA through 100-day SMA clustered within a $170 range. This tight grouping often precedes a significant expansion move. The direction of the breakout from this compression zone will likely determine gold direction for the next several weeks.

A daily close below $4,000 would open the door for a move toward the next major support at $3,850, which coincides with the 200-day moving average. On the upside, immediate resistance sits at $4,180, which aligns with the 100-day SMA and the July 3 intraday high. Above that, the $4,550 high is the ultimate resistance target for the medium-term bullish case.

## Risk Factors

The primary risk to the bullish case is further dollar strength. The US Dollar Index has been rallying on hawkish Federal Reserve commentary, and a sustained dollar rally would pressure gold further. Additionally, the Treasury yield curve has been steepening, increasing the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold.

Geopolitical risks remain elevated with ongoing US-Ukraine tensions, which typically support gold prices. However, the market has been focusing on monetary policy dynamics rather than geopolitical risk premiums in recent weeks. The July US jobs report scheduled for next week could be the catalyst for the next major move. A strong jobs number would likely push gold lower, while a weak report could trigger a sharp rally.

The CME FedWatch tool shows a 72 percent probability of a rate hold at the next FOMC meeting, with 28 percent probability of a cut. Gold typically performs well during rate cut cycles, so any shift in rate expectations would be a major catalyst.

Gold is at a critical juncture, testing the $4,000 to $4,050 support zone with oversold technical readings across multiple indicators.
