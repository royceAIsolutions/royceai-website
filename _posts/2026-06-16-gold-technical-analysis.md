Gold (XAU/USD) is trading near $4,369 on June 16, 2026, pulling back from six-day highs as sellers maintain control below the critical $4,400 resistance level. The precious metal has been oscillating in a tight range following a nearly 3% recovery fueled by US-Iran peace deal optimism earlier this week. However, technical indicators suggest the bearish bias remains intact as long as price action stays below the 21-day simple moving average near $4,400.

## Current Price Action

Gold opened the week at $4,312 and rallied to a high of $4,369 before encountering selling pressure near the psychologically important $4,400 zone. The metal is currently consolidating around $4,356, down approximately 0.3% from the intraweek peak. Volume has picked up noticeably during the pullback, suggesting that institutional sellers are active near resistance. The daily candlestick pattern shows a bearish engulfing formation forming on Tuesday as buyers failed to hold above $4,370. The broader weekly trend remains neutral, with price action oscillating between support at $4,300 and resistance at $4,400 for the past two weeks. The recovery from the June low of $4,212 has been steady but lacking the momentum needed to challenge the 200-day SMA at $4,454.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) on the daily timeframe is currently at 48.2, hovering just below the neutral 50 level. This reading indicates that bearish momentum is still marginally in control, though selling pressure is not extreme. The RSI briefly surged to 54.3 during Monday's rally but failed to sustain above the 50 midline. On the weekly chart, the RSI reads 44.7, confirming the medium-term bearish posture. A move above 50 on the daily RSI would be the first technical confirmation that momentum is shifting in favor of buyers. The four-hour RSI sits at 46.1, showing intraday weakness as sellers defend the $4,400 zone. The RSI has not flashed an oversold reading (below 30) since June 5, suggesting that the current pullback has more room to run before finding a durable bottom.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) on the daily chart shows the MACD line at +8.4 and the signal line at +12.1, with the histogram printing negative bars for the fourth consecutive session. The MACD crossed below the signal line on June 13, generating a sell signal that remains in effect. The histogram has been expanding deeper into negative territory, confirming that bearish momentum is accelerating. On the weekly timeframe, the MACD is also bearish, with the MACD line at -15.3 and the signal line at -8.9. The weekly histogram shows bearish divergence, as price made higher highs in June while MACD momentum made lower highs. This divergence pattern is a classic warning signal for trend reversals and suggests the recent recovery may be short-lived.

## Moving Averages

The moving average structure paints a mixed picture. The 21-day SMA at $4,400 is acting as immediate resistance and has not been breached since June 10. The 50-day SMA at $4,388 is also sloping downward, reinforcing the resistance zone between $4,380 and $4,400. The 100-day SMA at $4,352 is providing intraday support, with price bouncing off this level twice in the past two sessions. The 200-day SMA at $4,454 remains the key bull-bear demarcation line — a sustained break above this level would signal a major trend shift. Importantly, the 50-day SMA crossed below the 100-day SMA on May 28, a death cross pattern that typically indicates extended downside pressure. The EMA structure is similarly bearish, with the 12-day EMA at $4,367 below the 26-day EMA at $4,382, confirming short-term bearish alignment.

## Volume Analysis

Volume has been above average during the current pullback, with Tuesday seeing approximately 15% higher volume than the 20-day average. The elevated selling volume near resistance confirms that institutional distribution is occurring at the $4,360-$4,400 zone. On-Balance Volume (OBV) has been declining since June 10, indicating that volume is flowing out of the metal despite the recent price bounce. The Volume Price Trend (VPT) indicator is also showing a bearish divergence, with price recovering while volume trends deteriorate. Accumulation/distribution readings suggest that smart money is using the recovery rally to reduce long positions rather than adding to them. A contraction in volume below the 20-day average would signal that the selling pressure is exhausting, potentially setting up a reversal.

## Support and Resistance Levels

Gold is trading within a well-defined range with multiple levels of support and resistance. On the downside, the first major support sits at $4,300, a level that has held on four separate tests since June 5. Below that, the $4,250 level represents the June 9 low and a key Fibonacci confluence zone. The $4,212 level marks the June 5 swing low and the ultimate support before a potential retest of the $4,100 area. On the upside, resistance begins at $4,370 (Tuesday's high), followed by the critical $4,400 zone where the 21-day SMA converges with horizontal resistance from June 10. The $4,454 level represents the 200-day SMA and the most significant resistance level on the chart. A decisive break above $4,454 would open the path toward $4,500, which aligns with Citi's recent bullish target.

## Chart Patterns

The daily chart shows a descending triangle pattern forming since the May highs near $4,500. The lower boundary of the triangle runs along support at $4,300, while the upper boundary slopes downward from $4,500 to $4,400. This pattern typically resolves with a downside breakdown, with the measured move target near $4,100. However, the recent bounce from $4,212 suggests that buyers are defending the triangle's lower boundary effectively. A descending wedge pattern is also visible on the four-hour chart, which is a bullish reversal pattern when it breaks to the upside. The two conflicting patterns suggest that gold is at a decision point, with the direction of the breakout determining the next major move.

## Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the rally from the June 5 low of $4,212 to the June 10 high of $4,450 reveals that the current pullback has pierced the 61.8% retracement level at $4,303, bouncing cleanly from $4,300. The 50% retracement sits at $4,331 and has been tested multiple times. The 38.2% retracement at $4,359 is currently being tested as resistance. A failure to reclaim the 38.2% level would target a retest of the 61.8% level at $4,303. The 78.6% retracement at $4,263 is the next major support if $4,300 breaks. The Fibonacci extension levels from the same move show resistance at $4,441 (127.2% extension) and $4,500 (161.8% extension), aligning with the 200-day SMA and Citi's target, respectively.

## Bollinger Bands

The daily Bollinger Bands are currently contracting, with the upper band at $4,485 and the lower band at $4,248, for a bandwidth of approximately $237. This contraction follows a period of wide bands during the May sell-off and suggests that volatility is compressing ahead of a significant expansion move. Price is trading below the middle band (20-day SMA) at $4,368, confirming the bearish short-term bias. The bands are sloping slightly downward on the daily chart, consistent with the prevailing downtrend. A move above the middle band would be the first bullish signal, while a breakdown below the lower band near $4,248 would signal accelerating bearish momentum. The four-hour Bollinger Bands show a squeeze pattern that suggests an intraday breakout is imminent.

## Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart presents a bearish picture. Price is trading below the cloud, with the cloud spanning from $4,482 to $4,556 on the daily timeframe — well above current prices. The Tenkan-sen (conversion line) at $4,355 is below the Kijun-sen (base line) at $4,382, forming a bearish TK cross that has persisted since June 8. The Chikou Span (lagging span) is below both price action and the cloud, confirming the bearish trend. The Senkou Span A and B have formed a red cloud that extends into next week, suggesting that bearish conditions will persist. The Ichimoku setup is textbook bearish across all five components, with no bullish signals evident.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count suggests that gold completed wave (A) of a larger ABC corrective pattern at the June 5 low of $4,212. Wave (B) appears to have peaked near $4,450 on June 10, and the current decline represents wave (C) in progress. If this count is correct, wave (C) should extend below the wave (A) low of $4,212, targeting the $4,100-$4,050 zone. The internal structure of wave (C) shows a five-wave pattern developing on the hourly chart, with waves 1 and 2 complete and wave 3 currently unfolding. A 161.8% extension of wave 1 would target $4,210, coinciding with the June 5 low. The alternative count suggests that the ABC correction may be forming a flat pattern, which would limit downside to the $4,250 area.

## Market Sentiment

Market sentiment for gold is cautiously bearish. The CME FedWatch Tool shows a 60% probability of a rate hold at the upcoming FOMC meeting, which would be negative for gold as real yields remain elevated. The US Dollar Index (DXY) has stabilized around 99.5 after Monday's gap lower, and a resumption of dollar strength would add further pressure on gold. The US-Iran peace deal continues to reduce safe-haven demand, though geopolitical uncertainty around implementation provides a floor. COMEX gold futures positioning shows speculative longs decreasing by about 8% in the latest reporting week, while commercial hedgers have increased short positions. The Gold Fear and Greed Index reads 38, in fear territory, suggesting that a sentiment bottom may be approaching.

## Trading Recommendations

The technical picture favors a bearish bias for short-term traders. A short position below $4,400 with a stop at $4,410 and a target of $4,300 offers a favorable risk-reward ratio of approximately 10:1. For more conservative traders, waiting for a breakdown below $4,300 to confirm weakness before entering short with a target of $4,212 is advisable. Bullish traders should wait for a confirmed break above $4,400 on above-average volume before initiating long positions. A move above $4,454 (200-day SMA) would invalidate the bearish thesis and open the path toward $4,500. For swing traders, the current range-bound conditions suggest a mean-reversion strategy (buy near $4,300, sell near $4,400) until a breakout occurs.

## Risk Factors

Key risks to the bearish view include a dovish surprise from the Fed, which would weaken the dollar and boost gold. Escalation of geopolitical tensions beyond the US-Iran framework would revive safe-haven demand. A breakdown in the oil market following the Iran deal could create cross-asset volatility that benefits gold as a hedge. Conversely, a hawkish Fed stance or a sustained dollar rally would accelerate the bearish outlook. The descending triangle support at $4,300 must hold to avoid a rapid decline toward $4,100.

## Conclusion

Gold remains in a technical bearish posture below the $4,400 resistance zone. The RSI, MACD, moving averages, Ichimoku Cloud, and Elliott Wave counts all point to further downside potential toward the $4,300 support level and potentially $4,212-$4,250. A breakout above $4,400 on strong volume would be the first signal of a trend change, but current indicators do not support that scenario. Traders should watch the $4,300 level closely — a daily close below it would confirm the descending triangle breakdown and likely accelerate selling toward $4,100. Until then, range-bound trading between $4,300 and $4,400 remains the high-probability path.
