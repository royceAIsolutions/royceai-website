# FAS Technical Analysis: 3x Leveraged Financials ETF Tests Key Support Levels

The Financial Select Sector SPDR Fund (FAS), the 3x leveraged version of the popular XLF ETF, is at a critical inflection point as of June 26, 2026. Trading near the $160 level, FAS has pulled back from recent highs as the broader financial sector digests a shifting interest rate environment and mixed earnings signals from major banks and insurers. With the Federal Reserve's policy trajectory uncertain and institutional money rotating through financial sub-sectors at a record pace, technical traders are watching key support and resistance levels closely for the next directional move.

## Current Price Action

FAS is trading at approximately $160.25, down 1.8 percent from its June 21 close of $163.20. The pullback represents a 7.2 percent decline from the June 14 high of $172.60, suggesting that the leveraged financial ETF has entered a short-term corrective phase after its impressive 18 percent rally from the May 15 low of $146.10.

The intraday range on June 25 was $158.40 to $162.10, with the price closing near the session low at $159.80. This bearish close, combined with the third consecutive red candle on the daily chart, indicates that sellers remain in control in the near term. The 3x leverage amplifies both gains and losses, making position management particularly critical at current levels.

On a longer-term basis, FAS remains in a well-defined uptrend from its 200-day moving average of $138.50, which it has held as support since October 2025. The 50-day moving average at $155.80 is the immediate line of defense for bulls. A decisive break below this level would signal a trend change and could trigger a move toward the 200-day MA.

## Risk Factors

Leveraged ETFs carry unique risks that unleveraged products do not. The daily reset mechanism means that over multi-day holding periods, FAS can underperform 3X the underlying index due to volatility decay (also called "beta slippage"). In high-volatility environments, this decay can erode returns even if the underlying index moves in the predicted direction.

Additionally, the financial sector faces specific risks: unexpected Fed policy shifts, credit cycle deterioration, regulatory changes affecting banks and insurers, and geopolitical events that impact global financial markets. The 3x leverage amplifies all of these risks proportionally.

Traders should also be aware of the liquidity risk in leveraged ETF options during volatile sessions. Bid-ask spreads can widen significantly during market stress, increasing transaction costs.
