# Bitcoin (BTC/USD) Technical Analysis: June 1, 2026 — Bearish Momentum Builds Near $73,500

## Current Price Action

Bitcoin enters June 2026 trading at approximately **$73,500**, down from its May 6 peak near $83,000. The digital asset has been on a steady decline since mid-May, shedding roughly 11.5% over the past three weeks. Trading volumes have been lackluster, with day traders largely participating through CFD offerings rather than direct spot purchases. Institutional holders like Strategy (843,738 BTC) and SpaceX (18,712 BTC) continue to hold significant positions, but sentiment surrounding crypto scams tied to the SpaceX IPO has added negative pressure.

The broader macro environment remains uncertain. The Iran-US conflict continues without resolution, oil prices remain elevated, and recent CPI data came in hotter than expected — all factors that reduce risk appetite for assets like Bitcoin.

## RSI Analysis

Bitcoin's **daily RSI sits at approximately 49**, hovering near the neutral midpoint but leaning bearish. The indicator has been trending downward since peaking at 68 on May 8, reflecting the sustained selling pressure over the past three weeks.

On the **4-hour timeframe**, RSI is at 44, showing bearish momentum in the short term. Notably, there is a mild bullish divergence forming: price has been making lower lows while the 4-hour RSI has been making higher lows, suggesting that selling pressure may be weakening even as price continues to drift lower.

The **weekly RSI is at 55**, still above the neutral 50 level, indicating that the longer-term uptact remains intact despite the recent pullback. This divergence between daily and weekly RSI suggests Bitcoin may be in a consolidation phase rather than a full trend reversal.

## MACD Analysis

The **daily MACD is firmly bearish**. The MACD line sits at approximately -180, well below the signal line at -120, with a negative histogram of -60. This bearish crossover occurred around May 15 and has been widening since, confirming the downward momentum.

On the **weekly timeframe**, the MACD is at a critical juncture. The line is at 85, just above the signal line at 82, with a thin positive histogram of 3. If this weekly MACD crosses bearish in the coming sessions, it would signal a more significant medium-term trend reversal — a development that would alarm long-term holders.

The **4-hour MACD** shows the line at -45, below the signal at -30, histogram at -15. While still bearish, the rate of decline in the histogram has slowed, which aligns with the RSI divergence noted above.

## Moving Averages

Bitcoin's relationship with its key moving averages tells a mixed story:

- **20-day EMA (~$75,200)**: BTC is trading below the 20-day EMA, confirming short-term bearish momentum. The 20-day EMA is now sloping downward, a sign that the immediate trend favors sellers.

- **50-day SMA (~$72,800)**: Bitcoin is currently testing the 50-day moving average from above. This level has acted as dynamic support since early May. A decisive close below $72,800 would be a significant bearish signal, potentially opening the door to a move toward $70,000.

- **200-day SMA (~$68,500)**: The 200-day moving average remains well below current price and continues to slope upward, confirming that the primary long-term trend is still bullish. The 200-day SMA has not been tested since March 2026.

- **Golden/Death Cross Status**: The 50-day SMA remains above the 200-day SMA, maintaining the golden cross formation from earlier this year. However, the gap between the two averages is narrowing. If Bitcoin fails to hold the 50-day SMA, a retest of the 200-day average could trigger a death cross — a major bearish signal.

## Technical Outlook

**Bullish Scenario:** Bitcoin holds the 50-day SMA at $72,800 and the 50% Fibonacci level at $74,000. A bounce from this zone targets $76,100 (38.2% Fib) and then $78,000. A weekly close above $78,000 would invalidate the bearish structure and open the path back to $83,000.

**Base Case:** Bitcoin continues to consolidate between $72,800 and $76,100 as the market digests the recent decline. The 4-hour RSI divergence suggests a relief bounce is possible in the near term, but the daily MACD remains bearish, capping upside potential.
