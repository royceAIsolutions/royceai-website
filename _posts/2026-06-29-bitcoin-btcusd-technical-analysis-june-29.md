Bitcoin (BTC/USD) is trading near $59,800 on June 29, 2026, extending a brutal decline that has wiped nearly 23% from the April 2026 highs around $77,000. The cryptocurrency has now spent eight consecutive weeks in a bearish trend, with each attempt to bounce selling off at lower highs. The Crypto Fear and Greed Index has collapsed to 13 — Extreme Fear — suggesting capitulation may be approaching, but historical bottoms often coincide with sustained extreme fear rather than a single reading.

This comprehensive technical analysis examines multiple timeframes and indicators to assess whether Bitcoin is approaching a reversal zone or whether further downside remains the higher-probability scenario.

## Current Price Action

Bitcoin currently trades at approximately $59,800, down roughly 3.5% over the past 24 hours and approximately 12% from the local high of $68,000 reached in late May. The price has been making lower highs and lower lows since mid-April, establishing a clear downtrend structure on the daily chart.

The most recent significant move was a breakdown below the $63,000 support level on June 24, which had held as a consolidation base for nearly two weeks. This breakdown triggered accelerated selling that pushed BTC to $59,500 before a modest bounce to current levels. Volume on the breakdown day was approximately 40% above the 30-day average, confirming genuine selling pressure rather than a false breakout.

On the weekly chart, Bitcoin has now closed below the 200-week moving average for the first time since November 2023. This is a significant development for long-term trend analysis, as the 200-week MA has historically served as the bull/bear market demarcation line.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) on the daily timeframe currently reads 28, approaching oversold territory but not yet there. This is a critical reading in the context of the current downtrend:

- RSI has been below 40 for six consecutive weeks, indicating sustained bearish momentum
- The last time RSI reached oversold territory (below 30) was in early June when BTC hit $60,800, followed by a 7% bounce to $65,000
- On the weekly RSI, the reading is 34 — the lowest since the November 2022 bottom near $15,500
- RSI divergence: While price made a lower low on June 24, RSI made a slightly higher low, a subtle bullish divergence that could indicate weakening bearish momentum

The 4-hour RSI shows an interesting pattern: it has been oscillating between 20 and 35 for two weeks, a range-bound oversold state that often precedes a relief rally. However, in strong downtrends, RSI can remain oversold for extended periods, so this alone is not a buy signal.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) on the daily chart is firmly in bearish territory:

- MACD line: -1,850 (below zero and declining)
- Signal line: -1,200
- Histogram: -650 and expanding (bearish momentum accelerating)

The MACD made a bearish crossover on May 15 when the MACD line crossed below the signal line, and it has been widening since then. This is the longest continuous bearish MACD reading since the 2022 bear market. On the weekly chart, the MACD is at its most negative reading since the FTX collapse in November 2022.

A potential early signal to watch: the 4-hour MACD histogram has been making higher highs over the past three days while price has made lower lows. This bullish divergence on a shorter timeframe often precedes a short-term bounce, though it would need confirmation on the daily timeframe to signal a trend reversal.

- Wave 1: $77,000 to $68,000 (May 1-7)
- Wave 2: $68,000 to $72,000 (May 7-11, 5.9% retracement)

If this count is correct, Bitcoin may be in the late stages of Wave 3 or early Wave 4. Wave 3 often extends to 1.618 times the length of Wave 1, which projects to approximately $58,000 — very close to current levels. This suggests the most aggressive selling may be approaching exhaustion.

## Risk Factors

Several factors could invalidate the bearish analysis:

- **Spot ETF inflows resumption:** If Bitcoin spot ETFs see significant inflows (>$500M daily), institutional buying could reverse the trend rapidly
- **Fed policy shift:** Any dovish surprise from the Federal Reserve would benefit risk assets broadly
- **Short squeeze:** With the long/short ratio at 0.82, a move above $64,000 could trigger cascading short covering
- **Macro correlation breakdown:** If equities rally while Bitcoin continues falling, it suggests crypto-specific issues (regulatory, structural) rather than macro drivers

Conversely, risks to the downside include:
- **Regulatory action:** Any major jurisdiction announcing restrictive crypto regulations
- **Exchange issues:** Another major exchange facing solvency concerns
- **Correlation with traditional markets:** If the S&P 500 corrects, Bitcoin typically follows with higher magnitude

## Conclusion

Bitcoin remains in a confirmed bearish trend with multiple indicators supporting further downside. The death cross on moving averages, bearish MACD, and breakdown below key support levels all point toward a continuation of the decline toward $52,000-$54,000. However, the extreme fear reading, bullish RSI divergence on the 4-hour chart, and proximity to the 38.2% Fibonacci retracement suggest a relief rally is becoming increasingly likely in the near term.

The risk/reward currently favors bears for medium-term positions, but short-term traders should watch for a bounce toward $63,000-$65,000 as the lower Bollinger Band and volume profile support attract buyers. A daily close above $63,500 would be the first significant bullish signal in eight weeks.

Key levels: Support at $58,000 and $52,000. Resistance at $63,500 and $68,500. The trend remains bearish until price reclaims the 200-day SMA at $68,500.
