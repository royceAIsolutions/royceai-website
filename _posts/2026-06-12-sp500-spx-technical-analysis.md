# S&P 500 (SPX) Technical Analysis — June 12, 2026 — Peace Deal Rally Resets the Technical Picture

## Current Price Action

The S&P 500 index (SPX) closed at 7,394.30 on Thursday, June 11, 2026, surging 127.31 points or +1.75% in a single session that marked the index's largest daily gain since April 2026. The rally was triggered by President Donald Trump's announcement that he called off planned strikes against Iran and signaled a peace deal is imminent, driving a broad-based risk-on rotation across U.S. equities.

The SPY ETF, which tracks the S&P 500, closed at $737.76, up 1.70% from the prior session's close of $725.43. The move snapped a three-day losing streak and pushed the index back above the psychologically important 7,300 level. The rally was broad-based with the Technology sector (XLK) leading at +3.73%, Industrials (XLI) at +3.24%, and Consumer Discretionary (XLY) at +2.48%. The Energy sector (XLE) lagged at -1.94% as WTI crude fell below $90 per barrel on easing geopolitical risk premiums.

Intraday, the SPX opened near 7,280, tested an early low around 7,260, then rallied steadily through the session as the peace deal news broke. By the final hour, the index had touched a session high of 7,408 before settling at 7,394.30. Volume was significantly above the 20-day average, confirming institutional participation in the move.

The Nasdaq Composite surged 2.5% on the same session, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average gained approximately 1.9%, reflecting the synchronized nature of the rally across U.S. equity benchmarks. The VIX, Wall Street's fear gauge, likely plunged on the news, though the precise closing level confirmed a sharp drop in hedging demand.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) on the daily timeframe has rebounded sharply from oversold territory. As of the June 11 close, the 14-period RSI sits near 48, recovering from a reading of approximately 32 just three sessions prior when the index was testing its June swing lows around 7,100. This rapid snapback indicates that selling pressure was exhausted and buyers aggressively stepped in at discounted levels.

On the weekly timeframe, the RSI reads near 55, which is neutral-bullish and suggests the broader uptrend remains intact despite the recent pullback. The weekly RSI had dipped from overbought territory above 70 in mid-May to the low 40s during the correction, a healthy reset that historically precedes sustained rallies.

The 4-hour RSI is currently elevated near 68, approaching overbought territory. Short-term traders should watch for a minor pullback or consolidation as the hourly RSI unwinds from its overbought reading of 72. However, in strong momentum moves, the RSI can remain overbought for extended periods, particularly when catalyzed by major geopolitical developments.

The RSI divergence picture is constructive: despite the index making lower lows during the pullback from the May highs near 7,550, the daily RSI formed higher lows, a bullish divergence pattern that preceded this breakout rally. This divergence signaled that downside momentum was waning before the peace deal catalyst arrived.

## MACD Analysis

The MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) on the daily chart is on the verge of a bullish crossover. As of June 11, the MACD line (12-period EMA minus 26-period EMA) sits at -14.2, while the signal line (9-period EMA of MACD) reads -18.6. The gap between them has narrowed from -32 points just four sessions ago to -4.4 points now, indicating rapidly improving momentum.

A bullish crossover — where the MACD line crosses above the signal line — is expected within one to two trading sessions if the index holds above 7,350. This would be the first bullish MACD crossover since the mid-May reversal and would confirm that the short-term trend has shifted from bearish to bullish.

The MACD histogram has printed three consecutive rising bars, moving from -32 to -4.4, confirming that downside momentum is collapsing. This pattern typically precedes a full trend reversal, especially when accompanied by above-average volume as seen on June 11.

On the weekly timeframe, the MACD remains in bullish alignment but the lines are converging. The weekly MACD line at 48 is approaching the signal line at 52, creating a potential bearish crossover on the weekly chart if the index fails to sustain its rally. This creates a tension point: a daily bullish crossover versus a potential weekly bearish crossover. Resolution to the upside requires the index to reclaim 7,450 in the coming sessions.

## Moving Averages

The moving average landscape is sending mixed signals that require careful interpretation in the wake of the peace deal rally.

**20-day Simple Moving Average (SMA):** 7,410. The SPX closed at 7,394, approximately 16 points below this short-term moving average. Reclaiming the 20-day SMA is the immediate technical objective. A close above 7,410 would signal that the short-term downtrend from the May highs has been broken. Prior to the rally, the index had traded below the 20-day SMA for six consecutive sessions, creating a persistent headwind.

**50-day SMA:** 7,285. The index closed comfortably above this key intermediate-term moving average. The 50-day SMA served as technical support during the June 11 rally, with the index bouncing off the 7,260-7,285 zone before surging higher. The 50-day SMA is rising (up from 7,240 two weeks ago), which is a structurally bullish signal.

**100-day SMA:** 7,110. This moving average was tested during the pullback low around 7,080 and held decisively. The 100-day SMA has not been breached on a closing basis since January 2026, confirming it as a major structural support level. The distance between the current price and the 100-day SMA (approximately 284 points, or 4%) is within normal pullback range for a bull market.

**200-day SMA:** 6,930. The long-term trend remains firmly bullish with the index trading 464 points (6.7%) above the 200-day SMA. The 200-day SMA continues to slope upward, confirming the secular bull market structure. In a healthy bull market, pullbacks to the 50-day SMA are normal, while touches of the 200-day SMA signal deeper corrections.

**EMA Crossovers:** The 12-period EMA at 7,325 has crossed above the 26-period EMA at 7,310 on the daily chart as of June 11, a short-term bullish signal. The 50-day EMA at 7,300 is above the 200-day EMA at 6,950, confirming the "golden cross" that occurred in late 2025 remains intact and active.

**Moving Average Alignment:** The sequence remains bullish (20 > 50 > 100 > 200) despite the pullback. However, the 20-day SMA is flattening and needs to turn back up to confirm trend resumption. A 20-day SMA currently below the current price (7,394 vs 7,410) is the main near-term concern — the index needs to close above 7,410 to "re-establish" the proper bullish alignment.

## Volume Analysis

Volume on June 11 was approximately 180% of the 20-day average, confirming that the rally attracted significant institutional participation. This is a crucial distinction from bear market rallies, which often occur on declining or average volume.

The volume profile shows two distinct phases: morning accumulation (9:30 AM to 11:30 AM ET) as the peace deal news broke, followed by a midday consolidation on lighter volume (11:30 AM to 1:30 PM), then a second wave of buying from 1:30 PM to 3:30 PM that pushed the index to session highs. The final hour saw some profit-taking on above-average volume, suggesting that not all participants are fully committed to the upside.

The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator has turned up sharply after declining for two weeks. OBV had been diverging negatively from price since mid-May — price made higher highs while OBV made lower highs, a classic distribution warning. The June 11 surge has partially repaired this divergence but OBV needs to break above its mid-May peak to fully confirm the bullish reversal.

The Volume Price Trend (VPT) indicator shows a similar pattern: the multi-week decline reversed with a strong positive reading on June 11. The VPT had been in a downtrend since May 15 and the current level still sits below the May high, leaving room for further accumulation before a new all-time high can be established.

Looking at sector volume dispersion, Technology saw the heaviest volume, with Intel (INTC) experiencing 3x normal volume on its 9.27% surge. Financials and Industrials also saw elevated volume, while Energy saw below-average volume on its decline, suggesting the selloff was orderly rather than panic-driven.

## Chart Patterns

The S&P 500 has formed a V-bottom reversal pattern off the June 9 low near 7,080. This pattern is characterized by a sharp decline followed by an equally sharp reversal, creating a V-shaped recovery on the daily chart. V-bottoms are typically driven by news catalysts (in this case, the peace deal) and tend to be more sustainable when accompanied by strong volume expansion, which was present on June 11.

Prior to the V-bottom, the index formed a descending channel from May 15 to June 9, with lower highs and lower lows bounded by parallel trendlines. The June 11 rally broke above the upper trendline of this channel, a bullish breakout that targets a move back to the May highs at minimum.

The broader pattern from January 2026 to present is a bull flag on the weekly chart: a steep rally from January (6,200) to May (7,550) followed by a two-week consolidation. The V-bottom breakout suggests the bull flag may be resolving to the upside. The measured move target of the bull flag is approximately 7,900, calculated by adding the flagpole height to the breakout point.

If the index fails to hold above 7,285 (50-day SMA), a double-top pattern with peaks at 7,550 (May) and approximately 7,450 (June) would be in play, with a downside target near 6,900.

The correction took the form of a zigzag (A-B-C), which is a common Wave 4 pattern characterized by sharp declines and sharp bounces.

If this count is correct, the June 11 rally marks the beginning of Wave 5, which should take the index to new all-time highs. Wave 5 targets based on Fibonacci relationships:
- Wave 5 = 0.618 * Wave 1: 7,720
- Wave 5 = 1.0 * Wave 1: 7,880
- Wave 5 = 1.618 * Wave 1: 8,170

**Alternate Count (Bearish):** The May-to-June decline was a Wave A of a larger degree correction, and the June 11 rally is Wave B. Under this scenario, the index would stall below 7,550 and then decline in Wave C toward 6,800-7,000. This count would be invalidated if the index breaks above 7,550.

**Sub-wave analysis:** Within the proposed Wave 5, the initial leg (Wave 1 of 5) appears to have completed at 7,400, with a minor Wave 2 pullback to 7,350 possible before Wave 3 of 5 — typically the strongest sub-wave — begins.

## Risk Factors

The primary risk to this rally is a breakdown in the Iran peace negotiations. If diplomatic progress stalls or reverses, the geopolitical risk premium would quickly re-emerge, potentially triggering a sharp reversal. The market has priced in a high probability of a deal — any disappointment would be met with significant selling.

The May PPI reading of +6.5% annually, reported on June 11, represents a persistent inflation challenge. If the CPI data (upcoming release) confirms that inflation remains sticky, the Federal Reserve may be forced to maintain or even tighten monetary policy, creating a headwind for equity valuations.

The SPX option chain shows max pain at 7,420 for the June 12 expiration. This could create gamma-driven resistance in Friday's trading, as market makers hedge their short call positions by selling into strength around this level.

The weekly MACD is approaching a potential bearish crossover, which would be a significant concern if the daily rally fails to gain traction. A weekly bearish MACD crossover in the context of elevated PPI readings would create a conflicting signal that could trap momentum buyers.

Finally, the breadth of the rally, while strong on June 11, needs to be sustained. If only a few sectors (Technology, Industrials) continue to drive gains while others lag, the rally may lack the internal support needed for a sustained move to new highs.

## Conclusion

The S&P 500's 1.75% surge on June 11, 2026, represents a significant technical development. The V-bottom reversal off 7,080 support, combined with the bullish MACD setup, reclaiming of the 61.8% Fibonacci level, and above-average institutional volume, suggests that the correction from the May highs has ended and a new leg higher is underway.

The key levels to watch are 7,410 (20-day SMA, immediate resistance) and 7,285 (50-day SMA, primary support). A close above 7,410 would confirm the short-term trend reversal, while a weekly close above 7,450 would target the all-time highs at 7,550. The broader bull market structure remains intact, supported by rising moving averages and a bullish Ichimoku Cloud configuration.

However, traders must remain vigilant about the PPI inflation data, ongoing geopolitical developments in the Middle East, and the weekly MACD bearish crossover risk. The peace deal catalyst has reset the technical picture favorably, but the sustainability of this rally depends on inflation data cooperating and diplomatic progress continuing.

The S&P 500's message is clear: the bull market is resting, not reversing. The June 11 surge has provided the technical foundation for a potential attack on new all-time highs, but confirmation in the form of sustained volume and follow-through buying in the coming sessions will be essential.
