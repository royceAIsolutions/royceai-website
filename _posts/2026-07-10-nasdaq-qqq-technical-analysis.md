<h2>Current Price Action</h2>

The Invesco QQQ Trust ETF, which tracks the NASDAQ-100 index, closed at $723.28 on July 9, 2026, up $11.84 or 1.66 percent from the previous close. The 52-week range for QQQ spans from $551.56 to $748.65, with the all-time closing high of $745.34 recorded on June 2, 2026. The current price of $723.28 sits approximately 3 percent below the 52-week high, positioning the ETF in the upper quartile of its yearly range but short of reclaiming its peak.

<h2>RSI Analysis</h2>

The 14-day Relative Strength Index for QQQ stands at 51.9, placing it squarely in neutral territory. This reading is significant because it shows that the recent rally has not yet pushed into overbought conditions, leaving room for further upside without triggering exhaustion signals.

The RSI has recovered from the late June lows near 38, where selling pressure had pushed the indicator into oversold territory momentarily. The climb from 38 to the current 51.9 represents a complete cycle from selling climax to recovery, and the RSI now sits at a pivot point where the next significant move will determine the intermediate-term direction.

If the RSI can break above 55, it would confirm renewed buying momentum and target the 60 to 65 zone. Conversely, a rejection and drop below 48 would suggest the recovery has run its course and a retest of support is likely. The weekly RSI reads 49.7, indicating the longer-term momentum is still neutral but tilting slightly bearish after the June correction.

<h2>MACD Analysis</h2>

The MACD analysis presents a cautiously bullish picture. The crossover is valid and the histogram is expanding, suggesting the short-term trend is up. However, the distance from the zero line and the relatively low absolute value of the MACD line compared to previous highs suggests this is a recovery rally within a larger corrective structure, not the beginning of a new impulsive advance.

<h2>Elliott Wave Analysis</h2>

The Elliott Wave structure from the March 2026 low at approximately $620 to the June 2 high at $745 completed a five-wave impulsive sequence. Wave 1 of this sequence advanced from $620 to $660. Wave 2 corrected to $640. Wave 3 extended to $720. Wave 4 retraced to $695. Wave 5 completed at $745.

The decline from the June 2 high to the June 26 low at $693 represents a Wave A correction of the larger degree cycle. The current rally from $693 to $723 represents Wave B of that correction. If this count is correct, Wave C of the correction is still pending and would target a retest of the $693 low or potentially lower to the $670 zone.

The corrective structure is unfolding as a zigzag pattern, with Wave A declining in five sub-waves, Wave B currently rallying in three sub-waves, and Wave C expected to decline in five sub-waves. The Wave B rally has already exceeded the 38.2 percent retracement level, which is characteristic of a strong zigzag correction.

The larger degree trend remains bullish as long as the March 2026 low holds. The current correction is a normal and healthy part of the bull market, and the completion of the corrective pattern should set up the next significant buying opportunity.

<h2>Risk Factors</h2>

The primary risk factor for QQQ and the NASDAQ is the interest rate outlook. Any hawkish surprise from the Federal Reserve that suggests higher for longer rates would pressure growth and technology stocks disproportionately. The upcoming CPI and PPI releases will be critical for the near-term direction.

Geopolitical risks including trade policy uncertainty and regulatory actions against large technology companies could trigger profit-taking. The earnings season for Q2 2026 will be particularly important for validating the current valuation levels of NASDAQ-100 components.

Concentration risk remains a factor, with the top five holdings representing a significant portion of QQQ's weight. Any negative news affecting Apple, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Amazon, or Alphabet would have an outsized impact on the ETF's performance.
