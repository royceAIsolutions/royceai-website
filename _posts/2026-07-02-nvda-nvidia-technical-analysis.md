# NVDA (NVIDIA) Technical Analysis - July 2, 2026

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe is currently recovering from oversold territory. NVDA's RSI(14) touched approximately 32 during the June 29 low at $189.80, which is just above the oversold threshold of 30. This near-oversold reading correctly flagged the subsequent bounce to the $200 area. The current RSI reading is approximately 40-42, indicating that the stock has recovered from deeply bearish momentum but has not yet reached neutral territory (50).

On the 4-hour timeframe, RSI shows a more constructive picture. The shorter-term RSI(14) on 4-hour candles has recovered to approximately 45-48 after forming a bullish divergence pattern during the June 26-29 lows. Bullish divergence occurs when price makes a lower low but RSI makes a higher low, suggesting selling momentum is exhausting. This divergence was the early technical warning that the $190 area would likely hold as support.

The weekly RSI tells a more concerning story, reading approximately 35-38. This indicates that the intermediate-term trend remains bearish despite the daily bounce. Weekly RSI below 40 is a warning that the correction may not be over and that any bounces could be sold into. For a sustained trend reversal to occur, the weekly RSI needs to reclaim the 50 level.

No RSI divergence is present on the daily timeframe currently, as both price and RSI are moving higher together. This is a neutral-to-slightly-bullish configuration — momentum is aligned with price, but neither has confirmed a trend change yet.

The 4-hour MACD has already completed a bullish crossover, with the MACD line crossing above the signal line on June 30. This shorter-term crossover preceded the bounce from $189.80 to $200.09 and confirms that buying pressure is building at the shorter timeframes. However, the histogram bars are still relatively small, indicating that the momentum behind this crossover is modest rather than explosive.

The weekly MACD remains bearish, with the MACD line well below the signal line and the histogram printing negative bars. The weekly MACD histogram has been contracting since mid-June, which is a positive early sign, but a full weekly bullish crossover is likely weeks away under the most optimistic scenario. This multi-timeframe analysis — bullish on 4H, neutral-to-bullish on daily, bearish on weekly — is characteristic of a bottoming process rather than a confirmed reversal.

The converging support and resistance levels create a narrowing range that must eventually resolve. Given the alignment of multiple technical indicators (improving momentum, Bollinger Band contraction, support confluence), the probability slightly favors an eventual upside resolution.

Stop-loss levels should be placed at $189.50 for bullish positions and $206.50 for bearish positions. ## Risk Factors

Several risk factors could invalidate the technical analysis. First, a broader market selloff would likely drag NVDA lower regardless of its individual technical setup. The yellow market regime warrants caution, and any deterioration in macro conditions (spike in VIX above 20, credit spreads above 5%) would increase the probability of a downside resolution.

Second, the semiconductor sector faces ongoing headwinds from export controls and geopolitical tensions. Any negative news on this front could trigger a sector-wide selloff that would overwhelm technical support levels.

Third, NVDA's elevated P/E ratio of 30.64 makes it vulnerable to multiple compression if interest rates rise or growth expectations are revised lower. The stock's premium valuation provides less cushion against negative surprises.

Fourth, the descending channel pattern on the daily timeframe remains in effect. Until NVDA breaks above the channel's upper boundary, the dominant trend is bearish, and bounces within a downtrend are selling opportunities until proven otherwise.

## Key Takeaway

NVDA is at a critical technical juncture. The stock has bounced from a powerful support confluence at the 200-day SMA, 50% Fibonacci retracement, and the lower boundary of a descending channel.
