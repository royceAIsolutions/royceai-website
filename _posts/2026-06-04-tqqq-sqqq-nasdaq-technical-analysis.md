## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) for TQQQ across the 14-period daily timeframe currently reads at 67.37. This level is firmly in bullish territory but remains below the 70 threshold that would indicate overbought conditions. The reading suggests that while buying pressure is dominant, there is still room for additional upside before the ETF becomes technically overextended.

On the weekly timeframe, the RSI registers at approximately 76.81, which sits in overbought territory. This divergence between daily and weekly readings warrants attention. The daily RSI at 67.37 indicates near-term continuation potential, while the weekly RSI at 76.81 suggests that the broader trend may be approaching a point where a pullback or consolidation phase becomes increasingly likely.

Traders should watch for the daily RSI to push above 70 in the coming sessions. A sustained move above 70 would confirm strong bullish momentum but would also increase the probability of a short-term correction. Conversely, if the RSI reverses from current levels without reaching 70, it could signal weakening momentum and a potential trend shift.

The Stochastic RSI provides additional perspective on momentum exhaustion. If the Stochastic RSI crosses below 80 from an overbought reading, it would be a sell signal that often precedes a 3 to 5 percent decline in leveraged ETF positions.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) for TQQQ presents a constructive picture. The MACD line (12-period EMA minus 26-period EMA) is positioned above the signal line (9-period EMA of the MACD), maintaining a bullish configuration that has been in place since the most recent golden cross in mid-May.

The MACD histogram, which measures the distance between the MACD line and the signal line, remains positive at $1.23. This positive reading indicates that bullish momentum is intact and that the rate of price increase continues to accelerate. A histogram at $1.23 is a moderate reading — not at extreme levels that would suggest an imminent reversal, but strong enough to confirm the prevailing uptrend.

Traders should monitor the MACD histogram closely for any signs of contraction. If the histogram bars begin shrinking while the price continues to rise, it would create a bearish divergence that often precedes a trend reversal. On the SQQQ side, the inverse ETF's MACD remains deeply negative, reflecting sustained bearish pressure on the short side of the Nasdaq-100 trade.

## Moving Averages

The moving average structure for TQQQ is decisively bullish across all major timeframes. The 50-day simple moving average (SMA) sits at $63.19, placing current price well above this level with a spread of over 36 percent. This wide gap confirms the strength of the intermediate-term uptrend.

The 100-day SMA at $56.94 and the 200-day SMA at $54.40 provide additional layers of structural support beneath the current price. The 200-day SMA, often regarded as the definitive bull-bear line in technical analysis, sits approximately 59 percent below current trading levels — an unusually wide spread that reflects the powerful rally the Nasdaq-100 has experienced this year.

The exponential moving averages (EMAs) reinforce the bullish picture. The 12-period EMA continues to lead the 26-period EMA higher, maintaining the configuration that generates the positive MACD reading. The 20-period EMA is acting as dynamic support, with the price consistently bouncing off this level during intraday pullbacks.

A critical observation is that the moving averages are stacked in ascending order with the 20-day above the 50-day above the 100-day above the 200-day. This perfect alignment, known as a bullish moving average ribbon, is one of the most reliable indicators of a sustained uptrend. A breakdown of this ribbon, where shorter-term averages cross below longer-term averages, would be required to signal a meaningful trend change.

## Chart Patterns

TQQQ is currently forming a bullish flag pattern on the daily chart. The sharp rally in mid-May created the flagpole, while the recent sideways consolidation has created the flag itself. This pattern is one of the most reliable continuation formations in technical analysis.

The measured move target of the flag pattern, calculated by adding the flagpole height to the breakout point, suggests a potential target of approximately $95 to $98 on the TQQQ. This aligns with the resistance levels identified above and provides a technical basis for bullish positioning.

On the weekly chart, no clear bearish reversal patterns are evident. There are no shooting stars, bearish engulfing patterns, or evening stars that would suggest an imminent top. The weekly candles continue to print higher highs and higher lows, the fundamental definition of an uptrend.

If the flag pattern fails and TQQQ breaks below the lower trendline of the flag, the measured move would target a decline to approximately $65, representing a significant correction that would invalidate the immediate-term bullish outlook.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

Applying Elliott Wave principles to TQQQ's price action, the current rally structure suggests we may be in the third wave of a larger impulse wave. Third waves are typically the longest and strongest in Elliott Wave theory, characterized by accelerating momentum, expanding volume, and powerful breakouts.

If this wave count is correct, we are approximately 60 to 70 percent through the third wave, with additional upside remaining before a fourth-wave corrective pullback begins. The target for wave three aligns approximately with the $92 to $95 zone identified in the resistance analysis.

After wave three completes, a wave four correction would typically retrace 23.6 to 38.2 percent of the wave three advance, bringing TQQQ back to the $78 to $82 range. Wave five would then push to new highs, potentially exceeding $100.

The alternate count suggests that we may already be in wave five, which would mean the uptrend is more mature and closer to exhaustion. This alternate count would be invalidated by a move above $92 without a significant corrective structure forming first.

## Risk Factors

Leveraged ETFs carry unique risks that every trader must understand before taking a position. TQQQ and SQQQ reset their leverage daily, which means their long-term returns can deviate significantly from 3 times the underlying index return due to volatility decay.

In a flat but volatile market, TQQQ can lose value even if the Nasdaq-100 ends unchanged because of the compounding effect of daily leverage resets. This decay is most pronounced in high-volatility environments and during sideways markets.

Single-stock concentration risk is another factor. The Nasdaq-100 is heavily weighted toward the largest technology companies. A negative development affecting a few major holdings can disproportionately impact the index and by extension TQQQ.

Black swan events — unexpected geopolitical shocks, Federal Reserve policy surprises, or sector-specific disruptions — can cause rapid moves that trigger stop-losses before traders have time to react. Position sizing and risk management are essential tools for mitigating these risks.
