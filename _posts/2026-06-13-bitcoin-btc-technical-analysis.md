## Current Price Action

Bitcoin is trading at approximately $63,793 as of June 13, 2026, showing signs of a tentative recovery after a sharp pullback from the $73,000 resistance zone earlier in the month. The digital asset experienced a decisive rejection near the $73,000 to $75,000 range, which has acted as a formidable resistance ceiling going back to the April highs. The decline brought BTC down through several key support levels before finding a floor near the psychologically important $60,000 round number, where buyers stepped in aggressively.

The recent bounce from the $60,000 support zone back above $63,000 has been driven by a combination of technical factors and macro news flow. Reports of a potential US-Iran peace deal generated risk-on sentiment across crypto markets, while institutional accumulation at lower levels provided a bid beneath the market. However, the recovery remains measured and has not yet reclaimed any of the major moving averages that were lost during the sell-off.

The daily candle structure shows a series of higher lows forming over the past five trading sessions, suggesting that buying pressure is gradually increasing. However, each attempt at a rally has been met with selling near the $65,000 level, indicating that the market remains in a zone of indecision. A clear break above $65,000 would be the first meaningful bullish signal, while a breakdown below $60,000 would open the door to a retest of the May lows near $56,000.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe currently reads 42.3, placing it in bearish territory but above the oversold threshold of 30. This is a notable improvement from the June 6 reading of 28.7, when BTC was in deeply oversold conditions during the panic low near $60,000. The recovery in RSI from oversold territory is a constructive development that typically precedes at least a short-term bounce.

On the weekly timeframe, RSI sits at 46.8, continuing a decline from the overbought reading of 72.0 seen in early April. The weekly RSI is approaching the 40 to 45 zone, which has historically acted as a major support area for Bitcoin during bull market corrections. If weekly RSI holds above 40 and begins to curl upward, it would suggest that the current correction is a healthy retracement within a larger uptrend rather than the start of a bear market.

The four-hour RSI oscillates between 38 and 52, reflecting the choppy, range-bound nature of the current price action. Short-term momentum indicators are neutral, offering no clear directional edge for day traders. A sustained move above 50 on the four-hour RSI with correlated price action would be the first confirmation of a near-term bullish shift.

The daily RSI has not yet crossed above the 50 midpoint, which remains the critical threshold to watch. In previous correction cycles, a decisive daily RSI close above 50 has been the earliest technical signal that the correction phase is ending and a new uptrend leg is beginning. Until that happens, the path of least resistance remains sideways to lower.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator on the daily chart shows the MACD line at negative 487, with the signal line at negative 532. The MACD histogram has been contracting for the past four days, moving from negative 890 toward the current level. This histogram contraction indicates that downside momentum is decelerating, which is the first step in a potential bullish crossover.

The MACD lines themselves remain in bearish alignment — the MACD line below the signal line — which is the textbook definition of a sell signal. However, the narrowing gap between the two lines suggests that a bullish crossover could occur within the next five to eight trading sessions if prices continue to stabilize or drift higher. A confirmed bullish crossover on the daily MACD would be a significant technical event, as it has historically preceded sustained rallies of 15 to 25 percent.

On the weekly timeframe, the MACD is in a bearish crossover that began in early May. The weekly MACD histogram shows declining momentum, consistent with a correction that is still in progress at the higher timeframe level. Weekly MACD alignment typically takes several weeks to months to reverse, so traders should not expect a rapid turnaround. The daily MACD improvement must be sustained over time to influence the weekly reading.

The hourly MACD has already produced a bullish crossover and is now curling upward, consistent with the short-term bounce from $60,000. This lower-timeframe signal is providing some support for the current price recovery, but it needs to be confirmed by higher timeframe alignment before it can be considered a tradable entry.

## Moving Averages

Bitcoin's moving average structure tells a story of a market that has lost its short-term bullish alignment but is not yet in a full-blown bearish configuration. The 20-day Simple Moving Average currently sits at $66,800, declining at a rate of approximately $400 per day. Price is trading well below this level at $63,793, indicating that short-term momentum remains bearish. A reclaim of the 20 SMA would be the first requirement for any serious recovery attempt.

The 50-day SMA is at $69,200 and continues to slope upward, although the slope is flattening. Price trading below the 50 SMA is a bearish signal on the intermediate timeframe, and as long as BTC remains beneath this level, any rallies should be considered countertrend bounces rather than trend reversals.

The 100-day SMA provides a critical support reference at $61,500. Bitcoin has tested this level three times in the past two weeks and has held each time, confirming its significance as a major support zone. The 100 SMA is still rising, which is a positive structural feature. A breakdown below the 100 SMA would be technically damaging and would likely accelerate selling toward the 200 SMA.

The 200-day SMA is the most important long-term support level on the chart. It currently sits at $56,200 and continues to slope upward at a steady trajectory. The 200 SMA has not been tested since October 2024, and a move down to this level would represent a 12 percent decline from current prices. Many long-term institutional buyers have their buy orders clustered around the 200 SMA, making it a natural accumulation zone.

The exponential moving averages tell a similar story. The 12 EMA at $64,500 is below the 26 EMA at $66,100, confirming a bearish EMA alignment on the daily chart. A bullish EMA crossover — the 12 EMA crossing above the 26 EMA — would be a strongly bullish signal and would likely coincide with a MACD bullish crossover. This confluence of signals would provide a high-probability entry for trend-following traders.

## Volume Analysis

Trading volume during the decline from $73,000 to $60,000 was significantly elevated, with several sessions recording volume readings more than 1.8 times the 30-day average. High-volume selling during a decline is a hallmark of distribution, suggesting that larger players were reducing positions during the drop. However, the volume spike near the $60,000 low was accompanied by a rapid price recovery, which is more characteristic of a capitulation bottom than sustained distribution.

The bounce from $60,000 has occurred on declining volume, which is typical for countertrend rallies. For the recovery to have staying power, volume needs to expand on green days as buying interest returns. The On-Balance Volume indicator remains below its 20-day moving average, confirming that cumulative buying pressure has not yet returned to levels consistent with a new uptrend.

The Volume Price Trend indicator is flat, reflecting the range-bound conditions of the past week. The VPT is showing a slight bullish divergence, trending higher while price has remained range-bound. This divergence suggests that accumulation may be occurring beneath the surface, which would be a precursor to a breakout above the $65,000 resistance.

Exchange inflow and outflow data from on-chain sources shows that approximately 12,000 BTC have moved from exchange wallets to cold storage over the past week. This pattern of exchange outflows is typically interpreted as accumulation, as investors move coins off exchanges for long-term holding. The outflow rate has accelerated since the $60,000 low was established, supporting the thesis that informed capital is buying the dip.

This pattern is characterized by lower highs and lower lows within a wedge structure that is expanding rather than contracting. Descending broadening wedges are typically reversal patterns, and a breakout above the upper trendline would be a strongly bullish signal.

The wedge upper boundary currently intersects near $68,000, while the lower boundary tracks toward $58,000 by the end of June. The pattern resolution typically occurs when price breaks decisively through one of the boundaries with above-average volume. A break above $68,000 would target a measured move to approximately $82,000, while a break below $58,000 would target $48,000.

On the lower timeframe, the four-hour chart shows a potential double bottom formation with lows at $60,100 on June 6 and $60,050 on June 10. A confirmed close above $63,500 with volume would activate the double bottom pattern, projecting a target of approximately $67,000 for the measured move.

The 0.382 retracement level sits at $61,500, which coincides almost exactly with the 100-day SMA. Bitcoin has bounced from precisely this level multiple times in the past two weeks, confirming its importance.

The 0.500 retracement level is at $57,000, which is near the May swing low and the initial area of the 200-day SMA. A deeper correction to this level would be within the normal boundaries of a bull market correction — 50 percent retracements are common in healthy trending markets.

The 0.618 retracement level at $52,500 represents the maximum correction level that would still be considered a bull market pullback. A move below $52,500 would technically put Bitcoin into a bear market on the intermediate timeframe, as it would represent a retracement of more than 61.8 percent of the prior advance.

From the current price perspective, the key Fibonacci extension levels for a recovery move are derived from the decline from $73,000 to $60,000. The 0.382 extension of this decline is at $64,900 — near the current resistance zone. The 0.500 extension is at $66,500. The 0.618 extension is at $68,000, which aligns with the wedge upper boundary and would represent a significant technical victory for bulls.

The lower band extended to $58,800 at the peak of the selling pressure, and price has now recovered back toward the middle band at $65,000. The upper band is at $71,200 and declining.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count for Bitcoin suggests that the current correction is part of a larger fourth wave within an ongoing bull market impulse structure. The impulse wave from $38,000 to $76,000 from November 2024 to April 2026 represents a completed wave three of a larger-degree five-wave advance. The current decline from $76,000 is wave four.

In standard Elliott Wave theory, wave four corrections typically retrace 23.6 to 38.2 percent of wave three, and they tend to be complex, time-consuming affairs. The retracement to $60,000 represents a 32 percent pullback from the $76,000 high, which is within the expected range for a wave four. The internal structure of the decline shows a clear three-wave ABC pattern, with the A leg from $76,000 to $65,000, the B leg bounce to $73,000, and the C leg decline to $60,000.

If this wave count is correct, the current price action around $63,000 to $64,000 represents the bottoming phase of wave four. The next major move should be wave five to the upside, targeting new all-time highs above $76,000. The measured move target for wave five, using the standard technique of measuring the length of wave one and projecting from the wave four low, suggests a target in the $85,000 to $95,000 range.

The alternate count is that the decline from $76,000 is the first wave of a larger bearish correction rather than a fourth wave within an impulse. This count would see the current bounce as wave two of a bearish sequence, which would ultimately break below $60,000 and target much lower levels. While this count is possible, the price action at support levels — including the strong bounce from the 100-day SMA and the 38.2 percent Fibonacci level — favors the bullish wave four interpretation.

## Market Sentiment

The Crypto Fear and Greed Index is currently reading 38, which is in "Fear" territory — down from 72 in early April when Bitcoin was trading near $73,000. While fear readings are uncomfortable for holders, they are historically associated with market bottoms rather than tops. Major Bitcoin bottoms in 2018, 2020, 2022, and 2024 were all accompanied by Fear and Greed readings below 20.

Funding rates on major perpetual futures exchanges have turned mildly negative, indicating that short sellers are paying a premium to maintain their positions. Negative funding rates are often a contrarian bullish signal, as they suggest that the market is overcrowded on the short side and positioned for a short squeeze.

Open interest in Bitcoin futures has declined by approximately 18 percent from the April peak, reflecting deleveraging during the correction. A reduction in open interest during a price decline is characteristic of a healthy correction — it removes excess leverage from the system and sets the stage for the next leg higher.

Institutional flows, as measured by the spot Bitcoin ETF data, show a pattern of modest net outflows over the past two weeks, but the pace of outflows has slowed significantly. Several days in the past week have seen net inflows, suggesting that institutional interest is returning at lower price levels.

## Trading Recommendations

For swing traders, the current setup offers a favorable risk-reward profile for long positions with a stop below $60,000. The confluence of the 100-day SMA, the 38.2 percent Fibonacci retracement, the descending wedge lower boundary, and the double bottom pattern all point to $60,000 as a reliable support zone.

**Bullish scenario:** A breakout above $65,000 with volume would target a move to $68,000, followed by a retest of $73,000. A successful retest of $73,000 would open the door to new all-time highs above $76,000 within the coming weeks. Entry near $63,500 with a stop at $59,500 and a target at $68,000 offers a 3:1 risk-reward ratio.

**Bearish scenario:** A breakdown below $60,000 would invalidate the bullish pattern and target the 200-day SMA near $56,200. Below that, the 50 percent Fibonacci retracement at $57,000 and the $56,800 swing low are the next supports. A break below $56,000 would suggest that the larger correction is still in progress. Short entries below $59,500 with a stop at $61,000 and a target at $56,000 would be a reasonable bearish trade.

**Conservative approach:** Wait for either a confirmed daily close above the 20-day SMA at $66,800 for long entries, or a breakdown below the 100-day SMA at $61,500 for short entries. Trading the middle of a range — between $60,000 and $65,000 — carries elevated risk of whipsaw.

## Risk Factors

The primary risk to the bullish thesis is a breakdown below $60,000. If that level fails, the market structure would shift from a support-holding corrective phase to a distribution phase, potentially accelerating selling toward the 200-day SMA. Traders should track the weekly close relative to $60,000 as the most important risk management reference.

Macro risks include further escalation of geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, changes in Federal Reserve policy, and regulatory developments in the United States. The SEC's recent focus on stablecoin regulation and the potential for a strategic Bitcoin reserve bill at the state level create an uncertain policy backdrop.

Structural risks include a potential liquidity crisis in the crypto lending market and the impact of the upcoming Bitcoin halving cycle dynamics. As the market moves deeper into the post-halving phase, the reduction in new supply issuance provides a structural tailwind, but this effect is gradual and may be overwhelmed by short-term selling pressure.

## Conclusion

Bitcoin is at a critical juncture. The $60,000 to $65,000 range represents a zone where multiple technical factors converge — the 100-day SMA, the 38.2 percent Fibonacci retracement, the descending wedge lower boundary, and a clear double bottom pattern. The market is showing early signs of stabilization, with improving RSI readings, MACD histogram contraction, bullish divergence on the VPT, and evidence of institutional accumulation at lower levels.

However, the trend is not yet bullish. Price remains below all major short-term moving averages, the MACD is in bearish alignment, and the Ichimoku Cloud is bearish. The path to a sustained recovery requires a clean break above $65,000, a daily RSI above 50, and a MACD bullish crossover — none of which have been confirmed as of this analysis.

For traders, the risk-reward ratio favors bullish positioning near support with tight stops, but the market demands patience and discipline. The next decisive move — above $65,000 or below $60,000 — will likely set the direction for the next several weeks. Until that breakout or breakdown occurs, range-bound trading with careful risk management is the appropriate approach.
