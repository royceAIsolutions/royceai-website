## Current Price Action

The Direxion Daily Financial Bull 3X Shares ETF (FAS) closed Friday June 12 at $143.67, gaining $4.56 (+3.28%) in a powerful single-session surge that marked the leveraged fund's highest close since early April. The rally comes amid a broader rotation into financial stocks as investors reposition ahead of the Federal Reserve's June policy decision. FAS has now gained 5.01% over the past week and 11.83% over the past month, dramatically outperforming the broader S&P 500's flat-to-slightly-negative performance during the same period. The financial sector has emerged as a relative strength leader in early June, with banks and brokerages benefiting from rising bond yields and a steepening yield curve that expands net interest margins. FAS's 3x leverage amplifies the underlying Financial Select Sector Index moves, making this the most volatile and high-beta way to play the financial rotation theme.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on FAS's daily chart has surged to 62.4, climbing sharply from oversold territory near 32 just three weeks ago. This reading places FAS in neutral-to-bullish territory, with room to run before hitting the overbought threshold at 70. The weekly RSI reads 44.8, still below the 50 midpoint, suggesting the medium-term trend has room for further upside recovery before becoming extended. The monthly RSI sits at 38, indicating that on the longest timeframe FAS remains in a bearish posture after the significant selloff from its 52-week high. The RSI momentum divergence is notable: while price made lower lows in late May near $126, the daily RSI registered a higher low, a classic bullish divergence pattern that preceded the current breakout. On the hourly chart, RSI has reached 68, approaching overbought territory in the near term, which may trigger a short-term pullback or consolidation before the next leg higher.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator on FAS's daily chart generated a bullish crossover on June 9, when the MACD line crossed above the signal line after a 47-day bearish cycle. The histogram has since turned positive and expanded for four consecutive sessions, confirming accelerating upward momentum. The MACD line at -1.84 remains below the zero line, meaning the medium-term trend is still technically bearish, but the rate of change has shifted decisively positive. The weekly MACD is showing early signs of a bullish convergence, though the MACD line at -8.32 is still well below the signal line at -6.15, and a crossover at the weekly level would require several more weeks of sustained buying. On the monthly chart, MACD continues to deteriorate, with the histogram printing progressively larger red bars since February 2026, a reminder that the multi-month trend remains under pressure despite the recent rally.

A golden cross at this level would require FAS to sustain above $148 for approximately three weeks.

## Chart Patterns

FAS has formed a textbook ascending wedge pattern since the May 28 low of $126, with higher highs and higher lows on declining intraday range. Ascending wedges in downtrends can be bearish reversal patterns, but in the context of the bullish RSI divergence and volume confirmation, this wedge is more likely resolving bullishly as a flagpole-and-pennant continuation pattern. The pattern projects a measured move target of approximately $158, based on the height of the flagpole from the $126 low to the first peak at $142, added to the breakout level near $142. Additionally, a double-bottom pattern is visible on the weekly chart with lows at $126 in late May and $127 in mid-May, with the neckline at the April high of $158.80. Within this count, wave 1 fell from $184.40 to $158.80, wave 2 rallied to $172.30, wave 3 extended to $136.20, wave 4 bounced to $148.50, and wave 5 terminated at $126. ## Risk Factors

FAS is a leveraged 3x ETF, meaning daily returns are magnified threefold in both directions. Decay from volatility drag over time means long-term holding of leveraged ETFs underperforms the underlying index. The ETF rebalances daily, creating compounding effects that can deviate significantly from 3x returns over multi-week periods. The underlying Financial Select Sector Index is concentrated, with the top five holdings (Berkshire Hathaway, JPMorgan Chase, Visa, Mastercard, Goldman Sachs) representing approximately 40% of the index weight. A negative catalyst affecting any of these mega-cap components would disproportionately impact FAS. The Fed's June policy decision is the primary near-term risk, with a hawkish surprise potentially reversing the recent financial sector gains. Regulatory changes to capital requirements, dividend restrictions, or the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau's agenda could also weigh on bank profitability.

## Conclusion

FAS has staged an impressive breakout from deeply oversold conditions, driven by a rotation into financial stocks, bullish RSI divergence, and strong volume confirmation. The technical picture has improved markedly over the past two weeks, with the daily MACD bullish crossover, price reclaiming the 20-day and 50-day SMAs, and the first cloud breakout since April. However, significant overhead resistance remains at the 100-day and 200-day SMAs, the 50% and 61.8% Fibonacci retracements, and the April high. The key level to watch in the coming week is $145.65 (50% Fibonacci retracement) — a clean break above this level with continued volume would confirm the recovery has further to run toward $155-$160. The immediate risk is a short-term pullback to $136-$140 as overbought hourly RSI readings cool, which would provide a more favorable entry for the continuation move. The FOMC decision on June 18 will be the defining event, with the financial sector's direction for the remainder of Q2 likely determined by the rate path signals. Overall, FAS is in the early stages of a tactical recovery within a broader bearish trend, offering short-to-medium-term opportunities for traders who respect the 3x leverage risk.
