## Risk Factors

Traders and investors should consider the following risk factors when analyzing FAS:

1. **Leverage Decay:** As a 3X daily leveraged ETF, FAS suffers from volatility decay in sideways or choppy markets. The compounding effect of daily rebalancing means that FAS can underperform 3X the underlying index return in volatile, non-trending environments. The current consolidation phase is precisely the type of market condition that erodes leveraged ETF value.

2. **Sector Concentration:** FAS's underlying holdings are concentrated in financial stocks, which are sensitive to interest rate policy, credit conditions, and regulatory changes. A surprise rate hike, a commercial real estate crisis, or regulatory tightening could disproportionately impact FAS relative to broad market ETFs.

3. **Broad Market Correlation:** Financial sector performance is highly correlated with broader economic conditions. A recession signal (inverted yield curve deepening, rising unemployment claims, or a sharp decline in consumer confidence) could trigger a sector-wide selloff regardless of FAS's individual technical setup.

4. **Liquidity Risk:** While FAS has adequate daily volume, leveraged ETFs can experience significant bid-ask spreads during periods of market stress. Position sizing should account for potential slippage.
