# Next Week Market Outlook: June 8–14, 2026 — Technical Analysis of Major Indexes

## Current Market Context

As we close the trading week of June 5, 2026, the major US equity indexes present a picture of record-setting strength with a cautionary pullback in the final sessions. The S&P 500 closed the week at approximately 7,383, down 2.64% from its all-time high of 7,600 reached on June 1, but still up over 23% year-to-date. The Dow Jones Industrial Average reached a new record high earlier in the week, soaring 875 points on June 4 to close at fresh all-time levels before giving back some gains in Friday's session. The Nasdaq Composite experienced a sharper weekly decline of approximately 4.7%, driven primarily by a selloff in semiconductor stocks following Broadcom's cautious outlook.

The catalyst for the late-week pullback centered on the chip sector, where Broadcom's forward guidance disappointed market expectations, triggering a rotation out of high-beta technology names. This sector rotation has created opportunities in cyclical and value-oriented segments of the market, setting up an interesting dynamic for the week ahead. The broader market remains supported by a remarkably resilient economic backdrop, with inflation data trending softer than expected and the Federal Reserve maintaining its accommodative stance.

This week's outlook focuses on whether the S&P 500 can reclaim its record levels, whether the selloff in semiconductors stabilizes, and what key technical levels will define the trading range for the week of June 8 through June 14, 2026.

## Chart Patterns

The daily chart of the S&P 500 reveals a potential **Bear Flag** pattern forming after the sharp rally from 7,000 to 7,600:

- **Flagpole:** The 600-point rally from April 15 to June 1
- **Flag:** A descending channel from 7,600 to approximately 7,350 over the past week
- **Breakdown target:** 7,600 - 600 = 7,000 (the April milestone level)
- **Invalidation level:** A move back above 7,500 would negate the bear flag

The primary count suggests the current weakness is a buying opportunity within an ongoing uptrend, with the 7,200 to 7,300 zone being the ideal risk-reward area for adding exposure.
