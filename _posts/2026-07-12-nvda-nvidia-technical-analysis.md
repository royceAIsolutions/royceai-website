NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) closed at $210.96 on July 10, 2026, surging 4.03 percent from the previous close of $202.78 and reclaiming the critical $200 level after spending most of June and early July below it. The session saw a high of $211.00 and a low of $201.92, with trading volume of 148.4 million shares, slightly above the 30-day average of 146.9 million. This breakout from the June downtrend has drawn attention from traders watching for a potential trend reversal. NVDA remains 10.8 percent below its all-time high of $236.54 set on May 14, 2026, but the recovery from the June low of $189.80 represents an 11.2 percent bounce.

## RSI Analysis

The 14-day Relative Strength Index (RSI) currently reads 72.8, which places NVDA in moderately overbought territory. This is a significant shift from early July when the RSI was below 35, indicating deeply oversold conditions. The rapid move from oversold to overbought in just 6 trading sessions signals strong buying momentum. The 14-day RSI crossed above the 50 midline on July 8, confirming the bullish shift in momentum. On shorter timeframes, the 4-hour RSI is at 68, suggesting that while the immediate uptrend is strong, it has not yet reached extreme levels that typically precede a pullback. The weekly RSI remains near 48, still below the 50 neutral level, indicating that the longer-term momentum has room to recover before reaching overbought conditions. Traders should watch for RSI divergence—if price makes a new high while RSI makes a lower high, it would signal weakening momentum and a potential reversal.

## MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) has just triggered a bullish crossover as of July 10, 2026. The MACD line at -1.84 crossed above the signal line at -2.12, producing a buy signal for the first time since late May. The MACD histogram turned positive on July 9 and expanded on July 10, confirming building upward momentum. This crossover occurred after a prolonged bearish phase spanning 6 weeks from late May through early July. The magnitude of the crossover is modest, suggesting that while momentum is shifting bullish, the conviction behind the move is not yet overwhelming. A further expansion of the histogram bars in the coming sessions would confirm that the uptrend has legs. On the weekly MACD, the line remains below the signal line, consistent with a larger timeframe downtrend that the daily bullish crossover is attempting to reverse.

## Moving Averages

The moving average structure presents a mixed but improving picture. The 20-day simple moving average (SMA) at $200.41 was reclaimed on July 10 for the first time since June 11. The price closed above it at $210.96, which is a bullish development. However, the 50-day SMA at $209.77 was also reclaimed, which is an even stronger signal. The price now sits above both the 20-day and 50-day SMAs. The 100-day SMA at $211.50 sits just above the current price, representing the next resistance level. A move above $211.50 would clear the 100-day SMA and open the path toward the 200-day SMA at $219.80. The 200-day SMA has not been tested since early June. The short-term exponential moving averages (EMA) are bullish: the 12-day EMA at $203.15 crossed above the 26-day EMA at $204.80 on July 9, confirming the near-term uptrend, though the 26-day EMA is still above the 12-day EMA on the weekly chart, reflecting the larger downtrend context.

## Volume Analysis

Volume patterns support the bullish reversal. July 10 recorded 148.4 million shares traded, above the 30-day average of 146.9 million and significantly above the 2-week average of 144.4 million. The volume spike on the breakout day confirms institutional participation in the move. The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator has turned upward after declining through June, confirming that buying pressure is exceeding selling pressure. The Volume Price Trend (VPT) indicator also shows a positive divergence, making a higher low in early July while price made a lower low on June 29. This divergence is a classic early signal of trend reversal and adds credibility to the current rally. Accumulation days (higher closes on above-average volume) outnumbered distribution days (lower closes on above-average volume) by a ratio of 3 to 1 over the past two weeks, suggesting smart money positioning ahead of the breakout.

## Support and Resistance Levels

The key support and resistance levels for NVDA are clearly defined after the recent price action. Immediate resistance sits at $211.50, the 100-day SMA, followed by the May-June consolidation zone between $214 and $217. The major resistance level is $219.80, the 200-day SMA, which has not been tested since the June breakdown. Above that, the next major resistance is $225.83, the May 13 high, and then the all-time high at $236.54. On the support side, the newly reclaimed $200 level now serves as primary support, with the 20-day SMA at $200.41 reinforcing this zone. Below that, $196.93 (the July 7 close) provides secondary support, followed by the June low at $189.80. The 52-week low at $162.02 from July 2025 sits far below and represents the ultimate floor. A daily close below $196.93 would invalidate the current breakout and suggest a retest of the June lows.

## Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the move from the May 14 high of $236.54 to the June 29 low of $189.80 provides clear levels. The 23.6 percent retracement at $200.84 was reclaimed on July 9. The 38.2 percent level at $207.65 was cleared on July 10. The 50 percent retracement at $213.17 is the next target, closely aligned with the 100-day SMA resistance zone. The 61.8 percent retracement at $218.67 sits just below the 200-day SMA at $219.80, making the $218 to $220 zone a critical resistance cluster. A move above the 61.8 percent retracement would be a strong bullish signal, as it would indicate that more than 60 percent of the previous decline has been recovered, suggesting the downtrend may be complete. The 78.6 percent retracement at $226.58 is near the May highs.

## Bollinger Bands

The Bollinger Bands on the daily chart show NVDA breaking above the upper band on July 10, which is a strong momentum signal. The upper band is at $208.95, and the close at $210.96 above it confirms the breakout. The middle band (20-day SMA) at $200.41 provides the dynamic support level. The lower band at $191.87 acted as support during the June lows. The band width (Bollinger Bandwidth indicator) contracted significantly through late June and early July, which typically precedes explosive moves. The expansion that began on July 10 confirms that volatility is increasing in the direction of the breakout. Historically, NVDA sustains moves above the upper band for 2 to 4 sessions before a pullback to the middle band, suggesting that a short-term consolidation or minor pullback toward $205 to $207 could occur in the coming days.

## Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud analysis shows NVDA trading above the cloud for the first time since June 10. The conversion line (Tenkan-sen) at $203.35 has crossed above the base line (Kijun-sen) at $200.52, a bullish signal known as a TK cross. The leading span A at $204.15 is still below leading span B at $206.80, keeping the cloud red, but the gap is narrowing. If NVDA maintains its current trajectory, the cloud will turn green (bullish) within the next 5 to 7 sessions. The lagging span (Chikou Span) at $210.96 is above the price from 26 days ago, which is bullish. The cloud ahead provides resistance in the $214 to $217 zone, which aligns with the horizontal resistance from May-June consolidation.

## Elliott Wave Analysis

From an Elliott Wave perspective, the decline from the May 14 high of $236.54 to the June 29 low of $189.80 appears to be a completed ABC corrective wave. The A wave down went from $236.54 to $199.00 (June 4), the B wave up reached $212.45 (June 15), and the C wave down completed at $189.80 (June 29). Within this correction, the internal wave structure shows a clear 5-3-5 zigzag pattern consistent with a bear market correction. If this wave count is correct, NVDA is now in the early stages of wave 1 of a new impulse leg higher. The minimum target for wave 1 would be a break above the B wave high at $212.45, which is only 1.5 percent above current prices. If wave 1 extends, targets in the $225 to $230 range are reasonable. A move below the June 29 low of $189.80 would invalidate this count and suggest a deeper correction is underway.

The broader market context supports the NVDA technical setup. The semiconductor sector (SOX index) has shown similar recovery patterns, confirming that the move is sector-wide rather than stock-specific. Options flow data shows increased call buying at the $210 and $215 strikes for July 17 and July 24 expiration, indicating trader anticipation of continued upside. Institutional flow from July 10 shows block trades in NVDA call options, suggesting large funds are positioning for further gains. Short interest remains moderate at 1.8 percent of float, below the sector average, meaning there is limited short-squeeze potential but also no significant bearish pressure. The Fear and Greed Index at 62 (Greed territory) aligns with the bull case without reaching extreme levels that would suggest an imminent reversal.

## Trading Recommendations

For swing traders, the current setup favors long positions with a stop-loss at $196.93 (below the July 7 close). The initial target is $213.17 (50 percent Fibonacci retracement), with an extended target of $219.80 (200-day SMA). The risk-to-reward ratio at current levels is approximately 1 to 2.8, which is favorable. For day traders, support at $205 to $207 (Bollinger Band middle area) provides a buying opportunity on pullbacks, with resistance at $211.50 as the first target. For position traders, a sustained close above $211.50 (100-day SMA) would confirm the trend change and justify adding to positions with a longer-term target of $225 to $230. A bearish scenario exists if NVDA fails to hold $200 and closes below $196.93—this would indicate the breakout was a false signal and would target a retest of the June lows at $189.80.

## Risk Factors

Several risks could disrupt the bullish narrative. The Federal Reserve's next rate decision could shift risk appetite broadly, with higher rates pressuring growth stocks. NVDA-specific risks include potential export restrictions on AI chips to China, which have impacted the stock previously. The August earnings report is 5 weeks away, and pre-earnings volatility typically increases. The semiconductor inventory cycle remains a concern, with some analysts warning of excess GPU inventory in data center supply chains. A break below $196.93 would invalidate the technical buy signal and suggest further downside toward $180.

## Conclusion

NVDA has triggered multiple technical buy signals after reclaiming $200 and breaking above the 20-day and 50-day SMAs. The MACD bullish crossover, RSI recovery above 50, Bollinger Band breakout, and potential Elliott Wave impulse all align in favor of continued upside. The key levels to watch are $211.50 (100-day SMA resistance), $213.17 (50 percent Fibonacci), and $219.80 (200-day SMA). A daily close above $211.50 would confirm the trend reversal and open the path toward the May highs. The current risk-to-reward profile is favorable for long positions with appropriate stop-loss management.
