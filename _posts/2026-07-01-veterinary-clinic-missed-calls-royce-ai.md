# Veterinary Clinics Losing Patients to Missed Calls - Royce AI

## The Expensive Problem

Veterinary clinics across the United States are losing 25-30% of potential patients due to missed calls, with after-hours emergency calls being missed at nearly 100% rates. Industry data from the American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA) shows that veterinary practices receive an average of 150-250 calls per week, yet 25-30% go unanswered during business hours, and after-hours emergency calls are almost entirely missed.

When a pet owner calls with an emergency - their dog ate chocolate, their cat is having trouble breathing, their rabbit stopped eating - and no one answers, that pet owner doesn't wait. They call the next clinic. Studies show 75% of pet owners who don't reach a human on the first call will not call back - they simply move to the next available veterinary practice.

## The Financial Impact

Consider a mid-sized veterinary clinic seeing 400 patients per month at an average revenue of 350 dollars per visit. If they miss 25% of 1000 monthly calls (250 calls), and just 15% of those missed calls were genuine patients (37 patients), that's 37 lost appointments x 350 dollars = 12,950 dollars in monthly lost revenue.

Over a year, that's 155,400 dollars vanishing because phones weren't answered. For a veterinary practice operating on 10-15% net profit margins, this can mean the difference between a thriving practice and one struggling to cover overhead.
