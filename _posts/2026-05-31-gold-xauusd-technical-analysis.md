# Gold XAU/USD Technical Analysis May 31 2026: Post-Correction Rebound

## Current Price Action

Gold (XAU/USD) is trading at $4,540.17 per troy ounce as of May 31, 2026, recovering from a sharp corrective phase that saw the precious metal shed approximately 19% from its all-time high of $5,595 reached on January 29, 2026. The spot price gained $0.41 (+0.01%) in early trading, signaling a potential stabilization after weeks of volatile price action.

The recent two-week period has been characterized by a failure to break above the declining 50-day moving average, with gold oscillating between $4,393 (May 28 low) and $4,571 (May 25 high). This $178 range represents the tightest consolidation band since March 2026, suggesting that a significant directional move may be imminent. Trading volume on COMEX Gold Futures (GC1!) has been above average, with open interest expanding as institutional players reposition ahead of the June Nonfarm Payrolls report.

The geopolitical landscape continues to support gold's safe-haven appeal. Ongoing U.S.-Iran tensions and broader Middle East instability provide a baseline bid for precious metals. However, the strengthening of risk-on sentiment in equity markets has capped gold's upside, creating a tug-of-war between safe-haven demand and opportunity cost dynamics as real yields remain elevated.

## RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index for gold on the daily timeframe is currently at 44.7, indicating neutral-to-bearish momentum. The RSI has been trending lower since late April when it peaked at 68.4 during the failed attempt to reclaim the $5,000 psychological level. The consistent decline in RSI reflects persistent selling pressure throughout the correction.

On the 4-hour timeframe, RSI is at 41.2, approaching oversold territory but not yet triggering a reversal signal. The 1-hour RSI at 38.9 is the lowest reading in two weeks, and historically, readings below 40 on the 1-hour chart have preceded short-term bounces of 1-3% within 24-48 hours.

A notable bullish divergence is developing on the 4-hour RSI. While gold made a lower low at $4,393 on May 28 compared to the May 22 low of $4,431, the 4-hour RSI made a higher low (38.1 vs 35.4). This bullish divergence suggests that selling momentum is weakening despite the lower price, often a precursor to a trend reversal or meaningful rebound.

## MACD Analysis

The daily MACD for gold shows the MACD line at -48.5 and the signal line at -35.2, with the histogram at -13.3. The MACD line crossed below the signal line on May 12, generating a bearish crossover that coincided with the break below the psychologically important $5,000 level. The histogram has been printing negative (red) bars since then, confirming the bearish momentum.

However, the rate of decline in the MACD histogram is decelerating. The histogram printed at -18.7 on May 20, improved to -15.1 on May 25, and currently stands at -13.3 on May 31. This contraction of the negative histogram bars indicates that bearish momentum is losing steam, even though the trend remains technically bearish.

On the 4-hour timeframe, the MACD shows early signs of a potential bullish crossover. The 4-hour MACD line is at -12.8 and the signal line at -14.2, meaning the MACD line has actually crossed above the signal line on this shorter timeframe. This 4-hour bullish MACD crossover supports the RSI divergence signal and suggests a short-term bounce is likely.

Gold is currently trading below all major daily moving averages, confirming the intermediate-term bearish trend:

- **20-day SMA**: $4,685 (currently resistance, sloping downward at $18/day)

The alignment — price below all major SMAs with the 20-day below the 50-day below the 100-day — is classically bearish. The 200-day SMA remains the only moving average providing structural support, and gold has not seriously tested this level since March 2026.

Exponential moving averages tell a similar story. The 12-day EMA at $4,595 is providing immediate overhead resistance, while the 26-day EMA at $4,650 has been a consistent rejection level over the past two weeks. The 50-day EMA at $4,780 coincides with the 50-day SMA, reinforcing this as a major resistance zone.

A critical development to watch is the approach of the 100-day SMA from above. Gold is trading just $80 above this level at $4,540 versus the 100-day SMA at $4,620 (actually gold is $80 below). A break below the 100-day SMA would signal further downside toward the 200-day SMA at $4,285. Conversely, a reclaim of the 100-day SMA would be the first bullish technical signal in weeks.

Chart Patterns

Gold is forming a potential double bottom pattern on the daily chart. The first trough formed at $4,030 on April 15, followed by a rally to $4,890 on May 8. The second trough is currently forming near the May 28 low of $4,393. While the second trough is higher than the first (which is typical for double bottoms), the pattern would only be confirmed by a break above the intermediate peak at $4,890.

The measured move target for a confirmed double bottom would be $4,890 + ($4,890 - $4,030) = $5,750, which is above the January all-time high. While this may seem ambitious, the double bottom would signal a complete reversal of the corrective structure and a resumption of the long-term bull trend.

Alternatively, gold may be forming a descending triangle pattern with the horizontal support at $4,030 (April low) and descending resistance from the January high through the May 8 peak and current declining trendline. Descending triangles typically resolve to the downside, with a target of approximately $3,550. However, the bullish divergences and volume dry-down suggest that an upside resolution (fake-out) of the descending triangle is more likely.

The internal structure of Wave 4 suggests a W-X-Y double zigzag correction:
- **Wave W**: $5,595 to $4,850 (completed in February)
- **Wave X**: $4,850 to $5,180 (completed in March)
- **Wave Y**: Currently underway, targeting $4,030-$4,200

Wave Y appears to be forming as a five-wave decline:
- Wave (a): $5,180 to $4,650
- Wave (b): $4,650 to $4,890
- Wave (c): $4,890 to near completion, targeting $4,030-$4,200

If this wave count is correct, gold is in the final stages of the corrective phase and should soon begin Wave 5 to new all-time highs, potentially targeting $6,000+.

Risk Factors

Several risk factors could significantly impact gold's price trajectory:

1. **Nonfarm Payrolls (June 6)**: The upcoming NFP report is the most significant near-term catalyst. Strong jobs data could push real yields higher and gold lower. Weak data could trigger a gold rally toward $4,800.

2. **Federal Reserve Policy**: Markets are pricing a 42% probability of a rate cut at the June FOMC meeting. Any hawkish surprise could send gold toward $4,200, while dovish commentary could propel it above $4,800.

3. **US-Iran Geopolitical Situation**: De-escalation would reduce safe-haven demand and pressure gold lower. Further escalation could trigger a sharp flight to safety pushing gold toward $4,800-$5,000.

4. **US Dollar Strength**: Continued DXY strength above 104 would be bearish for gold, while a DXY break below 100 would provide significant tailwinds.

5. **Central Bank Purchases**: Central banks purchased over 1,000 tonnes of gold in 2025. Continued strong buying in 2026 would provide structural support and limit downside.

6. **Mining Supply**: Gold mining production costs average $1,200-$1,400 per ounce globally. Prices below $4,000 would begin to stress higher-cost producers, potentially reducing supply and providing a floor.

Conclusion

Gold at $4,540 is at a critical juncture following a 19% correction from January's all-time high. Multiple technical indicators — including bullish RSI divergence on the 4-hour chart, contracting MACD histogram, decreasing volume on price declines, and a potential double bottom pattern — suggest that the corrective phase may be nearing completion.

However, the alignment of moving averages remains bearish, with gold trading below all major SMAs. The 200-day SMA at $4,285 is the line in the sand for the long-term bull trend. A break below this level would signal a deeper correction toward $4,030 and potentially $3,800. Conversely, a reclaim of the 20-day SMA at $4,685 would confirm a trend reversal and open the path toward $5,000 and beyond.

The upcoming Nonfarm Payrolls report on June 6 is likely to be the catalyst that determines gold's direction for the remainder of Q2 2026. Traders should maintain tight risk management and wait for clear breakout confirmation before establishing directional positions. The converging technical signals make this one of the most consequential setups for gold in 2026.
