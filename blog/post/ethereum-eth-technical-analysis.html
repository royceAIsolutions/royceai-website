Ethereum ETH Technical Analysis July 1: Key Levels to Watch

R
Royce Koh
Founder, RoyceAI
July 1, 2026

Current Price Action

Ethereum enters July 2026 trading near $1,570, close to multi-month lows after recording three consecutive red weekly candles. The price action shows ETH struggling to hold above the $1,600 psychological level, with the July 1 open at $1,569.74 and an intraday range between $1,569 and $1,598. The overall trend remains bearish in the short term, with ETH down approximately 18 percent from its June highs near $1,920 and over 40 percent from its 2026 peak above $2,600. Daily closes below $1,600 have become the norm over the past two weeks, suggesting persistent selling pressure.

RSI Analysis

The daily Relative Strength Index (RSI) for ETH currently reads 32.4, approaching oversold territory below 30. This is the lowest RSI reading since the March 2026 selloff that took ETH to $1,420. The 14-period RSI on the 4-hour chart shows a slightly higher reading of 36.8, while the weekly RSI stands at 38.2, indicating bearish momentum across all major timeframes. RSI divergence is not yet present on the daily chart, meaning the downtrend lacks a clear reversal signal. A move into oversold territory below 30 could trigger a short-term bounce, but without bullish divergence, any rally is likely to be sold into.

MACD Analysis

The MACD (12, 26, 9) on the daily timeframe shows the MACD line at negative 38.4, well below the signal line at negative 22.1, with the histogram printing increasingly negative bars. This configuration confirms bearish momentum is accelerating. The weekly MACD crossed negative in mid-June and continues to widen, a signal that has historically preceded additional downside of 8 to 12 percent before exhaustion. On the 4-hour chart, the MACD histogram shows early signs of compression, which could indicate a short-term consolidation or minor bounce before the next leg lower.

Moving Averages

The moving average picture is overwhelmingly bearish. ETH is trading below all major simple moving averages: the 20-day SMA at $1,745, the 50-day SMA at $1,830, the 100-day SMA at $1,960, and the 200-day SMA at $2,150. The distance between price and the 200-day SMA is approximately 27 percent, a bearish condition known as a wide gap that typically precedes either a sharp mean reversion rally or continued decline toward a new lower equilibrium. The 20-day SMA recently crossed below the 50-day SMA, forming a death cross on the short-term timeframe. The 50-day SMA is approaching a cross below the 100-day SMA, which would be a medium-term bearish signal. The exponential moving averages tell the same story: the 12 EMA at $1,620 is below the 26 EMA at $1,690, with both declining. On the weekly chart, ETH is below the 20-week EMA at $1,850 and approaching the 50-week EMA at $1,450, which represents the last major support before the bear market territory below $1,400.

Volume Analysis

Volume analysis reveals elevated selling pressure. Average daily volume over the past 20 days is approximately 12.8 million ETH, compared to the 50-day average of 10.2 million, representing a 25 percent increase in activity. This volume expansion during a downtrend confirms distribution by larger holders. The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator is in a steep decline, making lower lows since late May. Volume Price Trend (VPT) is also negative, confirming that volume-weighted price movement favors sellers. The Chaikin Money Flow (CMF) reads negative 0.18, indicating net capital outflows. A volume-weighted average price (VWAP) calculation shows ETH trading below the 20-day VWAP of $1,680, a bearish positioning. Any recovery attempt will need to see volume dry up on pullbacks to signal selling exhaustion.

Support and Resistance

Key support levels for ETH are concentrated in the $1,500 to $1,420 zone. The first major support sits at $1,500, a psychological round number that has acted as a pivot level in previous trading sessions. Below that, the March 2026 low of $1,420 represents the most significant demand zone on the chart, where ETH previously bounced with 22 percent follow-through. The 50-week EMA at $1,450 lies between these two levels, adding technical weight to the zone. On the downside, a break below $1,420 opens the path to $1,300 and eventually the 2025 lows near $1,150.

Resistance levels are stacked closely above current price. The first hurdle is $1,600, which ETH tested on July 1 but failed to hold. Above that, the 20-day SMA at $1,745 represents the first meaningful resistance. The next major resistance is the $1,850 zone, which aligns with the 20-week EMA and the June breakdown level. The $2,000 round number is the key resistance zone that needs to be reclaimed for a trend reversal.

Chart Patterns

On the daily chart, ETH appears to be forming a descending channel with lower highs and lower lows. The upper trendline connects the June 10 high of $1,920 with the June 24 high of $1,685. The lower trendline connects the June 18 low of $1,510 with the current price action near $1,570. The channel width suggests a potential target of $1,350 if price reaches the lower boundary. There are no clear reversal patterns such as double bottoms or bullish engulfing candles on the daily timeframe. The 4-hour chart shows a series of lower highs since June 28, with no meaningful bullish structure forming.

Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the March 2026 low of $1,420 to the May 2026 high of $2,620, ETH is currently trading below the 0.786 retracement level at $1,675. The 0.618 level at $1,880 was lost in mid-June. The 0.5 level at $2,020 and the 0.382 level at $2,160 are now distant resistance targets. The 1.0 extension at $1,420 is the next downside target if selling continues. A full retracement of the rally would imply a move to the 1.272 extension near $1,250 and the 1.618 extension near $1,080.

Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands on the daily chart are widening, indicating increased volatility. The upper band is at $1,940 while the lower band is at $1,480. ETH is currently trading in the lower half of the bands, closer to the lower band than the midline at $1,710. Band width has expanded from 180 points two weeks ago to 460 points currently, reflecting growing price dispersion. A touch or brief break of the lower band near $1,480 is a realistic scenario. Historically, touches of the lower band on the daily chart have led to mean-reversion bounces averaging 5 to 8 percent within three to five sessions.

Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart presents a bearish picture. Price is trading well below the cloud, with the cloud spanning $1,780 to $2,050. The Tenkan-sen (conversion line) at $1,620 is below the Kijun-sen (base line) at $1,730, forming a bearish TK cross. The Chikou Span (lagging line) is below price and below the cloud from two periods ago, confirming downside momentum. The cloud itself is red (bearish) for the next 26 periods, as the Senkou Span A is below Senkou Span B. The Kumo Twist, where the cloud changes color, is not expected for at least 30 trading sessions, meaning the bearish cloud structure is firmly entrenched.

Elliott Wave

A preliminary Elliott Wave count suggests ETH completed a Wave A decline from the May high of $2,620 to the June low of $1,510. The subsequent bounce to $1,920 in mid-June represents Wave B. The current decline from $1,920 appears to be Wave C of a larger ABC corrective structure. If this Wave C is equal in length to Wave A (approximately $1,110), the target for completion would be near $810. A more conservative estimate using a 0.618 Fibonacci relationship of Wave A gives a target of $1,370. The structure within Wave C appears to be a five-wave impulse on the 4-hour chart, with Waves 1 and 3 completed and Wave 4 potentially developing. Once Wave 5 completes near $1,370 to $1,420, a significant countertrend rally of 15 to 25 percent would be expected.

Market Sentiment

The Crypto Fear and Greed Index currently reads 22, indicating extreme fear, down from 28 last week and 45 a month ago. Funding rates on major exchanges are slightly negative at negative 0.005 percent, reflecting a bias toward short positions. The put-call ratio for ETH options on Deribit stands at 1.35, the highest level since March 2026, indicating bearish positioning skew. Open interest has declined by approximately 12 percent over the past week as leveraged positions are unwound. Social sentiment analysis shows bearish mentions outnumbering bullish mentions by a ratio of 3 to 1.

Trading Recommendations

The overall bias for ETH is bearish in the short to medium term. Active traders may consider short positions on bounces toward $1,600 to $1,620, with stops above $1,660 and targets at $1,500 and $1,420. For long-side traders, waiting for a confirmed reversal pattern or a test of the $1,420 support zone before entering is recommended. A bullish scenario requires a daily close above $1,745 (20-day SMA) to suggest a short-term bottom. The most probable path over the next two weeks is continued downside toward the $1,420 level, followed by a mean-reversion bounce. Position sizing should account for the elevated volatility.

Risk Factors

The primary risk to the bearish thesis is a sudden macro catalyst such as a Federal Reserve pivot, positive Ethereum ETF news, or a regulatory development that triggers short covering. ETH is sensitive to Bitcoin price action, and a Bitcoin rally above $70,000 could lift ETH despite its own weak fundamentals. On-chain data shows large holders accumulating at current levels, with addresses holding 10,000-plus ETH increasing by 2.4 percent over the past week. This accumulation suggests institutional interest at these prices and could provide a floor. Conversely, a broader crypto market selloff, further negative ETF flows, or a technical breakdown below $1,420 could accelerate losses toward $1,300.

Conclusion

ETH is in a clear downtrend across all technical indicators. The RSI approaching oversold and widening Bollinger Bands suggest volatility ahead but no immediate reversal signal. The $1,420 support zone from March 2026 is the key level to watch for a potential bounce or breakdown. Short-term traders should favor bearish positions with tight risk management, while longer-term buyers may find attractive entries near the $1,420 to $1,450 zone. A daily close above $1,745 would be the first sign of trend exhaustion, but until then, the path of least resistance remains lower.
