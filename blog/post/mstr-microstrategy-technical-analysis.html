MSTR Analysis — Oversold Bounce From $82 Support

R
Royce Koh
Founder, RoyceAI
July 4, 2026

MicroStrategy (MSTR) closed at $100.77 on July 2, up 7.90% from the previous close of $93.39 and continuing a powerful bounce from the June 26 low of $81.81. The stock has recovered 22.4% in four trading sessions, driven by renewed buying interest following a brutal sell-off that erased over 80% of the stock's value from its 52-week high of $457.22. As a direct Bitcoin proxy with 1539 employees and a $35.3 billion market cap, MSTR remains tightly correlated with cryptocurrency sentiment and macro risk appetite.

RSI Analysis

The 14-period RSI currently sits at 40.9, indicating a decisive move out of oversold territory (below 30) where the stock spent several days during the June 26 low. The RSI on the most recent 14 days is 36.5, still below the 50 midline that separates bearish from bullish momentum. The rapid ascent from oversold readings below 25 to the current 40.9 level signals strong buying pressure but also suggests near-term consolidation is possible before a sustained uptrend can be confirmed. A break above RSI 50 would be the first technical confirmation of a trend reversal.

MACD Analysis

The MACD line at -0.58 is approaching a bullish crossover with the signal line, which would be the first positive MACD signal since mid-June. The MACD histogram has been narrowing since June 26, confirming that downside momentum is decelerating. The 12-period EMA at 100.66 crossing above the 26-period EMA at 115.28 would require price to recover another 14.5% — a realistic target if Bitcoin continues its recovery. Watch for the histogram turning positive (crossing above zero) as a secondary confirmation of bullish momentum.

Moving Averages

Price action relative to key moving averages tells a clear story. MSTR trades below the SMA20 at 109.27 and well below the SMA50 at 145.02. The EMA12 at 100.66 is critical — price closed just above it on July 2, the first time above this level since June 18. This is a positive near-term signal. The next resistance cluster sits at the SMA20 (109.27) and the EMA26 (115.28). A daily close above 109 would represent the first meaningful trend improvement. The SMA200 is not calculable with available data but given the 52-week range the stock has been in a confirmed downtrend since the April highs.

Volume Analysis

Volume patterns strongly support the bounce thesis. Average volume over the full data period is 21.2 million shares. The five-day average ending July 2 reached 39.1 million shares — 84% above the baseline — confirming institutional accumulation. The heaviest volume day in the sample was June 26 (45.7 million shares), the day of the low at 82.31, which matches the classic capitulation-volume pattern. Subsequent days showed declining volume on the bounce, which is expected during a relief rally but needs to see a volume pickup on any breakout attempt above 104.

Support and Resistance

The support structure has been clearly defined by recent price action. Primary support sits at 81.81, the 52-week low established on June 26. This level aligns with the Bollinger Band lower band at 78.73, creating a strong support zone between 78 and 82. Secondary support lies at 85 which held during the June 30 retest. Near-term resistance sits at 104.11, the July 2 intraday high. The next major resistance zone is 109 to 110, where the SMA20 converges with horizontal resistance from mid-June trading. Above that, the 115 to 117 zone represents the EMA26 and the June 18 close level.

Chart Patterns

The price action from April to June 2026 formed a clear downtrend channel with lower highs and lower lows. The break of the 100 level to the downside in June accelerated selling pressure. The bounce from 82 represents a potential failed breakdown attempt — if price holds above 82 and builds a higher low in the coming days, a double-bottom pattern may emerge with the neckline at 104. A break above 104.11 would confirm this pattern with an upside target of approximately 125 (measured move from the pattern). Below 82, the pattern invalidates and the next major support is unknown but likely in the 60 to 70 range given the absence of prior support levels.

Fibonacci Retracement

Using the June 18 high of 112.53 to the June 26 low of 82.31 as the reference swing, the 23.6% retracement sits at 89.43, the 38.2% level at 93.86, and the 50% level at 97.42. Price has already cleared all three of these levels — the 50% retracement was taken out on July 1 when MSTR closed at 93.39. The 61.8% retracement at 100.99 is the critical next level — the July 2 close of 100.77 sits just below it. A daily close above 101 would be a strongly bullish signal, confirming the retracement has reached the first major Fibonacci level. The 78.6% retracement sits at 106.09, aligning with the near-term resistance cluster.

Bollinger Bands

The 20-period Bollinger Bands show the upper band at 139.81, the middle band (SMA20) at 109.27, and the lower band at 78.73. Price has climbed from below the lower band during the June 26 panic to now trading between the lower and middle bands — a constructive recovery. The band width is still wide, reflecting elevated volatility. A normalization of band width would signal that the worst of the volatility is behind the stock. Price needs to reach the middle band at 109.27 for a meaningful improvement in the Bollinger structure.

Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud remains bearish, with price well below the cloud. The conversion line (tenkan-sen) is likely turning upward following the bounce, while the base line (kijun-sen) remains flat to slightly negative. The lagging span (chikou span) is confirming the downtrend, having been below price for several weeks. A cross of the conversion line above the base line (TK cross) would be a bullish signal, but this requires price to establish a sustained uptrend. The cloud is currently projecting resistance in the 110 to 120 zone for the coming weeks.

Elliott Wave

The decline from the April high of 186.01 to the June 26 low of 82.31 appears to be a completed five-wave impulsive move. Wave 1 down from 186.01 to 126.55, wave 2 up to 165.81 (May highs), wave 3 extended down to 82.31, wave 4 bounce to current levels, and wave 5 potentially truncated or still to come. If the count is correct, we are in a wave 4 bounce that could retrace 38.2 to 50% of the entire decline, targeting the 121 to 137 zone, before a final wave 5 low. However, the strength of the current bounce suggests it could extend further if Bitcoin rallies significantly.

Market Sentiment

MSTR is a sentiment-driven stock, heavily correlated with Bitcoin. The stock's sharp recovery from 82 to 100.77 in four sessions suggests a sentiment shift as Bitcoin appears to be stabilizing. Elevated volume on the bounce confirms genuine buying interest rather than a dead cat bounce on low volume. The Fear and Greed Index for crypto is likely moving out of extreme fear territory. MSTR's float of 330 million shares and 35.3 billion market cap provide reasonable liquidity for institutional accumulation.

Trading Recommendations

Bullish scenario: A break above 104.11 on above-average volume targets 109.27 (SMA20), then 115.28 (EMA26). A sustained move above 115 would confirm a trend reversal and open the path to 120 to 125. Entry on a confirmed close above 104 with volume support. Stop loss at 92.68 (June 29 close, representing a failed breakout).

Bearish scenario: Failure at 104 resistance and a drop back below 97 would suggest the bounce is exhausted. A break below 85 would retest the 82 support and risk a breakdown to new lows. The stop-loss for any long position should be placed at 92 to 93, below the 38.2 retracement level, to minimize downside risk in case the bounce fails.

Neutral to cautious scenario: Price consolidates between 93 and 104 for 1 to 2 weeks before making a directional move. This is the most likely near-term outcome given the speed of the bounce. Tighter stops and partial position sizing are recommended until the SMA20 is reclaimed.

Risk Factors

The primary risk is Bitcoin correlation. If BTC breaks below recent support, MSTR will follow immediately given the company's massive Bitcoin holdings. Macroeconomic headwinds, regulatory developments, or a deterioration in risk appetite could reverse the bounce. The negative P/E ratio of -2.51 means MSTR is valued purely on its Bitcoin holdings and future growth prospects, making it highly speculative. Low liquidity during after-hours trading can lead to wide spreads. The stock remains 78% below its 52-week high regardless of the recent bounce, and a full trend reversal requires time and confirmation.

Conclusion

MSTR is showing the first genuine signs of life since the April sell-off began. The 22% bounce from the 82 support zone is technically constructive, supported by elevated volume, an RSI emerging from oversold territory, and a reclaim of the EMA12 for the first time in weeks. However, the stock remains in a confirmed downtrend by any measure — below all key moving averages, below the Ichimoku Cloud, and well below the 200 level where it traded in April. The next few trading sessions will be critical. A break above 104 would confirm the bounce has legs and open a path to the SMA20 at 109. A failure at resistance would signal a consolidation or potential retest of the lows. Position sizing should reflect the speculative nature of the stock and the uncertain macro environment. The 82 support level is the line in the sand for the bull case — below it, the next support is unclear.
