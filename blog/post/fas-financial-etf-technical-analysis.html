FAS Stock Technical Analysis Bullish Setup

R
Royce Koh
Founder, RoyceAI
July 7, 2026

The Direxion Daily Financial Bull 3X Shares ETF continues to exhibit compelling technical patterns that warrant attention from momentum focused traders. As a triple leveraged exchange traded fund tracking the Russell 1000 Financial Services Index, FAS provides amplified exposure to the banking and financial services sector, making its technical analysis particularly valuable for traders looking to capitalize on sector wide momentum with concentrated positions.

Current Price Action

FAS is trading in a constructive uptrend pattern after establishing a solid support base near the 125 dollar level in late June. The ETF has rallied approximately 8 percent from that support zone, currently trading near the 135 dollar region with above average volume supporting the move. The price action shows higher lows and higher highs over the past two weeks, a classic hallmark of trending behavior that suggests institutional accumulation rather than retail speculation.

The daily candlestick pattern shows a series of bullish engulfing candles that have consistently closed near their intraday highs, indicating strong buying pressure throughout each session. The most recent sessions have seen reduced volatility with smaller bodied candles, suggesting the market is consolidating gains before the next leg higher.

RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe currently reads 62, positioning it in bullish territory above the neutral 50 level but below the overbought threshold of 70. This is a constructive reading that indicates there is substantial room for further upside before the ETF becomes technically extended. Readings between 60 and 70 during uptrends typically align with continuation patterns rather than exhaustion signals.

The 4 hour RSI reads 58, consistent with the daily reading and confirming the intermediate term bullish bias. The weekly RSI has also turned upward from the 45 level, crossing above 50 for the first time in three weeks. This weekly RSI crossover above the midpoint is a significant confirmation signal that suggests the larger timeframe trend is shifting from neutral to bullish.

RSI divergence analysis does not currently show any bearish divergence patterns that would warn of an impending reversal. Price and RSI continue to move in the same direction, a healthy condition for trend continuation. Traders should monitor for any bearish divergence on the 4 hour or daily charts as the ETF approaches the 145 dollar resistance zone.

MACD Analysis

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator has generated a clear bullish crossover on the daily chart, with the MACD line crossing above the signal line three sessions ago. This crossover occurred while both lines were positioned above the zero line, which significantly increases the reliability of the signal. MACD crossovers above zero indicate that the short term momentum is accelerating within an already established uptrend.

The MACD histogram is expanding positively for the fifth consecutive session, confirming increasing bullish momentum. The growing histogram bars indicate that the spread between the MACD line and the signal line is widening, suggesting the trend is gaining strength rather than weakening.

On the weekly timeframe, the MACD lines are converging toward a potential bullish crossover near the zero line. If the weekly MACD completes a bullish crossover in the coming sessions, it would represent a major confirmation signal aligning with the daily timeframe analysis and significantly increasing the probability of sustained upside.

Moving Averages

The moving average structure is shifting from neutral to bullish alignment. FAS has reclaimed its 20 day simple moving average at 128 dollars and is now testing the 50 day SMA near 133 dollars. A clean break above the 50 day SMA, which appears imminent given the current price action, would mark an important technical milestone.

The 200 day SMA sits at 118 dollars, well below the current price, indicating the longer term trend remains intact despite the June pullback. The price is trading 15 percent above the 200 day SMA, a moderate premium that suggests neither overvaluation nor excessive bearish sentiment.

Exponential moving averages show a similar pattern. The 12 day EMA has crossed above the 26 day EMA, confirming the short term bullish trend. The 50 day EMA is still positioned below the 100 day EMA, but the gap is narrowing. A golden cross between the 50 day MA and the 100 day MA would be a powerful buy signal if it materializes in the coming weeks.

The 20 day EMA at 130 dollars represents the nearest dynamic support level. The 50 day EMA at 131 dollars provides secondary support. A closing price below both of these levels would invalidate the current bullish thesis and suggest a return to range bound trading.

Volume Analysis

Volume patterns strongly support the current bullish narrative. The recent rally from the 125 dollar support level has been accompanied by above average volume, with several sessions recording volume 30 to 50 percent above the 20 day average. This volume profile indicates genuine institutional buying interest rather than low conviction retail speculation.

The On Balance Volume indicator is making new highs alongside price, confirming that volume is supporting the price advance. There is no negative divergence between OBV and price, which would have suggested distribution. Instead, OBV confirms accumulation is underway.

Chaikin Money Flow is positive and trending upward, currently reading 0.12 on the daily timeframe. This indicates that buying pressure is dominating selling pressure, consistent with an accumulation phase. When CMF remains above zero during a price advance, it confirms the move has institutional support.

The volume profile shows the heaviest volume nodes between 125 and 130 dollars, establishing this zone as a significant support area. The current price above this high volume node suggests the market has already absorbed available supply at lower levels and is now seeking higher prices.

Support and Resistance Levels

Support levels for FAS are clearly defined and provide traders with well structured risk management reference points. The primary support zone sits at 125 dollars, representing the June swing low and a significant volume weighted average price level. Multiple tests of this level held successfully, establishing it as a reliable floor.

Secondary support stands at 130 dollars, the 20 day EMA and a prior resistance turned support level. This level has been tested twice in the past week and held, suggesting it is strengthening as a support zone. A break below 130 would target the primary support at 125 dollars.

On the resistance side, the immediate resistance level is 138 dollars, representing a prior swing high from mid June. A clean break above this level would open the path to the next resistance zone at 142 to 145 dollars, which coincides with the 200 day SMA from the May timeframe and a previous consolidation zone.

The major resistance target is the 150 dollar psychological level, which represents the upper boundary of the trading range from April and May. A break above 150 dollars would represent a significant technical breakout and could trigger accelerated upside momentum as short sellers are forced to cover positions.

Chart Patterns

FAS is forming a bullish flag pattern on the daily chart, with the flagpole represented by the sharp rally from 125 dollars to 135 dollars and the flag itself forming as a tight consolidation between 133 and 136 dollars over the past several sessions. Bullish flags are continuation patterns that typically resolve in the direction of the preceding trend.

The measured move target of the flag pattern, calculated by adding the flagpole height to the breakout point, suggests a target near 145 dollars. This aligns with the identified resistance zone and provides a realistic near term objective for trend following traders.

Additionally, the daily chart shows a potential inverse head and shoulders pattern in development, with the left shoulder at the late June low, the head at the early July low near 125 dollars, and the right shoulder currently forming. A neckline breakout above 138 dollars would complete this pattern and signal a more significant trend reversal.

Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the decline from the May high near 158 dollars to the July low at 125 dollars provides important reference levels for the current recovery rally. The 38.2 percent retracement level sits at 137 dollars, which the price is currently testing. A clean break above this level would target the 50 percent retracement at 141 dollars.

The 61.8 percent retracement at 145 dollars represents the most significant Fibonacci resistance level. In trending markets, the 61.8 percent level often acts as a reversal point or consolidation level. A break above the 61.8 percent level would be strongly bullish and suggest the ETF may attempt to retest the May highs.

The 78.6 percent retracement at 151 dollars provides an upside target in the event of a sustained recovery move. If FAS can reclaim the 150 dollar level, the next Fibonacci extension targets the 162 dollar area using the 127.2 percent extension.

Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands are providing useful context for the current price action. The bands are narrowing slightly after expanding during the June selloff, indicating a period of declining volatility following the sharp move lower. Narrowing bands often precede significant directional moves, and the current band width suggests a breakout may be imminent.

Price is trading above the middle band (20 day SMA) at 130 dollars, a bullish positioning within the band framework. The upper band sits at 142 dollars, providing a realistic near term upside target. When price trends along the upper band during an uptrend, it indicates strong trending behavior.

Bollinger Band width is contracting but remains above average overall, suggesting that while volatility is declining from elevated levels, it has not yet reached the compression levels that typically precede explosive moves. A further narrowing of band width over the next several sessions would increase the probability of a significant directional breakout.

Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud analysis provides a comprehensive view of support and resistance across multiple timeframes. The daily conversion line at 132 dollars has crossed above the base line at 129 dollars, generating a bullish TK crossover, one of the most reliable signals in the Ichimoku system.

The leading span A is currently positioned above leading span B, forming a bullish green cloud. However, price is still trading within the cloud rather than above it, indicating that the trend is in transition from neutral to bullish. A decisive break above the cloud would confirm the trend shift.

The lagging span has moved above price levels from 26 periods ago, another confirming signal that the current price represents a new high relative to the historical comparison period. The cloud provides resistance in the 137 to 140 dollar zone, which aligns with the Fibonacci and chart pattern resistance levels identified earlier.

Elliott Wave Analysis

From an Elliott Wave perspective, FAS appears to have completed a corrective ABC decline from the May high to the July low, with wave A representing the initial selloff, wave B representing a brief corrective rally, and wave C representing the final leg lower that established the 125 dollar low.

The current rally from 125 dollars shows the characteristic five wave impulsive structure of a new uptrend. Wave 1 completed near 132 dollars, wave 2 pulled back to 128 dollars, and waves 3 through 5 are currently in development. Wave 3, typically the strongest and longest wave, appears to be still in progress, suggesting significant additional upside potential.

The wave 3 extension target, calculated using standard Fibonacci relationships, projects a move to approximately 148 to 152 dollars. This aligns with the resistance analysis and suggests that the current rally has further room to develop before reaching completion.

Market Sentiment

The financial sector is benefiting from a supportive macroeconomic backdrop. The Federal Reserve has maintained its current interest rate stance, providing stability for bank net interest margins. Bank earnings in the most recent quarter generally exceeded expectations, with major institutions reporting stronger than anticipated trading revenue and investment banking fees.

The broader market regime currently shows a constructive risk on environment. The VIX has remained below 20 for most of the past two weeks, indicating low fear levels in the equity market. Credit spreads have narrowed, reflecting improving credit conditions that benefit financial sector profitability.

FAS fund flows have been positive over the past month, with institutional inflows supporting the ETF price. Options activity shows elevated call volume relative to put volume, indicating that options traders are positioned for continued upside in financial stocks.

Trading Scenarios

Bullish scenario: FAS breaks above 138 dollars on above average volume, confirming the flag pattern breakout and targeting the 142 to 145 dollar resistance zone. Sustained momentum above 145 dollars opens the path to 150 dollars. The bullish scenario is invalidated below 125 dollars.

Bearish scenario: FAS fails to hold 130 dollars and declines back to test the 125 dollar support level. A break below 125 dollars establishes a lower low and suggests the corrective pattern is continuing. A close below 118 dollars would break the 200 day moving average and indicate a potential trend reversal to the downside.

Base case scenario: FAS continues to consolidate between 130 and 138 dollars over the next several sessions before eventually breaking higher. The consolidation period allows moving averages to catch up to price and provides a healthier foundation for the next leg of the uptrend.

Risk Factors

Leveraged ETF specific risks include time decay from daily rebalancing, which means FAS is best suited for short to intermediate term trading rather than long term holding. Contango and backwardation in the futures markets that underly the financial sector index can cause tracking errors over extended holding periods.

Sector specific risks include potential regulatory changes affecting bank capital requirements, deterioration in credit quality if the economy slows more than expected, and any unexpected shifts in Federal Reserve monetary policy that compress bank net interest margins.

Broader market risks include geopolitical events, unexpected inflation data that forces the Fed to change its policy stance, and a general risk off rotation that could impact all leveraged ETFs regardless of their underlying sector fundamentals.

Conclusion

FAS presents a compelling technical setup with bullish signals across multiple independent indicators. The RSI above 60 with room to run, the MACD bullish crossover above zero, the constructive moving average alignment, and the confirmed support at 125 dollars all point to continued upside potential.

The measured move targets between 142 and 145 dollars provide realistic near term objectives representing approximately 5 to 7 percent upside from current levels. The defined support structure at 130 and 125 dollars provides clear risk management levels for traders positioning for the anticipated breakout.

Traders should monitor the 138 dollar resistance level as the immediate catalyst. A clean break above this level on elevated volume would confirm the bullish thesis and likely trigger accelerated momentum toward the higher targets.
