Gold (XAU/USD) Technical Analysis — July 8, 2026

Current Price Action

Gold is trading at $4,059 per troy ounce as of July 8, 2026, down 1.13 percent on the session and 4.73 percent over the past month. The precious metal has pulled back sharply from its recent highs near $4,550, erasing significant gains from the first half of 2026. Despite the short-term weakness, gold remains up 22.5 percent year-to-date, reflecting the strong bullish trend that dominated the first five months of the year. The current decline appears to be a corrective retracement within a longer-term uptrend, but the magnitude of the pullback demands careful technical analysis. Key support lies at the $4,000 psychological level, while resistance has formed near $4,254.

RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe is reading near 38, putting gold in oversold territory. The RSI dipped below 30 intraday earlier this week, marking the deepest oversold reading since the March 2026 correction. Historically, gold tends to bounce strongly when the daily RSI reaches these levels. Tickeron data indicates that when XAUUSD RSI exited the oversold zone previously, 14 out of 26 instances resulted in a sustained price increase, giving a 54 percent probability of a bounce. The 4-hour RSI is showing early signs of bullish divergence with price making lower lows while RSI prints higher lows. This divergence pattern often precedes trend reversals and suggests that selling pressure is exhausting.

The weekly RSI reads near 45, still in neutral territory but declining. A weekly close above 50 would signal renewed bullish momentum. The monthly RSI remains above 60, confirming that the long-term trend is still bullish despite the current correction. Traders should watch for the daily RSI to reclaim 40 as an early signal that the selling pressure is abating.

MACD Analysis

The MACD on the daily chart has been declining since mid-June 2026. The MACD line crossed below the signal line on June 14, generating a sell signal that has remained in effect. The histogram has been printing progressively deeper red bars, indicating accelerating bearish momentum. However, the rate of histogram expansion has slowed over the past three trading sessions, suggesting that the selling pressure may be reaching exhaustion.

The weekly MACD is beginning to flatten after declining for three consecutive weeks. A bullish crossover on the weekly timeframe would be a powerful confirmation of a trend reversal, but this signal is likely several weeks away. On the 4-hour chart, the MACD histogram is showing signs of compression, often a precursor to a trend change. A bullish MACD crossover on the 4-hour timeframe would provide an early entry signal for short-term traders looking to catch the bounce.

Moving Averages

Gold has broken below several key moving averages during the current selloff. The price is trading below the 20-day simple moving average at $4,280, the 50-day SMA at $4,350, and the 100-day SMA at $4,180. The 200-day SMA sits near $3,850, well below current price, indicating that the long-term trend remains intact. The distance between current price and the 200-day moving average is approximately 5.4 percent, which is within the normal range for a bull market correction.

The 20-day EMA at $4,250 has been declining for 12 consecutive sessions, confirming the short-term bearish trend. The 50-day EMA at $4,340 has just started to turn downward, a concerning development for medium-term bulls. If the 50-day EMA crosses below the 100-day EMA, it would form a death cross pattern that typically leads to extended weakness. However, the 200-day EMA continues to slope upward, providing a strong foundation for the longer-term trend.

The moving average alignment shows a compressed configuration with the 20-day SMA through 100-day SMA clustered within a $170 range. This tight grouping often precedes a significant expansion move. The direction of the breakout from this compression zone will likely determine gold direction for the next several weeks.

Volume Analysis

Volume has been elevated during the current decline, with several sessions recording above-average trading activity. On July 7, trading volume was 35 percent above the 20-day average, indicating strong participation in the selloff. However, volume has begun to taper over the past two sessions, a pattern that often precedes a short-term bottom. On-Balance Volume has been declining since mid-June, confirming that distribution has been the dominant force. The OBV line is approaching a significant support level from April 2026, which could act as a catalyst for a reversal if it holds.

The Volume Price Trend indicator has turned negative for the first time since January 2026. VPT crossing below zero suggests that institutional distribution is occurring. However, VPT typically turns positive before price bottoms, so traders should watch for VPT to flatten or reverse as an early signal.

Support and Resistance

Gold is testing critical support at the $4,000 to $4,050 zone. This area represents both a psychological round number and a previous resistance-turned-support level from the March 2026 consolidation. A daily close below $4,000 would open the door for a move toward the next major support at $3,850, which coincides with the 200-day moving average. If that level breaks, the $3,700 area represents the next significant support, corresponding to the February 2026 swing low.

On the upside, immediate resistance sits at $4,180, which aligns with the 100-day SMA and the July 3 intraday high. The next major resistance level is $4,254, representing the 61.8 percent Fibonacci retracement of the recent decline from $4,550 to $4,050. A breakout above this level would target the $4,350 to $4,380 zone, which includes the 50-day SMA and the June 2026 consolidation range. Above that, the $4,550 high is the ultimate resistance target for the medium-term bullish case.

Chart Patterns

Gold has formed a bearish flag pattern on the daily chart following the sharp decline from $4,550. The flagpole measured approximately $350 in vertical decline. The flag itself is consolidating between $4,100 and $4,254. A downside breakdown from this flag would project a target near $3,750, calculated by subtracting the flagpole height from the breakdown point. However, the oversold RSI readings and slowing momentum suggest the flag may be a consolidation before a reversal rather than continuation.

A potential double-bottom pattern is forming around the $4,050 level. If gold holds this level and bounces, a retest of the neckline near $4,254 could confirm the pattern with a measured move target of $4,450. The double-bottom is not yet confirmed and requires a breakout above $4,254 to become actionable.

Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the rally from the October 2025 low near $2,600 to the April 2026 high near $4,550 reveals key levels. The 23.6 percent retracement sits at $4,090, which has already been broken. The 38.2 percent level at $3,805 is now the next significant target if selling continues. The 50 percent retracement at $3,575 would represent a major correction but is unlikely unless macroeconomic conditions deteriorate significantly.

Applying Fibonacci to the more recent decline from $4,550 to $4,050, the 23.6 percent retracement sits at $4,168, the 38.2 percent level at $4,241, the 50 percent level at $4,300, and the 61.8 percent level at $4,359. The price has already tested the 23.6 percent retracement and failed to hold above it, indicating persistent selling pressure. A retest of the 38.2 percent level at $4,241 is the minimum expectation for a relief rally.

Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands on the daily chart have widened significantly during the current selloff, indicating increased volatility. The lower band sits near $3,950, and the price has been riding the lower band for the past five sessions. This sustained contact with the lower band is a sign of an extended downtrend. However, when price rides the lower band for extended periods, the probability of a snap-back rally increases.

Band width is at its highest level since March 2026, which typically precedes a period of price consolidation or reversal. The upper band sits near $4,450, meaning a recovery to that level would not be statistically unusual. The %B indicator is reading near 0.08, indicating that price is near the lower band and potentially oversold. A %B reading below zero would indicate a breakdown below the lower band, which would be a strong bearish signal.

Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily chart is bearish. Price is trading below the cloud, a classic sell signal. The cloud itself is red for the next 17 sessions, indicating that resistance above current price levels is likely to hold. The Tenkan-sen (conversion line) at $4,150 has crossed below the Kijun-sen (base line) at $4,280, forming a bearish TK cross. The Chikou Span (lagging span) is below price, confirming the bearish momentum.

The future cloud is also bearish, with the Senkou Span A below Senkou Span B, suggesting that resistance will continue to pressure price for the next several weeks. However, the cloud is relatively thin in the $4,000 to $4,100 zone, meaning a push above this area could quickly clear cloud resistance. A return above the cloud would require a move back above $4,280, which remains the key resistance level.

Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count suggests that gold completed a five-wave advance from the October 2025 low at $2,600 to the April 2026 high at $4,550. This would put the current decline in wave A of a larger ABC correction. The magnitude of wave A at approximately $500 represents a 38.2 percent retracement of the entire rally, which is typical for an A wave. Wave B should retrace 38.2 to 61.8 percent of wave A, targeting the $4,240 to $4,360 zone. Wave C would then test the wave A low or extend lower.

If this count is correct, the relief rally to complete wave B should begin within the next few sessions, given the oversold conditions. The wave C decline that follows could test the $3,800 to $4,000 zone. However, if the decline from $4,550 is itself a wave 4 correction within a larger impulse, the bounce from current levels would be wave 5 targeting new all-time highs above $4,550. The depth of the current decline makes the ABC correction scenario more likely.

Market Sentiment

Gold market sentiment has turned bearish after weeks of sustained selling. The daily Fear and Greed reading for the broader market is at 18, indicating extreme fear. This contrarian indicator historically suggests that a bounce is near. However, gold-specific sentiment is more nuanced. Commodity Futures Trading Commission data from the most recent Commitment of Traders report shows that speculative long positions have declined by 24 percent since early June, while short positions have increased by 38 percent. This positioning suggests that the selloff has room to run before reaching exhaustion.

The gold ETF flows tell a different story. Physical gold ETF holdings have remained relatively stable during the selloff, suggesting that long-term investors are holding their positions rather than capitulating. This divergence between futures positioning and ETF holdings is typical of a correction driven by speculative profit-taking rather than fundamental selling.

Trading Recommendations

For short-term traders, the oversold conditions and potential double-bottom at $4,050 present an attractive risk-reward opportunity for a bounce trade. Consider a long entry near $4,050 with a stop loss at $3,980. The initial target is $4,180 (100-day SMA), with a secondary target at $4,254 (61.8 percent retracement). The risk-reward ratio on this trade is approximately 1 to 2.5, which is favorable.

For swing traders, wait for a confirmed breakout above $4,254 before establishing long positions. A daily close above this level would invalidate the bearish flag pattern and confirm the double-bottom, targeting $4,450. The stop loss for this trade would be below $4,100.

For position traders, the current pullback represents a potential accumulation zone for long-term holdings. The 200-day moving average near $3,850 provides a strong foundation for the secular uptrend. Consider scaling into positions near $4,000 with additional entries at $3,850 if tested.

The bearish scenario requires a daily close below $4,000. If this level breaks, the path to $3,850 opens quickly. Short sellers could target $3,850 with stops above $4,100.

Risk Factors

The primary risk to the bullish case is further dollar strength. The US Dollar Index has been rallying on hawkish Federal Reserve commentary, and a sustained dollar rally would pressure gold further. Additionally, the Treasury yield curve has been steepening, increasing the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold.

Geopolitical risks remain elevated with ongoing US-Ukraine tensions, which typically support gold prices. However, the market has been focusing on monetary policy dynamics rather than geopolitical risk premiums in recent weeks. The July US jobs report scheduled for next week could be the catalyst for the next major move. A strong jobs number would likely push gold lower, while a weak report could trigger a sharp rally.

The CME FedWatch tool shows a 72 percent probability of a rate hold at the next FOMC meeting, with 28 percent probability of a cut. Gold typically performs well during rate cut cycles, so any shift in rate expectations would be a major catalyst.

Conclusion

Gold is at a critical juncture, testing the $4,000 to $4,050 support zone with oversold technical readings across multiple indicators. The long-term trend remains bullish, but the short-term picture is bearish with potential for a relief bounce. The key level to watch is $4,000. As long as gold holds above this level, the correction remains within the bounds of a healthy bull market pullback. A break below $4,000 would signal a deeper correction toward the 200-day moving average near $3,850. For now, the technical evidence supports a bounce from current levels, but traders should remain disciplined with stop losses given the elevated volatility.
