NVDA (NVIDIA) Technical Analysis - July 2, 2026

Current Price Action and Market Context

NVIDIA (NVDA) closed at $197.58 on July 1, 2026, down from a previous close of $200.09 on June 30, representing a modest decline of 1.3% on the day. The stock opened at $196.21, reached an intraday high of $199.85, and tested a low of $193.45 before recovering into the close. Pre-market trading on July 2 shows NVDA at $197.26, essentially flat from the prior close, suggesting indecision heading into the regular session.

The broader market context shows the S&P 500 (SPY) trading at $745.76, comfortably above its 200-day moving average of $688.28, indicating a generally bullish macro environment. However, the market regime indicator is currently yellow, suggesting mixed signals and the need for caution. The VIX at 16.59 remains in complacent territory, which historically has preceded periods of increased volatility.

NVDA has fallen approximately 16.4% from its 52-week high of $236.54 reached on May 14, 2026, placing the stock in a technical correction from that peak. The 52-week low of $152.97 from July 2025 provides the lower bound of the yearly range. The current price sits in the middle third of this massive $83.57 range, leaving room for moves in either direction.

Volume on July 1 was 146.1 million shares, slightly below the 30-day average volume of 159.9 million. This below-average volume during the recent bounce from June lows suggests a lack of aggressive buying conviction, which is a moderately bearish signal that needs monitoring.

RSI Analysis

The Relative Strength Index on the daily timeframe is currently recovering from oversold territory. NVDA's RSI(14) touched approximately 32 during the June 29 low at $189.80, which is just above the oversold threshold of 30. This near-oversold reading correctly flagged the subsequent bounce to the $200 area. The current RSI reading is approximately 40-42, indicating that the stock has recovered from deeply bearish momentum but has not yet reached neutral territory (50).

On the 4-hour timeframe, RSI shows a more constructive picture. The shorter-term RSI(14) on 4-hour candles has recovered to approximately 45-48 after forming a bullish divergence pattern during the June 26-29 lows. Bullish divergence occurs when price makes a lower low but RSI makes a higher low, suggesting selling momentum is exhausting. This divergence was the early technical warning that the $190 area would likely hold as support.

The weekly RSI tells a more concerning story, reading approximately 35-38. This indicates that the intermediate-term trend remains bearish despite the daily bounce. Weekly RSI below 40 is a warning that the correction may not be over and that any bounces could be sold into. For a sustained trend reversal to occur, the weekly RSI needs to reclaim the 50 level.

No RSI divergence is present on the daily timeframe currently, as both price and RSI are moving higher together. This is a neutral-to-slightly-bullish configuration — momentum is aligned with price, but neither has confirmed a trend change yet.

MACD Indicator

The MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) on the daily timeframe is showing early signs of a potential bullish crossover. The MACD line (12-day EMA minus 26-day EMA) is currently at approximately -3.80, while the signal line (9-day EMA of MACD) is at approximately -4.20. The MACD histogram, which measures the distance between the MACD line and the signal line, has been contracting over the past three trading sessions, moving from -1.20 to -0.40. If this narrowing continues, a bullish crossover could occur within the next 2-4 trading sessions.

The 4-hour MACD has already completed a bullish crossover, with the MACD line crossing above the signal line on June 30. This shorter-term crossover preceded the bounce from $189.80 to $200.09 and confirms that buying pressure is building at the shorter timeframes. However, the histogram bars are still relatively small, indicating that the momentum behind this crossover is modest rather than explosive.

The weekly MACD remains bearish, with the MACD line well below the signal line and the histogram printing negative bars. The weekly MACD histogram has been contracting since mid-June, which is a positive early sign, but a full weekly bullish crossover is likely weeks away under the most optimistic scenario. This multi-timeframe analysis — bullish on 4H, neutral-to-bullish on daily, bearish on weekly — is characteristic of a bottoming process rather than a confirmed reversal.

Moving Averages

NVDA's moving average structure reveals a stock in transition. The 20-day SMA is currently at approximately $205.80, the 50-day SMA at $213.50, the 100-day SMA at $208.40, and the 200-day SMA at $194.20. The stock closed at $197.58, which places it between the 200-day SMA support at $194.20 and the 20-day SMA resistance at $205.80.

The proximity of the 200-day SMA at $194.20 is the most significant moving average story. The 200-day SMA acts as the primary long-term trend indicator, and NVDA tested this level during the June 26-29 period, dropping as low as $189.80 intraday before bouncing. The fact that NVDA briefly broke below the 200-day SMA but recovered above it is a critical observation — this is a common pattern during bull market corrections where the 200-day SMA acts as a magnet and then a springboard.

The EMA structure (Exponential Moving Averages) provides additional insight. The 12-day EMA at approximately $198.50 and the 26-day EMA at $202.80 are in a bearish alignment, with the shorter EMA below the longer. The 12-day EMA is flattening, however, which is an early sign that selling pressure is diminishing. A bullish EMA crossover (12 above 26) would confirm a trend change but has not yet occurred.

The most bearish alignment is in the 20/50/100-day SMA stack: all three are in descending order (20 below 50 below 100), which is a classic bearish configuration. For the trend to turn bullish, NVDA needs to reclaim the 20-day SMA at $205.80 and then the 50-day at $213.50. Until these levels are recaptured, the path of least resistance remains lower.

Volume Analysis

Volume analysis provides crucial clues about the strength of the current bounce. On June 29, when NVDA hit its low of $189.80, volume was 148.8 million — below the 30-day average, which is typical for a selling climax. The bounce day on June 30 saw volume increase to 166.5 million, above average, confirming institutional buying interest at the lows. However, July 1's pullback to $197.58 occurred on 146.1 million volume, which is below average and suggests the pullback was not accompanied by aggressive selling.

The On-Balance Volume (OBV) indicator has been declining since mid-May when NVDA was trading above $220. This declining OBV during a period of falling prices is expected and confirms that distribution (institutional selling) has been the dominant force. However, OBV has been showing signs of stabilization over the past five trading sessions, with a slight uptick from June 26 to July 1. This stabilization aligns with the price bounce and suggests that the distribution phase may be ending.

The Accumulation/Distribution (A/D) line tells a similar story. After declining steadily from the May peak, the A/D line has flattened over the past week, indicating that accumulation is beginning to match distribution at current price levels. This is a necessary condition for a bottom to form.

The Chaikin Money Flow (CMF) 20-day reading is approximately -0.08, still negative but improving from -0.18 two weeks ago. A positive CMF reading would confirm that buying pressure is exceeding selling pressure over the medium term. The improving CMF trend is encouraging but has not yet crossed the zero line.

Support and Resistance Levels

Identifying key support and resistance levels is critical for understanding NVDA's potential next move. The most important support level is the $190 zone ($189.80-$193.45), which encompasses the June 29 low and the July 1 intraday low. This zone also coincides with the 200-day SMA at $194.20, creating a confluence of technical support that should be difficult to break. Below $190, the next major support is at $180-$182, which was support during the April consolidation period. The final major support before the 52-week low is at $170-$172, which held during the April retracement.

Resistance levels are stacked above current prices. The first meaningful resistance is the 20-day SMA at $205.80, which also coincides with the June 24 high of $201.67 and the June 23 high of $203.77. The next major resistance zone is $210-$214, which encompasses the 50-day SMA at $213.50, the June 18 high of $211.39, and the June 15 high of $212.45. This is the most significant near-term resistance zone and will require strong buying volume to break.

Above $214, resistance becomes lighter until the $220-$224 area, which was support during May and would now act as resistance on any recovery. The ultimate resistance is the 52-week high at $236.54, which marks the peak of the May rally. A move above $236.54 would signal the resumption of the primary uptrend.

Chart Patterns

The dominant chart pattern on the daily timeframe is a descending channel that has been in place since the May 14 peak at $236.54. The upper boundary of this channel connects the May 14 high ($236.54) with the June 2 high ($232.28) and the June 18 high ($211.39). The lower boundary connects the May 19 low ($217.91) with the June 10 low ($199.92) and the June 29 low ($189.80).

NVDA is currently testing the lower boundary of this descending channel. A bounce from the lower boundary is the expected pattern behavior, and the bounce from $189.80 to $200.09 fits this expectation. For the descending channel to resolve bullishly, NVDA must break above the upper boundary, which is currently around $210 and declining. A break above $210 with above-average volume would be a strong buy signal.

On the 4-hour timeframe, a potential double bottom pattern has formed with lows at $192.13 (June 26) and $189.80 (June 29). The neckline of this pattern is approximately $200.63 (the June 30 high). NVDA nearly reached the neckline on June 30 at $200.63 before pulling back on July 1. A confirmed break above the neckline with volume would project a measured move target of approximately $209-$210, which aligns with the 50-day SMA resistance zone.

Fibonacci Retracement

Applying Fibonacci retracement to the entire rally from the 52-week low of $152.97 (July 2025) to the 52-week high of $236.54 (May 2026) provides the broader context. The 38.2% retracement level is at $204.53, the 50% level at $194.76, and the 61.8% level at $184.90.

NVDA has already tested and is currently trading near the 50% Fibonacci retracement level at $194.76. The bounce from $189.80 came very close to this level before reversing higher, confirming its significance. The fact that the 200-day SMA ($194.20) is nearly perfectly aligned with the 50% Fibonacci level ($194.76) creates a powerful support confluence that should hold on any further weakness.

If NVDA breaks below the 50% level, the 61.8% retracement at $184.90 is the next target. This is a critical level in Fibonacci theory — a break below 61.8% would suggest the correction is deeper than a normal pullback and could indicate a trend change. Conversely, a move above the 38.2% level at $204.53 would be the first sign that the correction is ending and the uptrend is resuming.

A more near-term Fibonacci retracement of the decline from the May 14 high ($236.54) to the June 29 low ($189.80) shows: 23.6% at $200.79, 38.2% at $207.59, 50% at $213.17, and 61.8% at $218.75. NVDA bounced to $200.63 on June 30, which is just below the 23.6% level. A move above $200.79 would target the 38.2% level at $207.59.

Bollinger Bands

The Bollinger Bands on the daily timeframe are currently contracting, which is a sign that volatility is decreasing after the sharp selloff. The upper band is at approximately $224, the middle band (20-day SMA) at $205.80, and the lower band at approximately $188. The bands are narrowing, suggesting that a period of consolidation is underway.

NVDA's price is currently trading in the lower half of the bands, between the middle band ($205.80) and the lower band ($188). The price tag of $197.58 is approximately 40% of the way from the lower band to the middle band, indicating that the stock is still in the lower part of its Bollinger range but has moved off the extreme.

The BandWidth indicator (band width divided by middle band) is contracting, which historically precedes significant price moves. A BandWidth contraction after a sharp selloff often leads to a breakout in the direction of the prior trend. Given that the prior trend before the correction was strongly bullish, this suggests the eventual breakout is likely to be to the upside.

Ichimoku Cloud

The Ichimoku Cloud on the daily timeframe provides a comprehensive view of support, resistance, and trend direction. The Conversion Line (Tenkan-sen, 9-period) is at approximately $198, while the Base Line (Kijun-sen, 26-period) is at $206. The Conversion Line is still below the Base Line, maintaining a bearish TK cross, but the gap is narrowing.

The Leading Span A (Senkou Span A, midpoint of Conversion and Base, shifted forward 26 periods) is at approximately $213, and Leading Span B (Senkou Span B, midpoint of 52-period high/low, shifted forward) is at $207. The cloud is thick between $207 and $213, representing significant resistance. NVDA would need to break through this cloud to confirm a trend reversal.

The current price of $197.58 is well below the cloud, which is a bearish configuration. For a trend reversal signal, NVDA needs to break above the cloud resistance at $207-$213. The Lagging Span (Chikou Span, current price shifted back 26 periods) is below the price action from 26 periods ago, confirming the bearish posture.

Elliott Wave Analysis

The Elliott Wave count from the May 14 high suggests NVDA is completing a corrective ABC wave structure. Wave A was the initial decline from $236.54 to $199.92 (June 10), a drop of 15.5%. Wave B was the bounce to $213.99 (June 22), recovering approximately 45% of Wave A. Wave C appears to be the decline from $213.99 to $189.80 (June 29), which is approximately 1.0 times the length of Wave A, meeting the minimum requirement for a C wave.

If this ABC corrective structure is complete, NVDA should now begin a new impulsive wave sequence (Wave 1 up) toward the $210-$220 area in the initial move. The alternative count is that a larger corrective structure is unfolding, with the ABC being only Wave A of a larger zigzag, which would imply another leg down after a bounce. The resolution of this count ambiguity will come from NVDA's ability to break above $214 (the Wave B high) which would strongly favor the bullish count.

Market Sentiment and Options Activity

The broader market sentiment is cautiously optimistic, with the S&P 500 showing strength above its 200-day moving average. The SPY is at $745.76, up 8.35% from its 200-day SMA, indicating a healthy uptrend. However, the credit spread at 4.0% (elevated category) suggests that the bond market is pricing in some risk, which aligns with the yellow market regime.

However, the below-average volume on the bounce suggests that large institutional buyers are not yet committing capital aggressively. Options flow data would provide additional clarity, but based on price action alone, the sentiment is one of cautious optimism with a wait-and-see approach.

Trading Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive technical analysis, NVDA presents a cautiously bullish near-term outlook with important caveats. The stock is in a bottoming process, not a confirmed uptrend. The bounce from key support at the 200-day SMA and 50% Fibonacci level is constructive, but the moving average alignment remains bearish.

Bullish Scenario: If NVDA can break above $205.80 (20-day SMA) on above-average volume, it would target the $210-$214 resistance zone. A move above $214 would confirm the descending channel breakout and target $220-$224. The measured move target from the double bottom pattern is $209-$210, which aligns with this outlook. Entry for aggressive traders would be on a break above $200.63 (June 30 high) with a stop at $189.50 and a target of $210.

Bearish Scenario: If NVDA fails to hold the $190 support zone and breaks below the June 29 low of $189.80, the technical picture would turn decisively bearish. The next support is at $180-$182, and a break below that would target $170-$172. A failure at $190 would invalidate the double bottom pattern and suggest that the correction is not yet complete.

Neutral Scenario: The most likely outcome in the near term is continued consolidation between $190 and $206. The converging support and resistance levels create a narrowing range that must eventually resolve. Given the alignment of multiple technical indicators (improving momentum, Bollinger Band contraction, support confluence), the probability slightly favors an eventual upside resolution.

Stop-loss levels should be placed at $189.50 for bullish positions and $206.50 for bearish positions. Position sizing should reflect the yellow market regime, with reduced exposure compared to a green regime environment. The risk-reward ratio for a bullish trade from current levels to the $210 target is approximately 1:2, which is favorable.

Risk Factors

Several risk factors could invalidate the technical analysis. First, a broader market selloff would likely drag NVDA lower regardless of its individual technical setup. The yellow market regime warrants caution, and any deterioration in macro conditions (spike in VIX above 20, credit spreads above 5%) would increase the probability of a downside resolution.

Second, the semiconductor sector faces ongoing headwinds from export controls and geopolitical tensions. Any negative news on this front could trigger a sector-wide selloff that would overwhelm technical support levels.

Third, NVDA's elevated P/E ratio of 30.64 makes it vulnerable to multiple compression if interest rates rise or growth expectations are revised lower. The stock's premium valuation provides less cushion against negative surprises.

Fourth, the descending channel pattern on the daily timeframe remains in effect. Until NVDA breaks above the channel's upper boundary, the dominant trend is bearish, and bounces within a downtrend are selling opportunities until proven otherwise.

Key Takeaway

NVDA is at a critical technical juncture. The stock has bounced from a powerful support confluence at the 200-day SMA, 50% Fibonacci retracement, and the lower boundary of a descending channel. The improving daily momentum indicators (MACD convergence, RSI recovery) and the bullish 4-hour divergence pattern suggest that the bottoming process is underway. However, the weekly indicators remain bearish, and the moving average alignment has not yet turned bullish.

The most prudent approach is to wait for confirmation of the trend reversal. A confirmed break above $205.80 with above-average volume would provide the confirmation needed to add bullish exposure. In the meantime, the $190-$206 consolidation range defines the near-term trading boundaries. The technical setup is constructive, but patience is required until the direction becomes clearer.
